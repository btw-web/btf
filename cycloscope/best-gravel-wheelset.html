All about tire and rim width

With time, both rims and tires of the bikes have evolved, so has the way of cycling. These days most of the bike tracks that entice the riders wanderlust are rough, which has given birth to various new types of bike wheels, for instance, Gravel wheels.

Gravel wheels and bikes are as such a new domain in the biking world. Hence, if you are buying a new wheel or rim for your gravel bike, it is best to do your research in the best way.

When you go on finding the ideal specifications of the best gravel bike wheelset, you may not get one. There are many brands, and they all have their own specifications for a tire to rim width, internal width, depth, and whatnot.

How is Tubular (Tubeless) tire different from Clincher tire?

Technology has disrupted every part of our life, even the bike wheels. As compared to previously used clincher tires, these days, people like tubeless tires more.

The sole reason that governs this is durability. Unlike clincher wheels that have a tube, tubeless tires are fitted to the wheel using a sealant that grips the rim to the tire and traps the air within.

Tubeless and clincher tires vary based on various factors:

  • Puncture resistant: Unlike clincher tires, tubeless tires are resistant to punctures and also provide low rolling resistance.
  • Fitting: As compared to a tubeless tire, the clincher tire is easier to fit into the rim. As tubeless tires have multiple layers of rubber, they are tough to fit on to the rim. They require sealant so you should bring that with you. 
  • Tire pressure: The tire pressure for the clincher wheel should be high for better performance. On the other hand, if you are using wider tires, the pressure of tubeless tires should be kept low.
  • Hassle: If a Tubeless tire gets punctured anyway, you can still ride for a small distance while protecting the tire. Taking off a tubeless tire is a pain and you’ll need new sealant for the repair. On the other hand, if a clincher tire gets punctured, you need to get off the bike and repair it immediately to move further, this is thought much quicker operation.

Frequently Asked Questions about Gravel Wheels


Are 700c tires good for gravel?

The larger 700C wheel rolls faster thanks to the lower rolling resistance. 700C wheels maintain comfort on rocky and bumpy roads without compromising speed. They are best for gravel riding and racing, but can mount slimmer tires compared to 650b, which are better for climbing difficult roads with loaded bikes.

What’s the maximum tire size I can mount on my rims?

The internal width of the bike wheel or rim should be around 0.5 times the width of the tire.
It is recommended that the tire’s width should not be less than 20% bigger than the rim’s internal width.
