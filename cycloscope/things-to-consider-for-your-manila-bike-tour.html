Things to Consider for Your Manila Bike Tour

Things to Consider for Your Manila Bike Tour

There’s no better way to explore Manila, the Philippine capital than on a bike. Not only can you save money and get the best vacation deals, but you can also experience awe-inspiring attractions up close and personal.

Plus, you can meet some of the friendly locals that you might miss when you’re riding a tour bus or private car. To add, Manila is a melting pot of culture and for a cyclist, the trip to its most sought-after destinations is one of a kind adventure that is one for the books.

On top of these, a lot of Manila roads are bike-friendly and are relatively within close proximity to breathtaking waterfalls, lakes, hills, and so forth, which also happens to be accessible by bike.

Putting all the aforementioned factors into consideration, it can be said that having a Manila bike tour is one of the best things that you can do regardless of your background. In line with this, if you need some tips and pointers about this matter, then you came to the right place. Continue reading for details.

Preparing for bike tours within Manila

If you’re serious about your bike tour, then you should consider doing some training at the closed-circuit trails of McKinley Hill that’s located within the vicinity of Camp Aguinaldo (Philippine National Police headquarters) or Fort Bonifacio (Philippine Army headquarters).

These trails are perfect for beginners since they are secure and properly maintained by the government. Alternatively, you can also hit the Quezon City Circle or the Acad Oval of University of the Philippines.

Touring out of Manila

As mentioned earlier, Manila is within close proximity to some captivating natural treasures. The long list includes the La Mesa Ecopark in Quezon City, Montalban, Tanay, and Antipolo that are located within the borders of Manila and Rizal, and Famy as well as Sta.

Rosa in Laguna. The said destinations are great for weekend bike rides, particularly for those who don’t have the luxury of time on their hands. If it’s the other way around, then you can consider heading over to Batangas, Pampanga, the Mountain Province, and so on.

The Mountain Province is a worthwhile choice because it’s home to the Banaue Rice Terraces, which is heralded by many as the 8th Wonder of the World.

Along the way, you can also head over to Sagada (a town in the Cordillera Mountains), then loop to Philippine Pali, the highest point (7,400 feet above sea level) within the vicinity of the Philippine highway system.

If you want to bring the level of fun to whole new level, then you can consider heading over to the Southern region of the Philippines particularly to Bohol and visit the Die Hard Trail in Loboc.

It’s a man-made forest that boasts of captivating views and so much more. Negros is another worthwhile option due to its view of a volcanic sand beach, seashore, and so on. Other notable Southern destinations are Leyte, Samar, Davao, Cagayan De Oro, and others.

Planning

Before pushing through with your trip, make sure to bring appropriate tools, accessories, and equipment. Also, it’s best to be armed with essential information prior to your trip.

So do your homework first and do extensive research before pushing through with your trip. In line with this, check the weather forecast beforehand. Remember, exerting a little effort is essential if you want a trip that safe, hassle-free, and everything in between.

During your ride

While on the road, make sure to be attentive, careful, and defensive since there are some dangers that may be encountered along the way. Furthermore, make sure to wear your protective gear during our ride.

It’s best also to wear vibrantly colored jerseys and use blinkers at night. Furthermore, it’s best to bring an emergency kit and hydration solutions.

Summing up

These are only some of the most essential pointers that you should consider if you decide to go on a bike trip around Manila and other Philippine destinations. By taking heed of them, you can be reassured that you’ll have an escapade that safe, hassle-free, and everything in between.

Also, either you have a cycling insurance or not, it’s your responsibility to practice caution and safety while on the road since you’re sharing it with drivers, other cyclists, and pedestrians, among others. So take note of the information here if you need a good reference.
