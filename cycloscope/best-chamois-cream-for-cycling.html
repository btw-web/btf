How to Apply Cycling Chamois Cream?

The process to apply chamois cream is simple; you just need to make sure that you apply it in the required volume. Overuse or over applying the cream can lead to irritation. Moreover, it is also necessary to make sure that you avoid intimate areas.

The chamois cream should be applied on the inner thighs, crotch area, underarms, butt cheeks, and anywhere else you feel your skin rubbed against your clothes. You can also apply the skin to the inner side of your clothes, like your shirt and riding shorts.

Here is how you can apply chamois cream:

  • Take about a quarter size lump of cream and rub it gently and loosely on the skin.
  • You can apply it on your skin or on the fabrics based on your preference (it is preferred to apply on the skin).
  • Reapply after hours if necessary or if it starts to feel itchy.
  • Apply it before cycling and wash it at the end of each day.

FAQs Abour Cycling Anti-Chafing Creams

Can you use Vaseline instead of chamois cream?

Although many riders use Vaseline (petroleum jelly) as a budget anti-chafing cream, that has been proven to be a bad practice.

Washing your chamois from the petroleum jelly will be impossible. The Vaseline remaining can easily attract bacterias which could cause an infection.

Note: Be careful while buying the chamois cream. The creams for women lack menthol and mint oils as they are not good for sensitive female organs. Buying a unisex cream is the safest bet!
