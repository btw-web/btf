11 Best Road Bikes for Ladies [from Cheap to Top, from Beginner to Pro]

Womens road bikes! The best road bikes for woman from cheap to top
Budget & entry level race bicycles for beginners (under 1,000 / 500$) and pros. For short, tall, or overweight females

What’s a women’s road bike? Is there such a thing as the best road bike for women? Should beginner ladies racers ride female-specific road bikes? In this article we’ll answer all these questions and give you our list of best road bikes for women from the cheapest to the top, catering to the needs of ladies of all sizes, short, tall, overweight – come find the right bike for you.

Even in the field of pro-race woman cycling, the era of women-specific bikes is kinda over. The best women’s road bike most likely won’t differ much from the best for men.

Nowadays the industry understands that a bike should be tailored to the specific body characteristics of each individual, to best fit the rider is the paramount priority. So the marketing term “women’s bike” has been dropped by most of the best bike brands, which now offer mostly unisex options.

Although we’ll list some female-specific road bike models in this article, most bicycle brands just offer extra-small-size frames for short ladies plus customization options to suit the shorter torso and arms and comparatively longer legs often found in women’s anatomy.

Here we present you what we consider to be the best road bikes for women in 2022, on a scale from cheap, through affordable to the very top road bikes for ladies.

In the bottom section, we’ll discuss the main characteristics a woman should look out for when buying a road bike, plus some components and accessories that could be upgraded or swapped for comfort and performance.

2. Trek Domane SL 6

Best women’s road race bike from beginner to pro

check availability

One of the aims of Trek when they first introduced the new version of the Domane in 2020 was to give better fits and more options to female and male riders alike.

All the versions of the Trek Domane for 2022 are available in extra-small sizes and components can be swapped to more ladies-friendly ones to best fit anyone’s needs.

The Domane SL 6 is a hell of a road bike, packed with hi-tech features. The Trek patented IsoSpeed system for example is a great invention in suspension.

It maintains the diamond-shaped frameset geometry but “decouples” the seat tube from the top tube, allowing the seat tube to flex with the forces of the road. The result is a bike that moves following the lines of the road while still feeling stiff and efficient like a traditional race bike.

Other goodies are the micro-adjustments of the seat-post, allowing to regulate the height of the sit with the utmost precision, and the Blendr compatible stem that makes it possible to attach gadgets directly on the stem for a clean and minimal look.

The frame is also mudguard compatible, a great feature for those ladies that don’t fear wet weather.

The Trek Domane also comes in much cheaper versions, also a budget aluminum version that is a great cheap women’s road bike for less than $1,000, the Trek Domane AL. So have a look at all the built options for the Trek Domane, you might have to look no further.

How To Choose The Right Road Bike For Ladies

So how should we girls go about buying the best road bike for our needs? What differs a female bike from a male one?

As we already mentioned in the introduction, women commonly have short torsos and arms and longer legs, whereas men’s arms and torsos are longer.

For a man and woman of the same height, the woman’s race bike will ideally have a shorter top tube, and thus a shorter reach (the distance from the saddle to the handlebar), while the saddle will be lower and closer to the pedals.

The bike doesn’t have to be a female-specific frame, to achieve this – a shorter stem will shorten the reach and the saddle position can be easily adjusted.

Ladies also commonly have smaller hands compared to boys, which can make it hard to fully reach the brake levers on drop bars. Smaller levers could be a thing to look into if your hands are small.

Last but not least is the saddle, women have wider sit bones compared to most men – so the rear of the saddle usually needs to be wider and have a cut-out or lower pressure chamber for your soft parts.

Components to replace on a women’s road bike

Based on what we said, the components you might think about replacing to adapt your road bike to your body are:

  • shorter stem
  • femal specific saddle
  • narrow handlebar
  • shorter reach brake levers

All these things are relative and really depend on your unique body shape and bone structure – this means they are not necessarily true for you.

If you have wide shoulders you won’t need a narrower handlebar, a standard one will do just fine. Same for the short stem for ladies with longer arms.

A woman-specific saddle though might be worth a try. The pelvic bone structure is quite different between males and females, and that’s quite universally true. Women’s saddles are indeed designed to accommodate the peculiar shape of a woman’s pelvis.

FAQs About Women Gravel Bikes

What’s the difference between women’s and men’s gravel bikes?

A woman gravel bike should have a shorter top tube, narrower handlebars, smaller brake levers, and a women-specific saddle. The rest of the geometry and components can easily be the same as a men’s bike.

Should I get a female-specific gravel bike?

Not really. If you can find the right size for you there are no issues in purchasing a standard or men’s bike. Check with a professional bike fitter to be sure of the right modifications to apply, if any.

How do I choose a women’s road bike?

Consider your body characteristics and find a bike that fits you. Shorter reach for shorter arms, narrow handlebars for smaller shoulders.

What bike should a beginner get?

If you’re serious about starting road cycling you should spend at least a grant ($1,000) on your first bike, (see Fuji Sport and Cube Axial). Spending any less would result in frustration and a new bike as soon as possible. If instead you’re not sure road biking is for you, go for a cheap amazon bike like the Schwinn Volare 1400 for less than $500.
