Kona Rove LTD 2022 – Review! – A Gravel Classic for Bikepacking

Kona Rove 2022 Review – Specs, geometry, weight, tire clearance

  • Frame Material: Chromoly Steel
  • Wheel Size: 650b
  • Number of Spokes: 32
  • Number of Gears: 22
  • Max Tire Clearance: 48mm
  • Drive Train: Shimano GRX 810
  • Weight: 11.1 kg

The Rove by Kona is a popular bikepacking bike available in 650b and 700c version, in aluminium or steel frame and fork.

As it often happens with Kona products, the Rove has become the go-to bike for a plethora of bikepackers all around the world – and of course there’s a reason.

A bike made for grinding miles over miles across mixed surfaces, it’s kind of a light version of the Kona Sutra, a touring classic that we feature in our selection of the best touring bikes.

It has slightly more aggressive riding position compared to the touring sister. With a 1.52 stack/reach ratio (pretty gravel standard), 61 trail, 435 chainstay and 1,048 mm wheelbase.

While the declared tire clearance goes up to 48mm, it has been reported to fit up to 2.1″ tires.

The Kona Rove LTD 2022 comes with a Shimano GRX 31-48t x 11-34t 22spd – a wide set of gears although not as ample as the Sutra.

The price of a Kona Rove LTD in 2022 starts from $2,500.

FAQs about Kona Rove

What is the weight of a Kona Rove LTD?

A size 54 steel full stock build Kona Rove LTD weights 11.1kg or 24.25lbs

What size of tire can I put on?

The Kona Rove comes with 47mm tires and can fit easily up to 48c. The max tire clearance is up to 50mm but the risk is to have mud built up and the wheel stuck.

Is Kona a good bike brand?

Kona is not a colossus like Specialized, Trek, or Cannondale but it has a long history in the manufacturing of adventure bikes. Its most popular models, the Sutra and the Rove, are used by thousands of cycle tourers and bikepackers and have been proven reliable, comfortable and to offer great performance. Kona bikes are also great value for money.
