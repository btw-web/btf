How to choose the best clipless shoes for mountain biking

Weight

Lightweight shoes require less energy to rotate them around when pedaling. However, lightweight shoes are not always the best.

And that is because lightweight shoes compromise on support, stiffness, and protection. The importance of this factor varies with style. People tackling climbing lanes value lighter shoes more than the gravity-focused options.

Retention

There are numerous retention systems that can provide a comfortable and secure fit. Some of these systems include Boa dials and ratchet; Velcro and lace mix; Velcro; and laces. Some shoes have combined two of the above systems for a more personalized and tailored fit.

Durability and Sole Stiffness

The outsole of most shoes is made from rubber, nylon, or carbon fiber. These materials differ in terms of applications and performance.

Carbon fiber is stiff; therefore, ideal for cross-country-style MTB shoes. Rubber provides better grip. Nylon is not stiff, but it’s more durable and offers reliable comfort.
