What to keep in mind while buying the Best Handlebar Tape | Buyers Guide

As you have explored some of the coolest handlebar tapes in the preceding section, it is time for you to make a choice! But wait! It is not over yet.

As there are multiple characteristics in the above-described products, you need to sure about what to look for. So, let us dive into the factors that you need to keep an oversight on while making your final pick.

Thickness: The core feature of the tape that you need to consider is the thickness. This is essential as enough thickness will prevent your hands from getting blisters while riding. The cushioning also prevents sore palms. The most comfortable bar tapes are usually the thickest!

Traction or Grip: The traction offered by the right handle tape is one of the most crucial attributes that you need to check. The better your grip on the handlebar, the best and most comfortable the handling. Check for the knurled or cross-hatch design on the tape surface.

Flexibility: If you are worried about tapes hanging loose from the handlebar, then you need to focus on this aspect. Thinner and more flexible tapes wrap better around the handlebars and are easier to install.

Material: The choice of material affects the comfort level of the tape. Most of the best handlebar tapes are available in cork, rubber, leather, or synthetic material. It depends on you which one you want to choose. If you want to give your bike a luxurious look, go for the cork tape. It will be expensive, though. For better durability and vibration absorption, choose leather tapes. They wear out really slowly. For gel-based cushioning and lightweight design, you can go for the synthetic handlebar tape.

Color: You must be thinking why color is a deciding factor, right? Well, if you go for light-colored taps, the probability of them losing their graceful look will be high. On the other hand, going for dark colors will free you from the hassle of changing the tape after some time.
