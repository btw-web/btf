The Genesis Day One is really worth a review! A cheap gravel bike that is comfortable, reliable, low maintenance and looks great – Let’s see more in details.

Ok, this could make someone’s nose turn up, where are the gears? Is this a single-speed?

Well, that’s how Genesis launched the Day One, and although the bike raised some interest, it was still too niche.

With the 2021 version Genesis gave a completely new look and added a Shimano Nexus 8 speed hub, to keep the bike low-maintenance but give the owners some more options to tackle hilly routes.

Since most gravel bikes have very similar characteristics, we thought of adding a few different options among our bike reviews, maybe we could give you some ideas you haven’t thought of.

With their efficiency and low-maintenance requirements, single-cog drivetrains are already a popular option for commuting, but more and more cyclists are starting to use gear hubs bikes for adventure rides on bikepacking bikes.

If you are not planning on extreme rides, considering a hub gear gravel bike like the Genesis Day One 10 is far from foolish.

The steel frame and fork are high-quality Genesis stuff, with ample room for full-length mudguards for year-round, dry bottom commuting and off-road rides, while the Promax DSK-300 disc brakes ensure adequate stopping power.

The Genesis Day One 2022 is a great buy for those looking for a maintenance-free commuter that could double as a light adventure/gravel bike.
