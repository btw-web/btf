2010 In Review

Please note: I didn’t write any of this…

The stats helper monkeys at WordPress.com mulled over how this blog did in 2010, and here’s a high level summary of its overall blog health:

The Blog-Health-o-Meter™ reads Wow.

Crunchy numbers

About 3 million people visit the Taj Mahal every year. This blog was viewed about 58,000 times in 2010. If it were the Taj Mahal, it would take about 7 days for that many people to see it.

In 2010, there were 595 new posts, growing the total archive of this blog to 885 posts. There were 663 pictures uploaded, taking up a total of 169mb. That’s about 2 pictures per day.

The busiest day of the year was August 3rd with 737 views. The most popular post that day was Route Planning.

Where did they come from?

The top referring sites in 2010 were en.wikipedia.org, facebook.com, forum.ctc.org.uk, puglia2010.com, and middleagecyclist.blogspot.com.

Some visitors came searching, mostly for eurovelo 5, cardboard box, decathlon reading, eurovelo, and eurovelo5.
