Visit Angkor Wat by Bicycle

Angkor Wat is best seen by bicycle. There is your answer.

Ride past beautiful ancient trees, ancient temples and water channels-built thousands of years ago. Explore Angkor Wat by bicycle with our Itinerary and Maps and be confident that you wont miss a thing.

Adventuring by bicycle through Angkor Wat gives you time to wonder and absorb the magic of this place. Its all easy flat riding, you will be amazed at the temples to notice the km’s tick by

So, here is a our General information, Itineraries and Maps for,

  • One Day
  • Two Days
  • Three days

General Information

Where to Buy your Ticket

The official is at the Angkor enterprise official ticketing agency. if you choose to purchase through an agent in town, be careful they don’t charge you too much

Here are the prices as of Sept 2018

  • 1 Day – 37 USD
  • 3 Day – 62 USD
  • 7 Day – 72 USD

Where to rent a Bicycle

Bicycle will be sold at many local stores. Ask your hotel receptionist for your nearest dealer (approx. 2-3 USD/day)

Bicycle pre-ride check

  • Tyres – There should be no cracking
  • Brakes – Squeeze to check condition
  • Lock – We have heard stories of theft

*Take a picture of the bicycle, just like hiring a car, they will check for damage and paint at return

What to bring for the day

  • Ticket
  • Water
  • Snacks/Lunch
  • Mosquito repellent
  • Sunglasses
  • Hat / Sunscreen
  • Rainjacket (check the weather forecast)
  • Picnic blanket (You will find some great picnic spots, top tip!)

NOTE:

Pack light, you do not need to carry around so much all day, water and food should be your only real weight.

Cycle to and from Angkor Wat via the Charles De Gaulle Blvd (road). This has a cycle path both sides and all tuk tuk drivers and local traffic are aware of you. Be sure to take this path when cycling home in the evening as there are street lights illuminating the street.

BEST TIP:

If you are buying a 1-day ticket, you should arrive the day before, purchase your ticket after 5.00pm (Office closes at 5.30pm) and then you are free to see the sunset from one of the sunset points that afternoon.

Sunset times

Winter: 5:30, Spring/Fall/Autumn: 6:00, Summer: 6:30

The big 5

Just quickly, whatever itinerary you choose, here ore our top 5 sites/temples not to miss

  • Angkor Wat
  • Bayon temple
  • Ta Phrom temple
  • Preak Khan temple
  • Phnom Bakheng (sunset views)

1 Day Itinerary

(Check sunrise time with receptionist)
30 mins before sunrise Be riding out of your hotel to catch the iconic Angkor Wat sunrise shot.
8am Ride north to Bayan temple. There will be a crowd building here already, peaking at 10am, get there early
9-10am Visit "Terrace of the Elephants" (Optional) Intricate carvings into the wall.
10-12am Explore the temples that take your fancy on the way to Ta Phrom (We recommend Thommanon)
12-1pm Lunch, roll out that picnic blanket wherever you like.
1-3pm Visit Ta Phrom temple. Tomb raider temple and amazing trees growing through temple
3-4pm Visit Pre Rup (Optional as you will return same way) Hindu temple, impressive views.
4-5pm Ride back to Angkor Wat
Sunset Phnom Bakheng viewpoint (Optional). This will involve a small hike.

*Don’t wait too long watching the last moments of the sunset. Ride home safely with enough light. There is a bicycle path, but without lights it is a danger.

2 Day itinerary

We recommend exploring the 2 days in two sections. North and East. 

This is not an addition to the 1-day guide. It is a unique 2-day tailored guide

Day 1 of 2 – North Route

(Check sunrise time with receptionist)
30 mins before sunrise – Be riding out of your hotel to catch the iconic Angkor Wat sunrise shot.
8am Ride north to Bayan temple. There will be a crowd building here already, peaking at 10am, get there early
9-10am Visit "Terrace of the Elephants" (Optional) Intricate carvings into the wall.
10-11am Ride to Preah Khan. Beautiful gardens and suroundings
11-12am Explore Preak Khan
12-1pm Lunch, lay that picnic under a tree or wherever you find
1-2pm Visit Neak Pean (Optional) buddhist temple on a manmade island
2-3pm Ride the most beautiful section to Thannamon (follow our map!)
3-3.30 Explore Thannamon, and no tourists!
3.30-4.30 Ride back past Bayan and to the sunset point, Phnom Bakheng
Sunset Phnom Bakheng (optional). This will involve a small hike.

*Don’t wait too long watching the last moments of the sunset. Ride home safely with enough light. There is a bicycle path, but without lights it is a danger.

Day 2 of 2 – East Tour Route

(Check sunrise time with receptionist)
Sunrise Srah Srang, (optional) or take a sleep in. This is not as impressive as Angkor Wat, but less tourists.
7-9am Ta Phrom temple, Tomb raider filmed here. Jungle overgrowing temple, Wow!
9-11am Pre Rup, a great sunset temple. A Hindu temple
11-12am East Baray, explore this area of ruined temples
12-1pm Lunch, get that picnic rug out again!
1-3pm Ta Som (optional, but worth the pedaling) little brother temple to Ta Phrom
3-4pm Cycle back to Pre Rup
4-Sunset Options to wait at Pre Rup for sunset OR watch the sun set on Angkor Wat for the last time

*Remember that Charles De Gualle boulevard has only limited lighting, get home before its completely dark

3 Day Itinerary

First 2 days the same as the 2-day tour above

Day 3 of 3 – Exploring Route “The missed bits”

(Check sunrise time with receptionist)
Sunrise Sleep in, you’ve seen the best two already
Before 8am Go back to Bayan to see it without the crowds.
8-10am Explore Phimeanakas, you would have rode past this on the first days.
10-12am Ride to the west gate and then follow the small inside perimeter path south to Chrung temple
12-2pm A great place to have lunch here
2-4pm Feeling fit, cycle along the perimeter past the south gate to Hendrix temple.

*Remember that Charles De Gualle boulevard has only limited lighting, get home before its completely dark

Alternative for Day 3

There is a small temple called Banteay Srei approximately 18 km north of Angkor Wat. The name means “Citadel of the Women” and features amazingly hand carvings in pink sandstone.

You will need a tuk tuk to get there. We did not go, but fellow tourists we asked were “happy” that they spent half of the day on visiting this temple.
