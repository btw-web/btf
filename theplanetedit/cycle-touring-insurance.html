Cycle Touring Insurance: What You Need To Know and the 6 Best Insurance Providers

Finding a good travel insurance policy to cover you for a cycle tour is really important. The risks of cycling in foreign places are generally higher than cycling at home, and being stuck with a huge medical bill is something you really want to avoid.

And while it’s important to be covered for your trip, finding good cycle touring insurance can be a headache. A real headache.

Most travel insurance providers will only cover cycling if it’s incidental to your trip, i.e. if you hired a bike for a day during your two-week vacation, but not if the primary purpose of the trip is cycling. As you can see, cycle touring is not covered under these grounds.

Bicycle insurance and cycle touring insurance are two different things

Remember when I said that finding good cycle touring insurance can be a real headache? Well, one of the other issues is that most travel insurance policies won’t cover the theft of your bicycle. Touring bikes are considered to be extremely stealable (which they are) and so insurers don’t want to pay out for them.

Essentially, bicycle insurance and cycle touring insurance are two very different things.

Bicycle insurance covers theft, damage and vandalism to your bicycle.

Cycle touring insurance is travel insurance that will cover you for medical expenses, personal liability etc while you are cycling abroad.

Most insurance providers will either protect you or your bicycle, but very few will protect both under one policy. Those that do usually only cover a bike worth Â£1000 or less. If this is you, then great! But if you’re the proud owner of a Â£1000+ touring bike, this might not be good enough.

If you want your bicycle to be covered as well as yourself, and don’t want to spend a small fortune on insurance, you’ll likely need to take out two separate policies. One for you, and one for your bike.

To be fair, I imagine if you own an expensive touring bike, you likely already have insurance for it. Just check that this insurance covers you in the countries you’ll be visiting and allows for cycle touring as an activity. You’ll also want to check the insurance will still cover theft of the bike if it were to be taken from, say, a campsite or while it’s locked up outside a shop.

Luckily for me, my bike is not worth much in terms of actual money, so I don’t bother insuring it while I tour.

What you need to know when choosing cycle touring insurance

What doesn’t insurance cover?

Cycle touring insurance is unlikely to cover accidents that happen if:

  • You are under the influence of drugs or alcohol
  • You are not wearing a helmet
  • You are cycling recklessly or unlawfully, such as passing through a red light
  • You crash on a path or road where cycling is prohibited
  • You travel to countries against FCDO advice, e.g. Syria, Afghanistan

Make sure to read up on the small print to understand what might make any claims void.

Does the policy protect your bicycle as well as you?

As I mentioned previously, there are very few travel insurance policies that will cover the loss, theft or damage of an expensive bicycle. Those that do will usually only cover a bike up to the value of Â£1000. If your bike is worth more than this, consider taking out a separate bicycle insurance policy to protect it.

How long will you be touring?

Some cycle touring insurance policies have a limit of how long they will cover you. For example, Yellow Jersey will provide up to 120 days of insurance, whereas Safety Wing will cover you indefinitely on a monthly subscription.
