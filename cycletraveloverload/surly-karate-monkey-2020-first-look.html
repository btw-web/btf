Surly Karate Monkey 2020 First Look!

Read Time:4 Minute, 2 Second

Let me start this post be saying how much I love Surly! They make some of the best touring and bikepacking rigs out there. And that is no different for the Karate Monkey. My goal with this article is to help you understand more about this bike and hopefully this is some kind of guide to making a more informed purchasing decision if you are looking to buy one of these sick bike!

Surly Karate Monkey 2020

Lets first take a look at the all new Surly Karate Monkey 2020 model. The Karate Monkey is a multipurpose hardtail mountain bike. And the keyword here is ‘multipupose’.

This bike was originally designed as a 29er (and the frame that helped spark the 29” wheel movement), the latest Karate Monkey is designed to perform just as admirably as a 27.5+ rig as well.

It is up to date with modern trail standards. This means you get dropper post compatibility, 44mm headtube, Gnot-Boost rear spacing, thru-axles, suspension corrected fork. Along with the Rock Shox 35 Gold RL fork option you can also pick up the new 2020 with a rigid fork.

Different 2020 Surly Karate Monkey Models

Surly has made a range of different Karate Monkey bikes to choose from. Two of which have suspension forks and another three that are rigid. They differences comes down to colour and some small component changes.

Wet Clay Rigid

The new Karate Monkey Wet Clay is a rigid build with the full Surly 4130 Chromoly Steel fork and frame. Which features a SRAM SX EAGLE drivetrain which gives you a 30t crank at the front and 11-50t cassette at the rear. The bike comes with Tektro HD-M275 Hydraulic disc brakes. The stock handlebars are the Salsa Rustler 31.8. With this bike you the Promax 2-Bolt seatpost. Standard for these bikes is the WTB ST i40 TCS rims and Surly Dirt Wizard 27 x 3”, 60 tpi Tubeless Ready tires.

Toxic Tangerine Suspension

One of the unique differences with this bike is the Rock Shox 35 Gold RL suspension forks. SRAM NX EAGLE drivetrain with 30t crankset and 11-50t cassette. Also the brakes are SRAM DB Level. Still the same size 180/160 rotors but the SRAM Centerline compared to the Tektro for the Wet Clay. The seatpost is a TranzX SP15, dropper post 100mm travel on XS 120mm travel on SM/MD/LG/XL.

Lemon Lime Soda Suspension

The Lemon Lime has a RockShox Sektor RL, 27.5 140mm travel, 51mm offset, 15×110 front axle, Disc suspension fork. With a SRAM NX Eagle rear derailleur with a SRAM Descendant 32T crankset with 11-50t cassette. With SRAM Level hydraulic disc brakes. You get the same TranzX SP15 seatpost, but it has a 80mm travel on XS/SM 120mm travel on MD/LG/XL.

Hi-Viz Black & Porta Potty Blue

These last two bike options are both rigid with all Surly steel. They come with SRAM NX that features a 30t crank and Sunrace 11 speed 11-42t cassette. And SRAM Level brakes. With the Promax 30.9mm seatpost.

You do get the clearance for up 29” x 2.5” or 27.5” x 3” tires. It is really a affordable MTB bike that works so well as a bikepacking machine. I know some bikepackers personally who rock this bike and it is some dreamy stuff! There are a few recommendations I would consider if looking to use this rig for adventure cycling!

Surly Karate Monkey Bikepacking

Changing the handlebars to something like the Surly Moloko Bar or any other bikepacking appropriate handlebars. Flat bars from my personal experience I don’t like as much as other alternative handlebars. You get more mounting options and hand positions. You should check out this article about, The Best Swept Back Handlebars – Ultimate List Of 22 MTB Bars. Which suggests some of the more popular handlebar bar options for bikepacking. These swept back bars of a generous amount of sweep which helps with a more upright posture and comfort for long days bikepacking. But having that said about flat bars you could upgrade grips with something like the Ergo grips.
