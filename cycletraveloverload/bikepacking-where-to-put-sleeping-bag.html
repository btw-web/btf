Bikepacking Where To Put Sleeping Bag

Read Time:2 Minute, 57 Second

When packing your gear for your next bikepacking adventure. It can be a little challenging to figure out where everything goes, over time and experience you learn to find a spot for everything.

When it comes to storing your sleeping bag on your bikepacking bike. I have found the best spot is in a saddle bag or seat bag. Without the compression sack. This allows your to really cram the sleeping bag down far into the saddle bag and making the most of every bit of space.

So are you new to the bikepacking world? Want to know where and how to pack a sleeping bag with all your other gear?

I come from a touring background. Which is where I got started in the world of bike travel. Most bike tourers just pack their sleeping bag in a pannier as this method of bike travel allows you to have so much storage space. So even having the sleeping bag in the stuff-sack it is no problem.

But if you ditch the panniers and go full bikepacking bag setup, finding a spot for your sleeping bag can be somewhat troublesome. 

That’s why I wrote this article to give you inspiration and new packing ideas, for your next overnighter or week long adventure. 

There are so many different angles you can approach this with.

So in this article, just like a previous one, which is very popular at the moment, Where should I store my tent Bikepacking? I go through all the possible ways to pack a sleeping bag for your next bikepacking adventure!

We recommend packing your sleeping bag in either a saddle bag or handlebar bag. Because a sleeping bag is a bulky but fairly light item. These two spots are best. If you pack too much heavier items in your handlebar bag or seat bag it can drastically change the way your bike handles.

It is ideal to pack your sleeping bag without the stuff suck into your seat bag or handlebar bag. But if you bags aren’t waterproof then make sure you use a waterproof dry bag before packing in either of these bags.

It works well if you cram the sleeping bag first into the saddle bag right down to the base of the bag as the sleeping bag contours well to the pointy end of saddle bags. Then you can pack other items like clothing and a sleeping pad. Or even use the saddle bag as a camp specific bag, where all the items to be unpacked at camp will be located. Then you don’t have to take out items from your bag that are not needed.

After a week or so of bikepacking you will get used to where to pack everything and what is easiest for you. It is worth noting also that you might want heavier items at the end closest to the seatpost if your bag sways too much. The bag I use doesn’t sway at all.

Also another seat bag options which is a little different to your typical bikepacking saddle bags, is the bag from Carradice The Camper Longflap.

You could also even try a dry bag strapped to your handlebars if you are on a budget. Which also works well. We recommend using the Sea to Summit Stretch Loc Straps which work a treat!
