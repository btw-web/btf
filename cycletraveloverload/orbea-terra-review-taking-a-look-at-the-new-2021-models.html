Orbea Terra Review – Taking A Look At the New 2021 Models

Read Time:8 Minute, 42 Second

The Orbea Terra is a great gravel bike option. Offering a large range of price options for all cyclists. Their new 2021 line up includes 11 different models. Ranging from up to $4,599.00 to $2,099. So what is the difference in all these bikes and what one might be better for you?

Well we are going to dive deep into the 2021 Orbea Terra line up and see!

The Terra is Orbea’s all road, gravel/cross bike. Designed to be at home on dirt, gravel and just about any other condition you might find your self riding. Although I must say it is more a gravel/cross bike than a mountain bike.

Featuring that more upright ‘all-road’ geometry, sitting you in a more comfortable all day riding position compared to a typical road geometry. But from what I can tell from the picture above. It is not the most upright positioning. Like a touring bike, for example. However it should again be more suited for cross riding and all day riding, with ease. And for different frame sizes the build has precise angles and dimensions to have max comfort.

Making this a good bikepacking option. If you are looking to ride mainly roads and hit some light gravel here and there. So you can explore that road less travelled.

The Terra has a decent amount of versatility when it comes to riding options. Don’t get me wrong there are other bikes out there with more bells and whistles. Especially to assist you when exploring and mounting gear. However, you get enough on this rig! Again with the ability to tackle, gravel, road, CX, winter training and commuting. Decent tire clearance with (from what I could find when researching), a max 40mm gravel tires to 28mm for road cycling. And you get the option to mount accessories, like mud guards.

One of the main details that Terra have highlighted in this bike. Is it’s comfort and stiffness. With small sections on the frame designed to absorb vibrations. And shapes that are optimised for compliance and comfort. While the bike also features Monocoque technology which is an advanced carbon construction technique.

Obera Terra Geometry

Now that we have got the bike line up out of the way. Let’s touch on what the overall geometry of the Terra is like when compared to some other gravel bikes and touring bikes for that matter. To understand where the overall geometry sits. For this I will give some examples of other bikes, that are fast (racer gravel bikes, comfortable all day gravel grinders and touring bikes) and let’s see where this bike fits in.

So above you can see the bikes we are comparing. Warning, it is not exact as the Terra and Grail are both Medium sized and I have opted for 54cm for both the Sutra and LHT. However, technically a medium sized bike ranges from 54 to 56 cm.

The head tube length on the Terra is 156 mm which is longer then some gravel bikes which gives evidence to a more comfortable upright riding position on the handlebars. Wheelbase is a little longer then some faster/racing gravel bikes with 1043 mm while not as long as a touring bikes or gravel bikes suited more for touring. Was great to see it have a 622 mm front centre which is comparable to the Sutra meaning you get just enough clearance from your feet hitting the tire while cornering. However the seat tube length is 554 mm which is considerably higher than all bikes we compared it to. Which in theory puts the rider at a higher seated position. It also has a rake of 51 mm and trail of 69mm.

Looking at these numbers we can figure out the Terra’s trail of 69mm is on the higher end of other gravel bikes which means it with a more stable feel at the front and potentially a little sluggish compared to gravel bikes with trails in that 50 to 65 mm range. Also the chain stay length at 430 mm is in that mid range for comparable gravel bikes. Meaning it will feel like a all-rounder and a little quick in the rear.
