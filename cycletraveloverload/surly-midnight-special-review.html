Surly Midnight Special Review

Read Time:4 Minute, 49 Second

I am a Surly bike fanatic! Everything about them I adore. So this article is all about the Midnight Special. I have heard great things about this bike and am looking into getting on. So I scoured the corners of the inter-webs to find all the information, good and not so good about this bike.

I have included all of my findings in this article. Consider it a well balanced review looking into many aspects of this bike. To hopefully give you more of a understanding whether you should or maybe, should not consider this bike, for your next adventure bike outing.

I personally have the Surly Long Haul Trucker, (check out my first hand review with over 10,000 km on the clock). My LHT has been customised to be a cross between a tourer and a off-road gravel eater. As the Midnight Special is a road plus bike that is designed to provide speed and comfort on all-day rides, it still has the capability to ride beyond the fresh pavement. The Midnight Special seems like the perfect option. That would be epic for some mostly road bikepacking explorations.

Great Things About The Midnight Special

  • The Midnight Special is fitted out with some nice components. Including the Shram Rival group-set – 11 speed
  • If you are a roadie then this would be perfect for you making you feel right at home
  • Has some eyelets on the front forks and the option for fitting a rear rack
  • The bike uses 650b sized wheels
  • The Midnight Specials is a combination between a racer and a gravel/touring bike. With the shorter frame geometry making it feel quick when navigating and steering. While it offers comfortability with the bouncy, vibration reducing 47 mm tyres. resulting in a amazing ride sensation
  • Has the responsiveness of a road bike and the ride feel of other touring bike options, that don’t maneuverer so quickly.
  • On the road is where it excels, it is quick and nimble on the roads and when you hit the gravel it holds up just as well.
  • You could mistaken this for a ‘gravel race bike’. The smaller wheels allow for better steering direction offer more precision when navigating around rocks and such. Compared to a larger wheel gravel or touring bike.
  • The Midnight Specials is fairly light weight which adds to its manoeuvrability.

Not So Great Things About The Midnight Special

  • It is a little on the expensive side at $2000

Keep These Things In Mind

  • If you are looking at some low rider front racks they should fit okay. But it may not be the best due to the geometry of the frame
  • It is a fat tyre ‘road bike’ with 47 mm WTB Horizon tyres
  • The geometry is more in the road bike category, then a gravel bike geometry
  • Wheel base is shorter
  • The short chainstay comes in at 425
  • If you are looking at this bike for loaded touring and riding uphill terrain when fully loaded. You may want to look at getting a wider cassette range.
  • The frame uses the ‘flat mount standard’ for the disc breaks. You also get the option on the frame and fork to run post mount brakes with adapters.
  • Max tire clearance for the Midnight Special frame is 650b x 60 (27.5 x 2.35in) or 700c x 42
  • What range and size of people will the Midnight Special fit? The toptubes range from 500–625mm across eight different frame sizes (40 to 64) and Surly’s hope is that this spread will work for riders between about 5’0” and 6’8”.
  • Also the sizing can be a little tricky to get right. It is recommended to go to your local bike dealer and get fitted and buy it from them.
  • The stock 47 tyres don’t have all the much tread
  • It is not suited for extended long gravel riding. Due to the dimensions of the Midnight Special, there is potential for fatigue in the handlebar position

  • So can you ride it off-road then? Well, as long as the off-road isn’t too gnarly, game on. The geometry of the Midnight Special is road bike, but the frame is built to be dependable and some gravel or smooth off-road is perfect. The steel frame and larger tires do their best to absorb bumps, while the increased tire traction allows riders to point the bars at any obstacle with confidence.
  • It falls more in the road bike category then the touring and gravel category
  • You can add after market forks, like the Whisky No. 9 CX fork is 395 x 45mm and is compatible with the stock 12mm thru axle wheel and flat mount disc brakes. The Midnight Special comes with a stock fork length is 400mm and the offset is 40 or 50mm.

Final Thoughts

If you are looking for a interesting road bike option to take on group rides but also are looking for a bike to do some light and shorter style touring. Then the Midnight Special is a great option for you.

Strengths
  • Superb handling
  • Very harmonious overall package
  • Robust and versatile
  • Great long-term value
Weaknesses
  • Weight
  • High price considering the spec
