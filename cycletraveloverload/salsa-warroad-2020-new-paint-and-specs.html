Salsa Warroad 2020 – New Paint and Specs!

Read Time:6 Minute, 53 Second

Salsa have just recently released there new line up of the 2020 Warroad. While it is challenging times for all around the COVID19 crisis that has hit us all. What a way to lighten the mood with some new bikes. And who doesn’t like a 80’s t-shirt themed all-road bike?

The newest in the Salsa all-road line up, it is a road bike that is capable to tackle some grave and off road riding conditions. Or as Salsa like to say “80% road, 20% whatever”. Built from a carbon fibre frameset, making this bike responsive and lightweight. With the added feature of the Class 5™ VRS when the rough gets rough or runs out!

The bike is designed around a endurance road geometry, which offers comfort for long days in the saddle. While also having fork and frame mounts for extra storage and mounting options, for those bikepacking trips. Also compatible with both 700c and 650b wheels.

The new 2020 Warroad comes in a variety of options, from Shimano Apex 1 to SRAM’s Force AXS. Available in a bunch of fresh new colours. The Warroad is made to be predominantly ridden on paved surfaces, but still has that option to take off road. The Warroad is said to be the most compact all-road bike in Salsa’s lineup, which is great for smaller riders. The fork has enough clearance for up to 700 x 30C or 650 x 42B tires with mudguards.

But lets take a look at all the different options you get to choose from. Here is a quick look at the bikes side by side!

Warroad Carbon Force AXS 700

Starting with these high end level 2020 Warroad option is the Force AXS 700. As you can tell it is a bike with some pizaz! The pink and yellow fade make the bike look like a vintage 80’s tee! I think I am getting Bubble O’Bill vibes from this bike, taking me back to my childhood. But in a good way! You get a full electronic drivetrain, with the Force eTap Groupset. With the SRAM Force AXS DUB, 48/35t crank and SRAM Force AXS cassette. There are a few things that are different on this bike compared to the others. Apart from the groupset you get the WTB SL8 142 Pro saddle, Salsa Guide Carbon seatpost, DT Swiss CR 1600, 12 x 100 mm, 700c rims, and the Salsa Cowbell Carbon handlebars. Everything is upgraded to carbon which is common with high end bikes like this. Price for this bike is $5,899 retail.

Warroad Carbon Ultegra 700

These 700 c bikes all come with the Teravail Rampart 700c x 28 mm, Tan sidewall tires, with slightly varying rims or wheelsets. The rims on this Ultegra 700 are the DT Swiss C 1800, 12 x 100 mm, 700c. Along with that you get the obvious Shimano Ultegra groupset with Shimano Ultegra, 50/34t crank and Shimano R7000, 11–32t, 11-speed cassette. Along with the Salsa Cowbell Deluxe handlebars (not carbon), WTB SL8 142 Race saddle, Salsa Guide Deluxe seatpost (same for the 105 models). All for a retail price of
$4,099

Warroad Carbon 105 650

The Warroad Carbon 105 is available in a 650B or 700C option. This 650B has nice wider tires if that is your thing. Instead of 28mm tires you get Teravail Rampart 650b x 47 mm tires for the extra suppleness. Everything on these bikes are the same except for the wheels. The 650B has the Shimano Tiagra 12 x 100 mm hub, WTB ST i23 TCS 2.0 650b rims while the 700C has the Shimano Tiagra 12 x 100 mm hub, WTB ST i21 TCS 2.0 700c rim. You still get a 2×11 drivetrain with the same amount of teeth as the Ultegra model. Both 700c and 650b are priced $3,199

Warroad Carbon 105 700

I am a fan of the fire red of both the 650b and 700c 105 Warroad bikes. I personally would opt for the 650b version as that would be the better option when it comes to off-road endeavours. But if you want a reasonably price endurance bike that is great for mostly road but able to go further the 700c option is available for sale at REI.com for $3,199.00

Warroad Carbon Apex 1 650

Then there was two! The good old 1X gravel builds, they are a personal favourite. These last two bikes again are both similar and only differ on wheelsets. They have mechanical disc brakes compared to the other bikes hydraulic brake options. And are 1×11 with SRAM Apex 1, 42t crank and SRAM PG-1130, 11–42t, 11-speed cassette. The 650b has the Teravail Rampart 650b x 47 mm tires. Pried at $2,599 on REI.com

Warroad Carbon Apex 1 700

Again with the 700c you get the same bike but with Teravail Rampart 700c x 28 mm tires. And priced the same at $2,599.
