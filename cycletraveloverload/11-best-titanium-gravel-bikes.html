11 Best Titanium Gravel Bikes

Read Time:6 Minute, 39 Second

Titanium bikes are some of the most high-end bikes that are the perfect machines for off-road adventures! In this article we dive deep into some of the best titanium gravel bikes on the market. When it comes to frame materials, nothing brings the WOW factor quite like Titanium. This magical metal combines the excellent ride feel of a steel frame, but without the weight penalty. Not to mention, just the beauty of a Titanium frame will keep your riding buddies drooling while they are struggling to hold your wheel. So without further ado here are the bikes in no particular order.

Bossi Bicycles Grit

The Bossi Grit Ti bike is available in various different builds, GRX 2X, FORCE 1, FORCE 1, AXS. Frameset is available from $3,570.00 and the lowest complete bike option, the GRX 2X is $7,115.00​. Born to ramble, the Grit is the type of bike you can take anywhere and do anything on, blending lightweight performance with ultimate durability. It is made from a 3AL-2.5V Titanium frame with butted seatpost, hydroformed top tube and oversized downtube. AL-4V press-fit bottom bracket. Bossi Torayca T800 Monocoque carbon flat-mount disc fork with triple rack mounts. Able to run both 700c or 650B wheels and a clearance for 45c (700c) and 2″ (650b) tires.

Lynskey GR300

So who is this bike really for? If you are keen to get out a bikepack or you need road, gravel, rugged commuter this bike ticks all the boxes. Plus you get that titanium comfort ride with a smooth finish. The bikes features a adventure-driven geometry. With clearance for 700c x max 45 or 27.5 x max 2.1″ tires. With a 3AL-2.5V tubeset for responsiveness, stability, durability, and comfort. And the option for mounting heaps of gear. With 1 top tube mount and 3 water bottle mounts and rack and fender mounts. Also the bike has a tapered headtube which makes for a precise handling bike when paired with the Lynskey’s Carbon Gravel Fork. Prices start from $3,400.00 (for a complete standard bike build)

Curve GXR “Kevin”

The Kevin is available as a frameset. You can also order a complete bike, but only based on availability. You may need to contact Curve if you are interest in this rig. To make the Kevin, Curve had to take the fun of a cyclocross bike, fused in some adventure feels and rolled it into one neat do-it-all package that can take you anywhere. You get the option for 700c or 650B. With 700c wheels with 28–45mm tyres for Road/Gravel/Cyclocross or go full HellCat with 650b (27.5”) wheels running 1.8–2.2” tyres. Frameset starts at $3,999 Aud

Sage Barlow Titanium Rival 1

Here we have an exclusive titanium bike build from Jenson USA. This bike has some history it was named after the that brought pioneers over the Cascade Mountains on the final leg of the Oregon Trail. It has a more aggressive geometry and handling to their road bike. But it still burs the line with comfort and toughness of the like of all-road bikes. You do get the option for mounting with integrated fender and rack mounts and three water bottle cage mounts. You get the strong, light and durable DT-Swiss C 1800 wheels and the SRAM Rival Drivetrain. It is a solid bike that I have heard the Ti frame climbs so well. It is a perfect do it all bike. Can’t go wrong with this bike for the epic gravel adventure, rainy commute to work, pounding out hours of base season miles, or a spirited ride with your buddies chasing KOMs! Checkout Jenson USA for this rad gravel bike build – priced at $5099.99

Litespeed Ultimate Gravel

The Litespeed Ti gravel bike is described as a aerodynamic, vertically compliant, dirt road speed demon with room for 45 mm tires. This bike has some history it was first introduced in the USPRO National Championship back in 1987 with wins under its belt. It is a bike that has race like tendencies but also packing stability for gear, while also climbing and descending with ease. Price starts at $4,649.00

Bearclaw Thunderhawk

I am new to being introduced to the Thunderhawk just found out about this bike a few days ago, if you are after a full titanium gravel bike. This is definitely a great option! Just look at this thing it is a weapon of a bike! It is available in two models; the high end Force 1 with Hydraulic Disc brakes and the Rival 1 with Mechanical Disc brakes. The Rival option is priced at $3,890 with the Force 1 is $1,800 more at $5,690.

Put in Bearclaw’s words the “Thunderhawk lends itself as a performance-minded 650b road plus bike that’s equipped to handle all road adventure rides, mixed-terrain endurance races, gravel grinders, and long bikepacking trips.” You get all the rack, fender and other mounting options you could dream of and the bike fits up to a 700x50c (29×1.9″) or 650x60c (27.5×2.35″) tires.

Ribble Adventure Ti – Sram Rival

Are you after the ultimate adventure titanium bike? I think I found one. Along with the Thunderhawk and Kevin bikes these are my top three Ti adventure rigs that all offer something great! The Adventure Ti from Ribble features a lightweight Titanium frame matched with a carbon fork and user friendly double-tap gear shifting of the SRAM Rival 1 x 11 speed setup. The bike comes with large volume WTB Ranger 2.8 tires and flared drop handlebars, offering control and clearance whilst using a handlebar bag and riding on the drops. Price from $2,463.78

Alchemy Ronin Ti

The Ronin Ti has been purpose-built for gravel riding, with the rider in mind! the S-bend seat and chain stays dampen any harsh feedback to the rider and keep the rear wheel tracking, all the while offering increased tire clearance for up to 45mm 700c tires or 2.1s with 650. You can pick up the base model Shimano GRX from $6,499

Genesis Croix De Fer Ti

I am a fan of the Genesis gravel bike line. That is no different to their De Fer Ti. Providing a still and efficient platform made from double-butted Titanium. They have kept the mudguard eyelets, rear rack mounts and generous tyre clearances. Offering a all round capable bike for any adventure you may have planned. Priced from £3699.99

Rondo Ruut Ti Gravel Bike

If you are in Australia and looking for a accessible bike. i know some of these options are only available in the States and UK. This rig is available at Wiggle Australia. Thanks to the titanium frame and carbon TwinTip fork it is built strong, comfortable and stylish. This fork has a specific feature which allows you to change the geometry to suit the terrain/riding type. The adjustable dropouts, can be set in the “HI” setting for steeper angles for a low and aggressive road bike feel, or the “LO” setting for a slightly longer wheelbase and slacker angles, making it ideal for endurance or off-road adventure riding. Priced at $6,799.00 AUD at Wiggle

Moots Routt 45

This rig is perfect for gravel and dirt riding, adventure riding, longer touring, and bikepacking! The shortened stays give the ROUTT 45 increased snappiness for climbing and cornering, while retaining the stable handling and comfort. it is optimised for 700 x 30-45mm tires, with a maximum tire size of 700 x 45mm. It has a pretty cool brakes, featuring the 6/4 titanium 3D-printed flat-mount disc mounts. Which are said to offer perfect rear wheel and brake alignment. Priced from $7,942.00
