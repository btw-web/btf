Tyres

Depending on the type of bicycle travel you do. Will depend on the type of tyres you use on your bike.

I have tested and am wanting to test a range of different tyres since I have been bike touring.

  • Gravel / Off-Road Tire
  • Mostly Bicycle Touring on road (little gravel)
  • Hybrid Style Tire for a bit of both

Gravel / Off-road type riding conditions

For this type of riding, more of the Bikepacking stuff. I have been using the WTB Riddler 45C Tyres. Nice chunky tyre, that as more tread on the out side of the tyre and more to the centre is has a little less. Helping with traction. and a better rolling resistance.

This tyre is not necessarily recommended for just on road bike touring. It has gravel and bikepacking style of riding in mind.

Bicycle Touring Tyres for Mostly Road

My first tyres on my touring bike were the Schwalbe Range Cruiser. They are good for mostly road bike touring. And seem to have lasted for over a couple 1,000 km. The rear tyre ended up wearing out the most. As a large amount of the load was on the back of the bike. Since using them I would prefer a tyre that is a little wider.

The other tyre that is more suited for road touring and a little off-roading. Is the Schwalbe Marathon Plus. This is a very popular tyre that is recommended by many bicycle tourers.

The reviews are quite phenomenal for these tyres. Someone had ridden up to 10,000 miles and they finally got a puncture, but this was at the point when the tyre was almost bald. Most common amount of miles one can expect with these tyres is any around 2,000 to 3,000 miles.

They are heavier then a standard tyre like the one mentioned before.
