17 Best Convertible Backpack Panniers in 2019!

Read Time:11 Minute, 17 Second

Ever since I found out about panniers that convert to backpacks, were a thing, I have been amazed. Such a useful product idea. These pannier backpacks are the best if you want to get of your bike and do some walking. You just take the pannier of and with the convertible back straps it becomes a backpack. It is always good when you buy a product that has multiple purposes.

Perfect for the daily commuter that does not want to carry their luggage on their back while cycling. But stills wants to cart their stuff around with them into work easily.

The bags included include innovative ways in which the clips to the rack are hidden away, or designed in other unique ways. Not obstructing the back and causing discomfort while in use as a backpack.

I myself have had the issue of carting around a heavy Ortlieb pannier in one hand while I go into libraries while on tour. I also share a nifty gadget that converts your current Ortlieb panniers into a backpack. There so good I am considering getting one!

So in a attempt to ‘shop around’ I searched the web for all the bags I could find. And I compiled the best convertible pannier backpacks and included it in this article in hopes that you get some benefit out of it.

Things to consider when looking at pannier backpacks

  • How does it hold up, in terms of durability?
  • Does it get dirty? and how easy is it to wash?
  • Has that specific pannier backpack had problems with hooks getting bent or loose?
  • Insure the size and dimensions, prices, weight and style all add up to your personal needs and requirements. Would your laptop fit and other gear you intend to use it for?
  • How easy is it to convert into a backpack or pannier?
  • Is it comfortable to wear as a backpack?
  • If you are only using one pannier on your bike rack when fully loaded, riding can change your bike control and balance
  • Is it a waterproof pannier backpack?
  • How does the pannier backpack fir onto your current bike rack setup?

Blackburn Wayside Backpack Pannier

The Wayside is a very stylish waxed canvas backpack pannier. The laptop pocket on the inside of the bag ends a few inches above the base of the bag meaning that your laptop stays of the base of the bag.

The Wayside attaches to a bike rack with velcro straps. That does take a little bit longer then expected (around 1 to 2 minutes). Wrapping the velcro and stowing the straps is slower than standard pannier hanging hooks.

Having that said the velcro straps seem to be thick enough to be functional and quality. If you are looking at converting quality from backpack to pannier you may want to look into the banjo brothers convertible instead (mentioned below).

The water bottle webbing on both sides of the Wayside are large enough to store a standard bike bottle easily or a medium sized reinforced Ulock.

This backpack pannier is a budget friendly option, pricing comes in at a reasonable $79.95 on Amazon. Other bags in comparison are more expensive or require proprietary rack systems.

Banjo Brothers Convertible Backpack Panniers

The Banjo Brothers delivers the goods with a waterproof backpack/pannier. Quick to attach and take off to your bike rack. It is also comfortable to wear on the back. They have really thought about how this bag is intended to be used.

Larger sized backpack that gives you potential to store heaps of gear. Very quality, versatile and durable bag, seems to last a while and stand up to wear and tear over time.

The inside of the bag does not have a organised section, just one big area to chuck stuff in, which is still great.

Some users of this bag have mentioned that they have had waterproof issues. You need to make sure when using this bag to fold the top over twice. Also the tow outer pockets are not quite waterproof.

Other things about this backpack pannier

  • The top flap does not cover the bag opening when in “pannier” mode. Instead, it covers the backpack straps. I’d be more confident of the waterproofness with an extra layer over that opening.
  • In pannier mode, the backpack straps are folded on themselves and held up with horizontal loops located on the straps. I’m not fond of this arrangement and instead use the sternum strap clips to pull the straps up.
  • The bottom of the pannier is secured with a closed triangle hook. The bag comes with two small metal hooks to add an anchor point to racks that don’t have a peg or similar protrusion.

This is another good budget bike bag option, just like the Blackburn Wayside. A review on Amazon summed it up nicely, “…..If you are on a budget and looking for a bike bag, save yourself the sunk costs of bags you don’t like, and just buy this one.” The Banjo Brothers Pannier Backpack comes in at $89.99 on Amazon.

Arkel Bug Pannier Backpack

Although a pricer bag compared to the first two mention in this article. The Arkel Bug is well thought out and sturdy. Working very well as a pannier, with a extra clip, making sure it stays well secure to the rack.

It is a mediocre backpack. The stiff backing is somewhat comfortable. While the hook sometimes ends up poking into your back. It seems all the quality making it a average backpack makes up for it as a pannier.

It is easy to hook the bag on and off onto the bike rack in pannier mode. Also includes a good array of compartments and excellent storage capacity.

Price on arkel-od.com is $214.99 CAD

Timbuk2 Deploy Convertible Backpack Pannier

A very stylish backpack pannier, one of the better looking ones on the market, I must say I personally like it.

A super spacious, easy clipping mechanisms, and super comfortable to wear as a backpack.

The bag is waterproof keeping clothes dry while being ridden during the winter through rain and wind.

Sadly, it has to be mentioned that a customer did have a clip issue where it broke from the bag after about 6 months of use. Still a really good bag but just not sure about its strength with the clips.

Costs $179.00 on Amazon

Two Wheel Gear Pannier Backpack Convertible 1.1

The Two Wheel Gear pannier backpack is $159.99 on Amazon. But for the quality you get it seems worth it.

The material is great and very functional, it looks fantastic, its easy to attach and take off from the bike rack. 

It would be better if the bag was slightly bigger, it struggles to fit a 15″ laptop.

NorthSt Bags Woodward Pannier Backpack

This bag is handmade and is waterproof, including a lifetime warranty. Allows for quick and easy pannier to backpack conversion, by tucking straps into a flap.

When buying a Woodward, have in mind that they hang rather low, so having low rider racks with these bags are not ideal.

$249.99 on northstbags.com

Ortlieb Vario Pannier Backpack

Good old Ortlieb provide the goods again with a pannier and backpack combo. You know it is waterproof if Ortlieb made it.

Comes in two different clamping size options to racks. The Quick-Lock3.1 or the Quick-Lock2.1 mounting system. The backpack straps hide away nicely, so when in pannier mode you would never know that it is a backpack too.

It does however require a few extra steps when installing. But it the mounting hardware as proven to be solid and durable.

The main compartment of the bag requires you to roll it closed. And as it is a velcro closure it is accessible to open when riding your bike in pannier mode.

As a backpack it is very comfortable and offer a generous amount of storage space.

From $169.95

To transform the bag, just unzip the back of the bag and fold the back panel upwards to reveal two padded shoulder straps that can be used for more comfortable transporting.
