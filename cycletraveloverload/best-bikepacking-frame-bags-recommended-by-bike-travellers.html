Best Bikepacking Frame Bags – Recommended By Bike Travellers!

Read Time:8 Minute, 49 Second

So you are looking for a frame bag? This article has compiled all the best frame bag brands into one resource. And we are going to talk about each option. I asked over 80 Bike Travellers what they personally use for a bikepacking frame bag and there is some great options here.

When I was looking for a bikepacking frame bag. I wanted something that was rather large in volume, to make the most of the frame triangle space. A full frame bag that is either custom made or available off the shelf, is the best option for extra storage for gear and food. As bikepacking is much more of a minimalist approach to bike travel then cycle touring.

But where do you store your water bottles with a full-size frame-bag?

This is a question that I wound was asked a lot by those who are new at adventure cycling. It makes sense, if you use a full frame bag you are using that prime realestate for a bag, so water about the water bottles. There are many alternative ways to store water on your bike. I wrote a article sharing 25 ways to store your water bottles on your bike. But here a few solutions.

  • Stem/feed bags or water bottle bags are a great solution, allowing you to hold 2 x 1L bottles on either side of your stem.
  • Some cargo cages are another awesome way to store water bottles. The Blackburn Outpost Cargo cage allows for water bottle cages to be attached to the inner side of the cage while still allowing for a dry bag to be fixed onto the fork.
  • You can also get the WOHO ANTI SWAY Saddlebag Stabiliser which allows for water bottle cages to be attached to either side of the saddle bag.
  • You can use something like a camelback bladder and store it in a frame bag as well. Some frame bags allow for a the straw to be fed through the bag and out so you can access it while riding.
  • Some frame bags are even designed to fit at least one water bottle on your frame while using the rest of the space for the bag.

What to pack in a frame bag?

When packing for a bikepacking trip. Gear distribution is something to think about. If you are running a saddle bag it is best to store the lighter stuff to prevent sway and the bag form sagging. While the frame bag is best for the heavier gear, as it sits in the middle of the bike it lowers your centre of gravity and balances out the ride best. Food is mainly what I pack in my frame bag and my cooking stove, also other heavier items.

Okay now lets get into the bikepacking frame bag brands and bag options that are the most popular and highly recommended by other bike travellers.

Rogue Panda Designs

Rogue Panda make some quality, lightweight bike bags for bike adventures! They believe that the backcountry should feel like home. So they make products that work so well that you hardly notice they’re there. From frame bags that fit your bike perfectly, to incredibly comfortable backpacks, they make gear with you in mind. Inspired by the landscapes of Northern Arizona. Their home base is in Flagstaff.

The make custom frame bags to fit your bike perfectly! They also make other universal bags but none of which are frame bags. They have five different frame bag styles to choose from. Starting from $140 to $220 or more. They even have a added touch, offering a fully custom State Flag Frame Bag. Their current lead time is 2 to 4 week.

Rockgeist

Another great custom frame bag making is Rockgeist with X-Pac and Cordura fabrics. They offer a wide range of styles and designs for your bike. Their lead time is approximately 4 to 6 weeks.

While you also get the option to buy some frame bag extras; full bolt on strapless, multi-color frame bag, Fiber Flight Zipper upgrade, Frame Guard Helicopter Tape, Round head bolts.

They even make custom solutions for full suspension bikes with slip frame bags. They even have a more premium league of frame bags to choose from. The Fiber Flight series are custom framebags made with the toughest, lightest fabrics available. Built to maximize durability while minimizing weight.

Revelate Designs

Revelate designs are based out in Alaska, they have been around the ‘bikepacking’ block for awhile now. And have well established themselves in making quality custom bike frame bags. They quiet a few option to chose from when it comes to frame bags.

If you want to shop online for Revelate Designs Frame Bags you can get them at REI.

Blackburn

If you are looking for affordable frame bags that are still some rather good quality then you should check out Blackburn. I personally have the Outpost frame bag and it is great. Allows for adjustability to fit almost as a full frame bag or half a frame bag. Theses bags are universal and not custom made. So you will not get a exact fit like some other custom frame bag options.

Restrap

Restrap make some really nice looking frame bags. They are probably my most favourite bike bag brand. They offer three different sized frame bags and a custom option.

Handmade in Yorkshire, the Cary Anything frame bags allow you to easily increase the load you carry for comfortable bike packing and touring. With no mounts or screws, the bags come in three different sizes to suit any frame. With fully waterproof zips, a 1000D cordura outer and VX21 fabrics, all our frame bags will keep your things safe and dry.

Apidura

Apidura is another popular bikepacking bag brand with bikepackers. I never really considered there bags for a while before they released the Expedition series which is waterproof. But now that they offer the Waterproof Expedition bags they are amazing!

The bags are not custom made, but come in a variety of different sizes.

I have been looking into the Expedition Full Frame Pack as a uprgrade with my current setup. Full frame and waterproof starting at £118.33 or $152.88

WOHO XTouring

WOHO is a Taiwan brand of bikepacking bags, making some really nice gear. They select the best material for the product and environment. Spending time on sourcing and using them to continuously improve the products. They develop their products by real experience while cycling and camping. The bags have been tested in extreme environments like Tibet, Silk road, Mongolia.

Bike Bag Dude

If you are in Australia and looking for quality custom made frame bags and bikepacking bags then Bike Bag Dude has you covered. You get two options with the smaller lightweight half frame bag and a full custom fit to your bike frame bag. As a value for money option it makes sense to pay the extra $30 AUD for all the space you can get out of a full frame bag.

TreadLite Bike Bags

TreadLite Bike Bags is owned and owned and operated by Scott and Mary Hawn in Adelaide South Australia. With Scott’s love of all things bike and Mary’s love of creating new things coupled with her master sewing skills, making and selling high quality, handmade bike bags was a natural fit. They make some great bike bags and their frame bags are a wonderful choice.

Porcelain Rocket

Oveja Negra

Since they started in 2012 and have been manufacturing top shelf bikepacking gear in the Upper Arkansas Valley of Colorado. Born in a 300 square foot hole in the wall shop in the old mining town of Leadville. Oveja Negra gear is designed, tested, and sewn by cyclists that work hard to insure your adventures go off without a hitch. 

Alpkit

Alpkit make smaller bikepacking frame bags and have quiet a few variety half frame bag options. The Stingray Custom Frame Bag is a full frame bag option, that can be custom fitted to your bike and is the favourite with adventure bikepacking. Costing $75.00

Bedrock Bags

Bedrock Bags have a full custom fit frame bag available. Utilizing their PicturePerfect photo template process. Making it easier for you, to get the same tailored Bedrock fit! Click here for PicturePerfect instructions. These bags start at $170.00
