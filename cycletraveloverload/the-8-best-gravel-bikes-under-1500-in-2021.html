The 8 Best Gravel Bikes Under $1500 in 2021

Read Time:6 Minute, 31 Second

So they seem to be some of the most popular articles on the site. In this articles we will take a look at the 8 best gravel bikes ready for you to explore and crush gravel moving into 2021.

I just updated the best gravel bikes under $1,000 including more 2021 updated gravel bikes. All the bike bikes in this article that you are reading will be between $1,000 to $1,500. So if you want to find even more bang for your buck it might be worth heading over to the sub $1000 gravel bike article here.

Why Pay Up To $1,500 For A Gravel Bike?

But if you are willing to spend that little bit more, you can expect to generally get slightly better quality components, with some bikes featuring GRX 400 or Apex setups. Compared to the more modest groupsets, like Claris or Sora that is more common on bikes around $1,000.

While also being slightly lighter-weight overall. Especially the bikes with aluminium frames and carbon fork builds. As carbon fork is generally out of the budget range for sub $1000 gravel bikes. You can expect those bikes to be around that 10 kg mark. Most of the bikes feature mechanical disc brakes with some hydraulic bikes in the list.

Most of them are good for adventure cycling, with rather upright stack/reach ratios over that 1.5 mark (with some exceptions.)

2021 Specialized Diverge Base E5

The first bike in this article doesn’t have the best components but because it is a “high-end” brand it had to make the list. This Specialized Diverge Base E5 gravel bike features a Shimano Claris groupset. With a aluminium frame and carbon fork build, mechanical disc brakes and 700 x 35mm tires. The bike has a predictable mid range trail length and an all-rounder mid length chainstay. With a stack/reach ratio at that 1.51 sweet spot

This results in Specialized’s most progressive geometry they have created for a drop bar bike, giving riders a capable and stable bike in the dirt, that still feels responsive and nimble on the road. The bike also has a reasonable tire clearance for a gravel bike with 47mm of tire clearance for a 700c wheel and 2.1” with a 650b wheel. Priced from $1,200

2021 Vitus Substance V 2

Marketed as an adventure road bike this Vitus Substance is capable to take you on some off the beaten path adventures. With a 2 x 9 Sora, FSA groupset (again not the highest end components here.) Alloy frame and carbon fork build, weighing 23.7 lbs / 10.7 kg, mechanical disc brakes and rolling on 650b x 47 wheels and tires. However these rims are compatible with 700c.

Slightly more aero stack/reach ratio at 1.49, the bike has a higher end and stable trail length and a chill, mid range chainstay length. Priced at £899.99

2021 Masi Giramondo 700c

The Giramondo from Masi has to be one of my personal favourite gravel bikes that you can pick up for a truly bargain price for what you get. It speaks value for money! The fact that you get upper mid-range components with the 3 x 10 Deore, GRX 800 that also has a huge gear range! That’s 18.78″ to 112.13″ gear inches!! A full steel build, quality Tubus front and rear racks, with 700 x 45c tires.

This bike is adventure ready, and is best if you plan to go on epic bikepacking adventures or tours around the world! With bar-end shifters it is a mash between a traditional touring bike and gravel bike!

The Giramondo has a super upright 1.55 stack/reach ratio, predictable mi range trail, a chill to stable rear with a longer length chainstay than most other gravel bikes. Making enough clearance and balance to run rear panniers if you wish! All that for a tasty $1,299

2021 Norco Search XR A2

If you want a gravel bike that is a bit more quick and snappy, something like the Norco Search might be worth looking at. A do-it-all bike that craves adventure and redefines gravel.

It does feature a much lower 1.45 stack/reach ratio, putting you in a more racer position. Comes with a 2 x 10 GRX 400, Tiagra, mid-range components and mechanical disc brakes. With 700 c x 38 mm tires.

It has a quick feeling, shorter chainstay than most gravel bikes and a mid/high length trail making it predictable and stable in the front. Priced at $1,499, this bike is also available in the Norco Search XR Flat Bar option with a 1 x 11 Deore groupset and hydraulic disc brake setup, for $1,149

2021 Marin DSX 2

Another flat bar gravel bike option is Marin new DSX 2 alloy frame and carbon fork, upper mid-range components gravel bike. With the 1 x 12 Deore groupset and hydraulic disc brakes, it is the ultimate gravel commuter! With 700C x 45mm tires. Shimano Deore 12-Speed, 10-51T rear cassette and a Forged Aluminum, 42T Steel Narrow Wide Chainring, priced at $1,099

2021 Breezer Radar X

I did mention the Breezer Radar Expert in the under $1,000 gravel bike article (however the new 2021 model is over that price and is a wonderful gravel bike.) But we are going to take a look at the new all steel Radar X mid-range component gravel bike. Featuring a 1 x 11 Apex, FSA groupset, hydraulic disc brakes, 700 x 52c (29 x 2.25) wheels/tires. With a rather limited FSA Comet Modular, 38T crank and SRAM PG-1130, 11-42T cassette and lower then ideal 1.43 stack/reach ratio. Not the best option for long gravel adventures in the saddle. Still would make a great day ride gravel grinder!

With an extremely high length trail making it almost sluggish but definitely stable feeling in the front and a chill feeling mid range chainstay length. Priced at $1,499. If you want a more adventure capable and comfortable bike, the Breezer Radar Expert is your best bet!

2021 Fuji Jari 2.1

The all new Jari 2.1 by Fuji is an alloy frame and carbon fork build with mid-range components and mechanical disc brakes. With a 2 x 10 Tiagra, FSA groupset, 700c x 37 mm tires, FSA Vero Pro, 46/30T crank and Shimano HG500, 11-34T cassette.

Amble mounting options on fork, top tube and bottle cage mounts inside frame. Still featuring that interesting padding under the top tube for lifting the bike up. This bike is adventure ready with a generous and upright 1.57 stack/reach ratio, stable and high-end length trail and mid-range chainstay length making it an all-rounder and rather chill in the rear. Priced at $1,399

2021 Liv Devote 2

Looking for the ultimate women’s gravel bike under $1,500? The Liv Devote 2 is a favourite. If you want some other women’s gravel bike options, check out this article. But this is the most budget friendly option.

The Devote 2 features an alloy frame and carbon combination with modest components, that include the 2 x 9 Sora, FSA groupset, mechanical disc brakes and 700c x 38 mm tires.

A rather upright 1.58 stack/reach ratio, stable to almost sluggish front end with a high-end length trail and short to mid-range chanistay, putting it in the quick and all-rounder category. Priced at $1,200
