17 Lightest Gravel Bikes You Can Buy!

Read Time:8 Minute, 39 Second

If you want a bike that goes further than a road bike, allowing you to explore places that you otherwise couldn’t on a road bike. But want to keep the speed and weight down then look no further.

Below you can find some of the lightest gravel bikes on the market. How much do gravel bikes weigh in the first place? In general gravel bike can range from anywhere from 11 kg up to 13 kg but even still this is around 2kg heavier then most road bikes.

If you are looking to get a gravel bike you maybe asking yourself, does weight matter on a gravel bike? It really depends on how you intend to you use your bike. But as gravel grinding blends well with bikepacking, which is riding with a tent and all your gear to survive nights on end in the wilderness, weight doesn’t really matter too much here. But if you are someone who is looking to take KOMS and compete against your roadie mates. You might want to consider gravel bikes that are designed to be ridden fast! And these bikes in this list will help being a lightest options on the market.

What is the lightest gravel bike?

1. Specialized Crux

Self proclaimed as the world’s lightest gravel bike. The Specialized S-Works Crux is said to weigh 6.9kg (16.21 lb), which is their top of the line option. While the Crux Comp weighs in at 8.5 kg (18.73 lb), which is their base line model in the 2022 lineup.

Offering some of the best tire clearance to weight ratios on the market with the option to run up to 47c tires while also offering a performance gravel geometry. With a 1.46 stack/reach ratio for a 56cm frame. The performance focused geometry “creates the feeling of being ‘in’ the bike for incredible confidence while still feeling quick and nimble at the bars.”

To get lightweight gravel bike status the Crux had to drop the rack, fender and bag mounts allowing riders to engage in a straightforward riding experience. Making it not the most ideal bike for bikepacking in this regard, you can still mount bags though. Also the bike provides a 10mm longer reach to give riders a more ideal weight distribution and the shorter stem offers more controlled steering.

2. Lauf Anywhere Ultimate

The Lauf Anywhere lightest bike build comes in at 7.7 kg (17.0 lbs) for a medium sized frame. This bike is at home as a mixed-terrain designed to absorb those bumps when riding the pavement with a dash of gravel. The bikes is as much at home loaded with bags and ready for a trip into the wild, going anywhere!

With a tire clearance for up to 45mm 700c tires. Oh and in case you needed one the bike comes with a bottle opener!

3. Open U.P.P.E.R

Offering all the standard performance gravel bike features. Such as dual wheel size compatibility and a dropped chainstay. These bikes find that balance between road bike speed plus go-anywhere capabilities. Making them fast but also able to go on rad adventures. The idea behind the U.P.P.E.R bikes is the option to run multiple tire setups.

Road tires to make it a very fast road bike, turn it into a go-anywhere bike with gravel tires 32-40mm or run 650b mountain bike tires allowing it to be pretty much unstoppable. It does feature a road riding position for that extra speed even if you run MTB tires. Meaning you can get to the trails faster. Weighing just under 8 kg!

4. Salsa Warroad

The Salsa Warroad top line model weighs 8.0 kg (17.7 lb). I was amazed by this bike when it was realised back in 2020, simply by its colours and design. It is essentially an endurance all-road bike. That is suited for 80% road and 20% whatever else! Compatible with 700c or 650b, the endurance road geometry positions you for long-haul comfort, and extra frame and fork mounts help keep you supplied for all-day ride.

5. Scott Addict

The Scott Addict Gravel Tuned bike weighs in at 8.1kg (17.86 lb). A super-light build that can handle rough terrain and fits chunky tires while also having enough mounts to carry liquids and go bikepacking, allowing you to explore which is what all bikes should be about!

6. Trek Checkpoint

The Trek Checkpoint SLR 9 eTap weighs 8.10 kg (17.86 lb). Looking for a true gravel grinding power house? This is Trek’s best carbon gravel bike, and it’s built for crushing the world’s greatest gravel races. The lightweight 700 Series OCLV Carbon frame has gravel-smoothing IsoSpeed and a nimble-yet-stable progressive geometry. 

7. Giant Revolt Advanced Pro

As Giants first carbon gravel bike which made an appearance three years ago. Offering a comfortable ride, progressive frame geometry and low weight. The Advanced Pro 0 weighs in at around 8.11 kg (17.88 lb).

The Giant Revolt as seen some geometry improvements for the 2022 model. Seeing the front wheel being pushed further out adding stability also a lower bottom bracket helps here to. It looks like they are trying to appeal to a broader audience of riders who maybe need that extra stability to go on bike adventures. Thats way you get the option to extend the chainstay 10mm. Also rumour has it that you can fit up to 53mm 700c tires.

8. Liv Devote Advanced Pro

One of the lightest bikepacking bikes. It also featured in our list of the best women’s gravel bikes you can buy. The Liv Devote comes in at around 8.2 kg (18.08 lb)

Suited for bikepacking to gravel grinding to adventure rides. A clean and contoured finish with internal cable routing. The bike comes with clearance for up to 45c tires. A lightweight build, with some models coming equipped with vibration absorbing D-Fuse seatpost, or dropper seatpost. Dampening all that off-road terrain you will be exploring.

9. Cervélo Áspero 5

The lightest weight I could find on the wen for this bike was around 8.2 kg (18.08 lb) however I couldn’t find the weight for the Áspero 5 Red XPLR eTap AXS Disc bike which is a sub $10,000 bike, so you would expect some lightweight for that price tag.

Built to race not roam. A new breed of gravel rider is emerging and this bike is placed to serve that rider. One who is looking to race the trails and go all out pushing their personal limits. Build to preform like a road bike but off-road.

10. Salsa Warbird

Made for gravel racing, fire-road riding, and road riding the Salsa Warbird geometry focuses on stability and comfort. Offering that extra comfort on long rides. It features many mounts to carry gear for bikepacking adventures. This is a great bike for those looking to ride fast, bikepacking adventures on road or gravel. The Warbird is an agile, light, nimble, very refined, and entirely capable gravel bike. Perfect if you want to ride fast! The Carbon AXS Wide model weighs in at 8.3 kg (18.3 lb).

11. Cannondale Topstone

The Cannondale Topstone Carbon 5 weighs approximately 8.46 kg (18.65 lb). The bike features rear Kingpin suspension on fast rolling 700c wheels (up to 700c x 37 or 650b x 48 tire clearance.) Offering on bike comfort, control and speed from tarmac to outback!

12. Ridley Kanzo Fast

Featured as one of the fastest gravel bikes in our list of the fastest gravel bikes & gravel race bikes. The Kanzo Fast weighs about 8.5 kg (approx 18.74 lb)

Combining a gravel-specific geometry with aerodynamic tubing and wide tire clearance. You get the option to run up to 42 mm tires. With a stack/reach ratio of 1.52 for medium frame. Meaning a slightly more upright riding position in comparison to the Crux.

13. Juliana Quincy

The Juliana Quincy CC GRX build weighs 8.51 kg (18.76 lb). Another women’s gravel bike that’s lightweight, strong, and comfortable. Taking on the knowledge gained from years of mountain bike innovation, resulting in a stiff yet great off-road feeling bike.It includes size specific fork offsets, 3 water bottle cage mounts and you get room for a 45mm 700c or a 2.1″ 650b tire.

14. Ibis Hakka MX

The Hakka is a versatile bike, great for road rides, cross and of course gravel, and even some backcountry exploration. With the sub­tle sculpt­ed chain­stays it gives the frame clear­ance for up to 700 x 40c or 27.5 x 2.1” tires. Weighing in at 8.53 kg (18.8 lb)

15. Santa Cruz Stigmata

Dedicated to making the best mountain bikes, Santa Cruz have taken what they have learnt from mtb’s and implemented that in their new gravel bike. The Stigmata was released in 2007 and at the time it was categorised as a cyclocross bike. This is before gravel bikes were a thing. it is a great do-it all drop bar bike machine. That is also able to ride fast if you need. Made from a carbon fibre construction, weighing around 8.57 kg (18.89 lb) and clearance for 700×45 mm or 650×53 mm tires.

16. 3T Exploro

Looking for the most ultimate aero-adventure gravel bike that is super lightweight? The 3T Exploro comes with the largest tore clearance of all bikes in this list. With room for up to 27.5 x 2.1″ MTB tires! Oh, it weighs 8.7 kg (19.2 lb).

This bike has changed gravel racing and riding forever. The bike combines road speed with mountain toughness. Winning Dirty Kanza, Jeroboam, RAT1000 to name a few events. Having the capability to ride fast on any surface, paved, gravel or singletrack!

17. Canyon Grizl

We included the Canyon Grizl cause it is also around 8.7 kg. But for a dedicated bikepacking gravel bike thats a really good weight! With clearance for up to 50mm rubber. The bike to take you on real adventures on the rough stuff.
