Jones H Bar Alternatives

Read Time:7 Minute, 48 Second

Are you looking for some handlebars that are similar to Jone H Bar? But maybe looking for something a little less expensive and just want to see what other options you can get? This article we are going to dive into the different options for your adventure bicycle.

But first for those of you that don’t know, what are Jone H Bars? On first impression they may seem rather unconventional but they are said to ensure more power, comfort and control. Giving you a wide range of better hand and body positions. You also get 45 degrees of sweep which puts your wrists in a natural and relaxed position for lasting comfort. They come in three different styles; Loop, Cut, Bend and the Loop bars are priced from $119 which is their most popular type.

There are so many other handlebar options that are similar to the Jones H Bars. List above are just some of the potential options to choose from. But lets go into a bit more detail and have a look at each of these handlebars.

If you want to read more about other bikepacking and touring handlebar options. I wrote another article with many other bars, which I recommend you to read after this one.

Velo Orange Crazy Bar

These bars are intended to be used for touring on paved and unpaved roads. But they are also suited to gravel grinding as well. The widest point of these bars are 67cm. This creates rather good control on rough surfaces and around town. The Bullhorn section of the bars provide a streamlined position for smoother roads and headwinds. Theses Crazy bars have different bar area sizes. The main bar is 22.2mm so MTB grips, levers, and shifters fit. The “horns” are 23.8mm, so road levers – including inverse levers – fit, as do bar-end shifters. Clamp area is 25.4mm. Made from 6061 aluminum and heat treated, these bars weigh around 450 grams and you also get a 45 degree sweep. Priced from $75.00

On-One Mickey Handlebar

The availability of some of these bars will be much harder to find. These are available in the UK from Planet X and for a rather cheap price. It has much less sweep then the Jones bar at 9 degrees. But it is a supremely comfortable low rise, MTB style flat bar that has been transformed into the perfect Touring, Bike Packing and Adventure riding handlebar. If you are someone who prefers a more flat MTB bar setup compared to a drop bar then the On-One Mickey bars might be a great choice for you. It has easy ergonomics, plenty of width for the broadest shoulders and front facing luggage bar it’s perfect for any bikepacking or cycle touring adventure. Has a rise of 14 mm, the bar weighs in at 476 grams and you get a handlebar that is 760mm wide. Priced from only £24.99

Surly Open Bar

A upright handlebar design, that is equally suited to cruising, commuting and even some singletrack. Designed with a little bit of flex to take some of the harsh out of your ride. The angle makes it comfy for your wrists. The width enables loads of control. It’s got plenty of room for grips, shifters and brake levers. It has a knurled clamp area and is made from durable 4130 Chromoly steel. Available in low rise and no rise versions. The width of these bars are 666mm and a clamp area of 25.4mm. The rise is 40mm or you can opt for the no rise option as mentioned. Sweep angle is 53 degrees and the handlebar weighs 475 grams. Priced from $50

Surly Moloko Handlebar

These Surly Moloko bars offer quiet a similar handling position as the Jones H Bars. You even get the option to have a custom fitted Surly Bag that mounts inside the bar. This handlebar offers a multitude of hand positions to keep you comfortable and in control over the course of a long day riding on varied terrain. You also get a variety of mounting potential all over this handlebar. Dangle a handlebar bag from it, strap some lights to it, throw a GPS on it (if that’s your thing), and ride all day. It comes in at a standard width of 735mm but if you want a shorter bar you can cut it down to 685mm with given cut marks to make the alteration. Made from a Chromoly steel, you get 34 degrees of sweep angle. Weighing in at 709 grams and has a clamp diameter of 31.8mm. Starting from $95.00 from Jenson USA and the handlebar bag costs $65.00

Origin8 Space Off Road II

Unfortunately could not find much information about these handlebars, as the sales pages really aren’t that descriptive. But some bike travellers recommend using these bars as a replacement for the Jones H Bars. Offering a 50mm forward sweep and a 30 to 40 degrees back sweep (had different numbers from Amazon and offical website). Weighing in at 340 grams. You get the option for 31.8mm or 25.4mm mounting options. Priced on Amazon from $55.00. But don’t take my word for it. Read the reviews here.

Titec J-Bar

These handlebars are harder to find for sale online, I had some luck finding a couple listings here and there on eBay but somewhere like Amazon they are out of stock. So supply for this handlebar is rather limited. But still worth mentioning in this article as they are another alternative option to the Jones H Bars. Made form a 6061-T6 Drawn & butted tapered welded tubing. The bars width is 660mm (26 inches). You get a 45° sweep on outside grip extensions. And the handlebars weigh in at 440 grams. They sell MSRp for $90, the one I found new on eBay was $70.

On-One Mary Bars

These bars are available from PlantX, we have no affiliation with them, but are sharing these with you as they are so budget friendly. Available for those of you who are located in the UK, these handlebars cost only £24.99 and they have heaps in stock. Mary is On-One’s classic alt-bar. They like to call it their extreme sweep riser bar. It’s a bit odd, but makes a lot of sense once you give it a go. You get a 645mm width, A 37.5mm rise, with 21 degrees up sweep, 85mm back sweep on the 31.8mm bar. They two different clamp options come in different weights. 25.4mm bar clamp is 306 grams and the 31.8mm bar clamp is 289 grams.

Soma Clarence-AL bar

These Soma Fabrications handlebars were actually designed with a similar sweep to the Jones H Bar. The Clarence makes it easy to throw the bike around in technical situations. Plus the grip position feels really natural. The 31.8mm version is slightly wider than the 25.4mm. Made from a 6061-T6 aluminum. You get a 34° back sweep, 19° upsweep, 37mm rise, 670mm width and a clamp diameter of 31.8mm. Priced from $50.99 on Amazon.

Bontrager Crivitz

These Bontrager Handlebars are the same as what the Trek 1120 has stock. A great alternative to the Jones H-Bars. Bontrager’s Crivitz bars have a wide stance and swept-back grips making them great for commuters and city riding. It’s made from light and tough 7075 aluminum with a 25-degree backsweep for comfort and efficient steering. They have a 25 degrees bend and weigh in at 270 grams. Priced around $90.00

FSA – Metropolis Handlebar

These Full Speed Ahead Handlebars are available for a reasonable price at Jenson USA for $70.99. It’s an ergonomic, city-bike-focused design with a wing shape, and can be flipped either direction for a high flyer or low rider type of ride. Designed for urban and city style of riding they still would be an okay option for an alternative to the H-Bar. They come in a 620mm width, 32mm rise, a 46.5 degrees backsweep, 21.6 degrees upsweep and weighing in at 300 grams.

Origin8 Transit Ergo Handlebar

If you are after some rather cheap and budget friendly handlebars these might be the go. Categorised as a all around urban touring bar they are made from a AL6061 alloy construction. With a clamp size of 31.8mm and 630mm width. With rather good reviews on Amazon for a inexpensive bar at $32.28.

Satori Noirette Plus

If you are after another super budget option again like the Transit Ergo, this handlebar provides just that. With a light weight and elegant design for relaxed lifestyle riding. Flat wing shape with smooth transition from 31.8mm bar clamp for good looks & better grip. 110mm middle section for clamp-on accessories. These bars come in either a 630mm or 660mm width, 34 mm rise, 41 degrees sweep and weigh in at 315 grams (for the 660mm width bar). Costing only $
