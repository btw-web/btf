Surly Straggler Vs Midnight Special

Read Time:4 Minute, 43 Second

The Surly gravel bike bike battle! Well which bike is best? We are going to compare these two iconic gravel bikes side by side and see what the difference is between each. When it comes to price the Straggler is $350 cheaper at $1650 when the Midnight Special is $2,000. But what really is the difference and which one is best for you and your riding goals?

The Straggler is classified by Surly as a “mountain biker’s road bike.” It is a
disc brake pavement and steel gravel bike while the Midnight Special on the other hand is considered as all day road plus bike that tackles pavement & gravel. Essentially in a sentence the Straggler is a more at home commuter gravel and road bike while the Midnight Special is more of a bike that can be taken out for “all-day” adventures. But that is not to say you cannot take the straggler out bikepacking too.

Surly Straggler

The Surly Straggler comes with a 1×11 SRAM Apex which is a slightly lower end drivetrain option to the Midnight special. When looking at similarity both bikes are made from a 4130 CroMoly Steel frame and fork. Accomodation for 650b and 700c wheels, both have aluminium wheels and mechanical disc brakes. The straggler has the Hayes CX Comp, 160 rotors brakes and Alex Adventurer 2, tubeless ready rims with Surly Knard 700cx41, 33TPI tires (depending if you get the 650b or 700c, these with vary. The Straggler can be picked up in 650b with frame sizes from 54 cm and smaller.) And clearance for 650b x 41mm or 700c x 41mm tires with fenders!

With the Straggler it comes with forward-exiting horizontal dropouts for singlespeed compatibility and wheelbase adjustability. Which is something the Midnight Special does not have.

Also when looking at the Stragglers components you get the SRAM Apex 42T crankset and SRAM 11-42t 11spd cassette. The crank on the Straggler has 2 more teeth and is bigger then the Midnight Special.

That is the basics of the build. The biggest difference in these bikes is the geometry. But more on that soon. First lets take a quick look at the Midnight Special. Again the Straggler is priced at $1650 at Tree Fort Bikes

Surly Midnight Special

So the Midnight Special comes with a SRAM Rival 1 drivetrain. This is a slightly better performing spec then the Apex. This results in a smaller crank with the SRAM Rival 1 40T and SunRace 11-42t cassette. And the brakes are the TRP Spyre with 160mm rotors.

It comes with clearance for 650b x 60mm or 700c x 42mm tires without fenders (650b x 50mm or 700c x 38mm with fenders) That is one of the better benefits of this bike, housing much wider tires for those really fun off-road endeavours.

The wheels that come with this bike are the same Alex Adventurer 2 rims but with WTB Horizon, 650b x 47 tires. Higher volume tires allow better fine-tuning of tire pressure on varying surfaces and create a wider contact patch for increased traction. This is what attracts me more to the Midnight Special (that tire clearance!) You also get the 12mm front and rear thru-axles which is quick release on the Straggler. Again the Surly Midnight Special is priced at $2,000 at Tree Fort Bikes

Geometry – Straggler Vs Midnight Special

Geometry can play a big part in the difference of how I bike rides and feels. It is definitely something to consider when buying a bike. On first impressions I was expecting the Midnight Special to be more of a road bike geometry. And that provided to be true with the slightly shorter chainstay and wheelbase for a more responsive and snappy control. But all the measurements below are in mm and are for both 56 cm sized bikes.

Also the standover height for the Midnight Special is 17.6 mm taller then the Straggler. So as you can see by default the Straggler is a lower standing bike. So if leg over bike is a issue for you when getting on and off and you cannot seperate the bikes any other way then this is a benefit I guess.

If you are a Single Speed junkie then the Straggler will appeal to you with those drop outs. And is probably best suited to those looking to get into gravel biking but also want a reliable commuter.

Overall I would recommend the Midnight Special, it is essentially a road bike with fat tires. If you are after a road bike with more versatility and adaptability in various types of terrain then this bike has your back. When I think about it the Straggler kind of missing the point it is not really any kind of bike. You should feel comfortable taking it anywhere. It isn’t particularly fast, but isn’t slow either. It’s a cross check with disc brakes, designed to be versatile and utilitarian, but not really special in any way.

If you plan to cart gear and use your bike for transporting cargo in the form of panniers and a heavier load. You might be better off with the Straggler for its longer wheel base to avoid heel striking when compared to a shorter wheelbase on the Midnight Special.

It seems if you are coming from a roadie background and are looking for a bike with larger tire clearance the Midnight Special is your go to.
