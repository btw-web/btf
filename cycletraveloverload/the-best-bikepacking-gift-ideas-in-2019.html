Click Stand

So what is a Click Stand you may ask? It is a simple little gadget that would be perfect for any bike traveller. As bikepacking and touring your bike is fully loaded with heavy gear and that can make it particularly difficult to manoeuvre and lean against objects. There are many ways around this. Like a more traditional bike stand. but the Click Stand is different!

They are a foldable portable kickstand for your bicycle. So once folded you can pack it away and pull it out when ever you need. There is no need to worry about if the Click Stand will be able to support your bike or not. They come in six different models from a carbon fibre mini stand or the max Click Stand, which is best for heavier, fully loaded touring bikes.

The stands are able to come in various amounts of folding segments which allow for the stand to fold smaller which is ideal for bikepacking! And they also come in a huge variety of colors to suit your style.

Wahoo Elemnt Roam GPS

This little gadget is what I plan to get very soon. It is a great gift for a bikepacker as navigation is a very important aspect. This Wahoo Roam GPS has all you need for all your bikepacking adventures.

It has a simple set up and on-device navigation with color display. The device features a smart “back-on-track” setting which re-routes your direction to get you to where you need to go, if you ever veer off track.

The crystal-clear 2.7″ Gorilla Glass color display provides a stunning viewing experience. Allowing you to see your route and data with ease. It also has a ambient light sensor, so depending on your environment lighting the devices backlight will adjust to match the conditions.

The battery life on this GPS is pretty good with 17 hours of battery before you need to recharge. But it is another thing that you will have to charge. When on multiple day adventures this is something to consider, but I guess it is better then getting lost.

It weighs no more then 3.3 oz (93.55 grams) and is waterproof for up to 5 ft. Priced at $380.00 at REI. But you should read the reviews to see how good this bike computer really is!

Voile Straps

These are probably the most handy thing you could buy for any bikepacking enthusiast and they would be greatly appreciated. Voile Straps allow you mount just about anything to your bike with ease. Great for if you want to over load your bikepacking rig with extra gear without the need to get other mounts. Or like you see above the are a security feature that keeps your water bottle fixed well in place on the water bottle mount.

So lets go back! Violet Straps were invented almost three decades ago as a unique way to bind skis together. But since then they have been used to secure, bundle, and repair almost anything on the go. And have become very popular in this bikepacking space.

The versatility of these straps never fail to amaze, with all the creative ways the people have come up with using them. They come in four different styles and material. And you can also order some with your own custom branding on them. Maybe customize with that special someones name on them, that would cool! They are a really cheap way to strap more stuff to your bike and are a gift that would come in very good use. Starting at $5.95

Gore R7 Shakedry Trail

When it rains you you still want to get out and go bikepacking there is no need for it to stop you. With a Gore-Tex jacket it will keep you waterproof. It is designed to be a running jacket but it is more then capable at being that jacket that keeps you dry when it pours.

It features everything you need in a rain jacket for your bikepacking adventures. Abrasion resistant, durably waterproof, highly breathable & lightweight, it is a go-to for any kind of adventure in the outdoors for that matter. Not to mention its packability for when it is not in use. Weighing in at 175 grams and priced at $299.99

Dissent 133 Waterproof Gloves

Lets face it! When travelling by bike in those colder months of the year, your hands can get really cold. A solution to this is the Dissent 133 waterproof gloves.

So how did they get the name “133”? Well based of the UK, there are, on average, 133 days of rainfall per year. They were designed to allow you to ride those wet days and this is a tool that will help you get it done. The gloves are designed around a three-part Layered Glove System. Priced at $75.06 USD

Ryders Anti Fog Riding Glasses

Ryders offer some stylish and practical Anti Fog Riding Glasses options that are ideal for bikepacking. They have tapped into a common problem that occurs in sports that you may use eyewear. Things are going to get foggy, well unless you use these glasses.

The back of their lenses have a military-grade, hydrophilic layer. This layer absorbs and disperses water vapour/humidity throughout the material, eliminating the opportunity for it to condense on the surface as fog.

They have even provided a solution for the front side of the lenses to. As so often when you are riding in wet conditions when water gets on the lenses it become hard to see. Ryders treat the front side of the lens independently with a hydrophobic coating. It defends against saturation by shedding water to provide a clear view, free from large drops and water marks. Priced at $116.99 they are a specific pair of glasses, best suited for bikepacking.

2020 Salsa Cutthroat with Shimano GRX 810 Di2

No gift list is complete with some newly released bikepacking bikes for 2020! This new Salsa Cutthroat is amazing! The ultimate bike that has been designed for self-supported, ultra-endurance, mixed-surface racing. Built from the ground up, this rig features some new updates. With a new fork, updated frame geometry and a host of other refinements to this high-end. The top end model also goes to Di2. The bike pictured above the ultimate option but it also comes in other models too. Cutthroat range consists of four models, from the top specced GRX 810 Di2 version at $5,799 USD, down to the Apex 1 build for $2,699 USD.

Speaking about the Di2 it features Shimano’s electronic shifting groupset which provides effortless and flawless gear changing every time. The Cutthroats carbon frame and fork is designed to provide a very smooth ride, dampening most road chatter. The unique ride characteristics of this bike make it part gravel and part mountain bike. Ready for any kind of bikepacking adventure!

Kona Sutra LTD 2020

The other bikepacking bike gift option new for 2020 is the Kona Sutra LTD. A steel frame bike that is

“a drop-bar bike designed by mountain bikers, for mountain bikers.” As simple as that statement may sound, the result is one of the most versatile, category-shattering rigs in the mainstream bike market.” – Logan Watts, Bikepacking.com

The steel bike is great at bump absorption and ride quality. It has SRAM Rival 1 hydraulic disc brakes, a 1x drivetrain and beefy yet fast-rolling 700x50c tires. The wider bars with a 16-degree flare provide great control on that rough stuff. Price at $2,417.50 USD
