Surly Krampus vs Karate Monkey

Read Time:5 Minute, 49 Second

The battle of the Surly trail bikes! Krampus or Karate Monkey? On the surface they look identical! What’s the difference?

We are going to compare these two iconic trail bikes side by side and see what the difference is between each. When it comes to price they are the same both coming in at $2,200! But what really is the difference and which one is best for you and your riding goals?

The Krampus is classified by Surly as their “dedicated 29+ trail bike” while the Karate Monkey is consider more versatile at home as both a 29er and 27.5+ trail bike. But there are more difference than just that. Let take a closer look. Both of these bikes are available in a front suspension or rigid fork option.

Surly Krampus

Both the Krampus and Karate Monkey comes with 1 x 12 NX Eagle, high end components. Both made from steel and suspensions options have the RockShox 35 Gold RL fork. However the Krampus fork is suited for 29″ – and has 120mm travel, 51mm offset and 15X110MM AXLE. The seatpost on the Krampus is the TransX JD-YSP15 30.9 with 120mm travel. The tires that come with this bike are the Surly Dirt Wizard 29 x 3”. When it comes the gearing both are the same with the SRAM NX Eagle 30t crank and SRAM EAGLE 11-50t cassette. But does the different wheel and tire sizes make a difference when it comes to gear inches?

With the Krampus and its 29 x 3″ tires you get a 18.29″ climbing gear and a 83.23″ highest gear. Both Krampus and Karate Monkey offer the modern trail standards: dropper post compatibility, 44mm headtube, Gnot-Boost rear spacing, thru-axles, suspension corrected fork. The Krampus has clearance for up to 29″ x 3″ tires. If fitting wider tires is high up on your list, the Krampus has the slight edge over the Karate Monkey allowing for an extra half inch max tire width. But is limited to only 29+ wheels. Priced at $2,200 or you can pick up the rigid fork option for $1,550 (which is SX Eagle)

Surly Karate Monkey

The Karate Monkey has pretty much all the same gearing as the Krampus and most parts are identical. The main difference is the TranzX SP15, dropper post 100mm travel on XS 120mm travel on SM/MD/LG/XL on the Karate Monkey and the Surly Dirt Wizard 27 x 3″ tires. Which gives the Karate Monkey a slightly more limited 18.48″ climbing gear not buy much, only 0.19″ and a highest gear of 84.09″ which gives you a little more on the downhills.

The only other difference is the fork on the Karate Monkey which is the RockShox 35 Gold RL, 27.5 – 140mm travel, 44mm offset, 15X110MM AXLE, disc fork.

As you can tell from comparing this helpful image above for both bikes. This Karate Monkey is slightly less capable when it comes to tire clearance. They are both perfect for single track, equally good for bikepacking and same for commuting, (not as ideal). It also has clearance for 29” x 2.5” or 27.5” x 3” tires. Priced at $2,200 or you can pick up the rigid fork option for $1,550 (Which is SX Eagle)

Not much else to seperate these two bikes. Oh, I almost forgot, geometry! One of the more important aspects of a bike and the way it handles comes down mostly to geometry.

Geometry – Krampus Vs Karate Monkey

Geometry can play a big part in the difference of how I bike rides and feels. It is definitely something to consider when buying a bike. I just assumed at first that both these bikes had the same measurements. But on closer inspection this is not the case! Taking a look at either bikes chainstay lengths, we can see that the Krampus has a 435mm chainstay which puts it in the mid range and makes it feel rather quick and near an all-rounder. Then the Karate Monkey is 423 mm which is more suited to ride quick and jumpy in the rear. The wheelbase on the Krampus is 1121 mm and Karate Monkey is 1103 mm. Even the BB Drop is 5 mm lower to the ground on the Krampus and front centre length is longer by 7 mm. All this suggests that the Krampus would be the better bike geometry wise when it comes to bikepacking. As that extra length and lower centre of gravity in theory would make the bike ride a bit more comfortably under a load of gear. Plus you get the option to run slightly wider tires on the Krampus which makes it more ideal for bikepacking. Also the Krampus is slightly more upright than the Karate Monkey.

Overall if you are after a bikepakcing bike between the two I would recommend the Krampus, which would have slightly better comfort, however I have met many bikepackers who use the Karate Monkey and do just as well. If the option to run either 29er or 27.5+ is your dream then the Karate Monkey is a hot favourite.
