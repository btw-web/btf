Read Time:9 Minute, 9 Second

I have compiled what I believe are some of the best gravel forks on the market. There is a variety here. You get choices from steel to carbon and suspension forks, specifically for gravel bikes. So if you are looking for a more supple gravel fork for your bike you have come to the right place.

Understanding Fork Specs

But first if you know not much about forks. You need to understand which fork is going to best and suited to your bike and situation. There are many different variables when it comes to forks and I am going to try my best to identify and explain them for you.

Brake Type – different forks are designed around the different type of brakes that will be mounted on them. The two most common types are Flat mount disc and post mount brakes. Flat mount brakes are when the rear caliper is affixed with two bolts running through a flattened frame surface and then threading directly into the caliper. While post mount side of the fork with a extended piece that is part of the fork. Some forks come in both option while some come in only one.

Mounts and Material – these are obvious ones but for mounts you may want to consider some if you are looking to mount cargo cages on your forks or bottle cage for bikepacking. While the material is generally of the three mentioned earlier; steel, carbon and often suspension are made from aluminium.

Rake – What is rake? It is also known as Offset, which more accurately describes what it is: the hub’s offset from the steering axis.

Axle to crown – Is a common measure to find the length of the fork. Generally measuring from the top of the crown to the centre of the axle.

Front Hub Spacing – It is the measurement at the end of each leg of the fork across.

There are a few other things to consider in a gravel fork so make sure you check everything to make sure it is suitable for installation on your current setup.

Steel Gravel Fork

When it comes to steel gravel fork it seems these are less popular. Lighter weight is optimal for gravel. But I do personally like my steel gravel and adventure bikes. And here is one recommendation of the more rare steel gravel forks that you can consider.

Surly Straggler Fork

The Straggler is Surly’s go to gravel bike. So it makes sense to consider the fork that is on this bike. A brazed Chromoly fork with room for tires up to 50mm. It has fender and rack mount eyelets at the dropouts, mid-blade threaded rack mounts, and threaded eyelets on the sides of the crown for accessories. The Straggler fork is equipped with a 51mm IS disc caliper mount and has no accommodation for rim brakes. It is available for $135.00 from Nashbar.

It offers a highly efficient 30mm of travel. The fork is designed for gravel and cylcocross frames with 700c or 27.5” wheels and a tapered steer-tube. The Lauf is available for hubs using 12mm or 15mm thru-axles. One of the awesome things about this fork is it requires Zero Maintenance. The fork has zero moving parts, lab tested with over 1 million compressions with no signs of wear and tear. Priced at $990.00 over at Jenson USA.
