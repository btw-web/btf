Ultimate List Of Best Gravel Bikes Under $2000

Read Time:17 Minute, 15 Second

So you are after a gravel bike? This budget range of $2000 or under is probably the perfect place to get a best value for money gravel bike. At this range you can get access to really good gravel bikes with high end components and great build quality.

In this article we are going to show you a big list of gravel bikes between $1000 to $2000 USD and we will break down each bike and show you which is the better option for your money.

The only criteria I considered for selecting these gravel bikes, was that they had at-least a 1.45 stack/reach ratio, so there is nothing here that is too areo and down low, like a road bike. (Ideally anything 1.5 and over is most upright and comfortable if you plan to ride long days in the saddle and do some overnight exploring.)

Also all these bikes have mid-range level components or higher. I didn’t opt to consider bikes with modest components. So the base level components you can expect in this list are bikes with; Tiagra, Alivio, Apex, X5 and up from there in quality.

And obviously the last criteria I selected for choosing these bikes was that they are the newest models, so 2021 or 2020 models are only included.

Okay so here is a quick list of all bikes mentioned and at the bottom of this article you can find an in-depth comparison table.

2021 Masi Giramondo 700c

The cheapest adventure ready gravel/touring bike between $1,000 to $2,000. Which is the best at climbing. Not exactly a gravel bike even though it is consider by others to be in this category. I would call it more a touring bike. It made its way in to list for being the cheapest option in this budget range. You can tell it is more suited to touring and adventure cycling with the longer chainstay (then most gravel bikes) giving a stable ride feel when under a load.

It is also the best bike if you are looking for an incredible climbing gear. With a 18.78″ granny gear that is up there with some mountain bikes. And it has the largest overall gear ratio in this list with 0.67 to 4.0

The 29 x 2.0″ tire clearance and 3 out of 5 star rated, 3×10 Deore, GRX 800 drivetrain also make this bike a great buy for only $1,299. it is however on the heavier side weighing 32.0 lbs / 14.5 kg.

2021 Breezer Radar X Pro

This Breezer is the best gravel bike with the largest tire clearance under $2,000. Making it one of my personal favourites. Large tire clearances is a game changer for these type of bikes. It can open up a whole new world of adventure! Oh, yeah, you get room for a whopping 29 x 2.5″ tire. The bike comes with 700x57c Goodyear Peak Ultimate tires.

The bike is a full steel build and features a relaxed geometry that is a little sluggish at the front end and more chill in the rear. With a 4 out of 5 star rated 1×11 GRX 800, GRX 600 drivetrain. That is a little limited with a 26.09″ to 100.01″ gear range. It is a little heart breaking to see such a large tire width and then have a bike with very limited climbing gear. Most terrains where you can put these tires to the test are up steep single tracks. Also it’s on the heavy side at approx 13.78 kg. But this bike is still great if you wish to grind flat gravel roads!

Unfortunately we don’t live in a perfect world where we can have huge clearance gravel bikes, that are super light, highest end components, adventure ready accessories and geometry. And all that for an affordable price. Well if a bike does exist please let me know!

The other thing that I like about this bike is all those eyelet mounts. So many options for mounting gear on the forks, to even the seat stays, which is a game changer! Also the overall aesthetics of this bike is just so damn good! That purple really pops. Honestly this would be my choice of bike, you just can’t go past that tire clearance if that is what you are looking for. And if you plan to ride relatively flat terrain and are not worried about a heavier bike.

2021 Canyon Grail 7

This gravel bike takes the cake for the lightest gravel bike under $2,000! (From what we researched. It was rather extensive.) To be honest we were unable to find all weights for the bikes here. However the 2021 Canyon Grail 7 weighs 20.7 lbs / 9.4 kg which is pretty light for an aluminium frame and carbon built gravel bike.

Along with its weight, the Grail 7 also has one of the highest end components with the 2×11 GRX 800, GRX 600 drivetrain and hydraulic Shimano GRX RX600 brakes.

It also has a decent gear range with 24.32″ to 115.53″ and an okay tire clearance at 42mm. It is a bike that will feel rather quick in the rear and stable upfront.

2020 Masi CXGR Supremo

The best gravel bike for riding fast under $2,000 goes to the Masi CXGR Supremo. It is rather limited when it comes to climbing, but for speed downhill it takes the cake. Thanks to the 120.50″ highest gear. You can expect to reach in your highest gear at 100 rpm around 38 mph or 61 km/h on this bike. Thanks to the Praxis Works Alba M30 48/32t crank and Shimano HG-700 11-34T cassette.

This bike ands the next bike are similar in downhill riding capabilities. This Masi is slightly better at speeding downhill thanks to the 700x40mm tires compared to the 700×38 on the Giant bike below.

This Masi is a steel frame and carbon fork build, with a 2×11 105, Praxis drivetrain (3 out of 5 star) and mechanical disc brakes. The bike also comes with a dropper post. It is predictable up front and more of an all-rounder in the rear.

2021 Giant Revolt Advanced 3

Also the full carbon Giant Revolt Advanced 3 comes in a very close second fastest with a 25.83″ to 119.81″ gear range. That 119.81″ thanks to the FSA Omega crank with 32/48 and the Shimano Tiagra rear cassette 11×34 it is able to get 119.81 gear inches when on the fastest setup. Which will keep you spinning downhills more so than all the other bikes here.

For example the top speed you can expect to reach on this bike in your highest gear at 100 rpm is around 38 mph or 61 km/h. So similar gravel bikes will struggle to keep up with the Revolt Advanced 3 while riding downhill or sprinting.

The bike comes with 700x38c Giant CrossCut AT 1 tires and room for up to 45mm tires. However it does have a rather underwhelming 2×10 GRX 400, FSA drivetrain. Which is rated 2 out of 5 stars, and as a bike for $2,000. It would be great to see some better components. However you are getting the full carbon build.

2021 Norco Search XR A Suspension

This Norco is the best suspension gravel bike under $2,000. What a combination! I really love a gravel bike with a suspension fork. The Norco Search XR A Suspension comes with the SR Suntour GVX Gravel fork with 50 mm of travel. Along with the suspension setup you get a 4 out of 5 star high-end 1×11 GRX 800, GRX 600 drivetrain.

This rig comes with 700×40 Vittoria Terreno Dry tires and clearance for 700c x 45mm tires on the fork and the frame only fits up to 700c x 42mm. It is rather quick feeling in the rear. The bike also features a dropper post and has an okay gear range of 26.26″ to 100.60″.

2021 Specialized Diverge Comp E5 EVO

The best flat bar gravel bike under $2,000 goes to the Diverge Comp E5 EVO. As far as flat bar gravel bikes go, this is the bees knees. There are some cheaper and more expensive options out there. But for a $1,600 price tag you get upper-mid range components with the 1×11 NX, Praxis drivetrain. Hydraulic Tektro HD-R290 brakes, in an aluminium frame and carbon fork build weighing around 10.29kg.

The bike comes with clearance for up to 700 x 47mm or 650b x 2.1” tires and a dropper post. It has a rather stable front ride feel and acts like an all-rounder up front. it also has a decent gear range of 26.41″ to 101.17″ which makes it great as an all-road commuter gravel bike.

The next few bikes made it in this order of the list for having the highest quality components for the price. Being all 4 out of 5 star rated drivetrains. Having that said, high-end components are not everything to decide on a the best bike. The rest of the bikes are honourable mentions.

2021 Vitus Substance VRS 2

Starting with this Vitus it is the cheapest bike to have the 2×11 GRX 800, GRX 600 drivetrain at $1,776. Alloy frame and carbon fork build this bike comes with 650b x 47 WTB Byway TCS Road Plus tires which is wider than a majority of gravel grinders.

it weighs in at 23.1 lbs / 10.5 kg and actually has a pretty good gear range with 23.35″ to 110.92″ and a ratio of 0.88/4.18. While having a geometry that is rather chill in the rear and predictable and stable up front.

2021 Masi Tavolo GRX

This Masi has 2×11 GRX 800, 105 (which again is considered a 4 out of 5 star rated) and for the drivetrain and components only this is what makes it another good option for value for money at $1,799. Plus it is a steel frame and carbon for combo, which has me personally very interested. And it is not much difference in weight at 23.4 lbs / 10.6 kg compared to something like the Vitus (above) which is alloy and carbon. But you get a more superior material for the frame. Especially if you have adventure riding planned.

Could not find the max tire clearance for this bike but it does come with 700x40c Kenda Flintridge tires and hydraulic disc brakes. The gear range is 24.32″ to 115.53″. While it feels predictable to handle up front and in the rear it acts like an all-rounder. Making the geometry of this bike well suited for bikepacking on less gnarly terrains.

2020 Diamondback Haanjo 5C EXP Carbon

The Diamondback Haanjo 5C EXP Carbon deserves a spot in here. Not because it has the best quality components. It has a 2×10 Deore, Tiagra (rated 3 out of 5 stars). But because it is full carbon and weighs 22.15 pounds or 10.05 kg, which is 3rd lightest in this list. And it has a very appealing tire clearance with potentially up to 2.1″ (at-least the rims can hold that wide a tire). Stock wheels are 650×47 WTB Byway. Also this bike has one of the better climbing gears with a 22.82″ to 110.92″ gear range. It does have mechanical brakes not hydraulic and is $2,000. Overall this bike stacks up well in most categories and is a good choice.

2021 Scott Contessa Speedster Gravel 15

The Scott Speedster Gravel bike has high quality components but is limited with its 700x35C Schwalbe G-One Allround tires and max 38mm tire clearance. But it is about 6th overall when it comes to climbing, With a 23.97″ to 113.88″ gear range and a 0.88/4.18 gear ratio. For a dollar under $2,000 it is right on the max of this budget. But limited if you want to fit wide tires. The Alloy and Carbon build does come out to a weight of 21.9 lbs / 9.9 kg which makes it 500 grams heavier than the Canyon Grail 7 (lightest bike in this list from the numbers we could gather.) This bike does have a decent gear range compared to other bikes, just limited in tire clearance.

2021 Rocky Mountain Solo 50

A bike designed for adventure, and to feel more snappy in the rear this Rocky Mountain Solo 50 still has higher end components and a decent gear range for a gravel bike with 26.26″ to 100.60″. An Alloy frame and carbon fork build, it is on the longer side for the trail then most other gravel bikes making it more stable and almost sluggish up front. Running 700 x 40c WTB Venture tires and hydraulic disc brakes.

2021 Masi Brunello GRX11

This Masi is limited when it comes to climbing and this is no thanks to the 1×11 drivetrain, which is high end in quality. But it only gets a 26.26″ to 100.60″ gear range, and the bike has room for up to 700c by 45 mm tires. It is definitely one of the more interesting designed bikes. With the skinning tubes and angles. An alloy frame and carbon fork build it comes with 700×40 tires and room to fit 45mm. That 1×11 GRX 800, GRX 600 means it is up there with component quality.

Merida SILEX 400

The Silex 400 by Merida is a allloy frame and carbon fork build that weighs 22.8 lbs / 10.4 kg. It does come with a 2×10 GRX 400, GRX 600 (2 out of 5 star quality components). But it has a 22.81″ to 114.87″ gear range which means one of the better bikes for climbing. it comes with 700x38C Maxxis Rambler tires and can fit up to 42mm or 650b x 50.

As you can see this table can be super helpful to see where each bike stacks up against each other. I have not included another 10 bikes which are listed. If you want some more bikes I would recommend looking further into them. But these bikes here in this article are the best you can buy for under $2,000. Hope it helped and thanks for stopping by.
