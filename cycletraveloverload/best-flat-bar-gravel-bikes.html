Best 22 Flat Bar Gravel Bikes In 2022 – The Ultimate List

Read Time:13 Minute, 26 Second

As gravel grinding is becoming more and more popular. More bike travellers are noticing how fun it is to ride off the beaten path. At least this is my own personal experience anyway. On first inspection there only seems to be the standard drop bar gravel bike option out there. But drop bars are not always the best solution. Which is why some bike brands have come out with flat bar gravel bike options.

Some would say that flat handlebars offer you better bike control, especially when we are talking about riding off-road. They offer better steering leverage which is ideal when you also have added weight of a bikepacking handlebar bag or front panniers. Flat bars a generally great for people who are just getting into cycling. If you are someone who is a beginner when it comes to gravel bikes then a flat bar bike might be a great choice.

With flat bars though you are not in the most aerodynamic position when compared to a drop bar gravel bikes. Which is still okay, because you could change the flat bars to some alternative handlebars options that are out there. These bikes make great gravel commuters!

2022 3T Exploro RaceMax BOOST Flatbar

The only flat bar e-gravel bike in the list. The all new Race Max Boost Flat Bar by 3T is a weapon. The 3T Exploro line is one of the more aero gravel bikes on the market. If you want a bike to ride fast paces around your city look no further. Combined with the electric power you are going to be unstoppable.

Just like all the other Exploro models you get approximately 54mm tire clearance allowing you to go on rad adventures into the wilderness or the office. Some say the flat bar setup feels compact and very well-centred.

2022 Poseidon Flatbar Redwood

Featured as one of the best value gravel bikes, The Poseidon Redwood also comes in a flat bar urban adventurer option. One of the better gravel bikes to offer ample tire clearance. They have take the geometry of the Poseidon X (featured below) and amped it up so you can eat up gravel with enough tire to absorb the vibrations of a rutted-rocky trail. Redwood wheels have 27.5″ thru-axle/tubeless compatible rims. Allowing a 2.8″ tire to clear this frame without issues. Allowing you to explore trails, or even take the more scenic route to work.

State Bicycle 4130 All-Road Flat Bar Gravel Bike

Categorised as the “gravel, work, commuter, do it all bike” with unique frame styling that really stands this bike apart from the rest aesthetically. Although its inspired by retro mountain bike style it includes features to keep a 2022 gravel grinder happy! Thru-axle on frame with 100/142 spacing giving you the option to run up to 53 c tire. They sell the bike with 700c and 650b wheels, so you could order both and run the 700c for the work commute and then switch over to 650b for that mountain bike look and to rip trails. Personally I would just run the 650b cause it looks so cool.

2022 Salsa Journeyman Flat Bar

Perfect for if you plan to wander the backroads, bikepack or race on gravel, the Journeyman Flat Bar is a great budget friendly gravel grinder for doing just about anything.

Specialized Diverge Expert E5 EVO

View at Pro’s Closet

The Specialized flat bar gravel bike has to be a personal favourite at the moment. You could say this bike is redefining the notion of gravel grinding.

When it comes to the bikes geometry it is more progressive then the new drop bar Diverge. With an increase in the reach, slackened head tube and lowered the bottom bracket, resulting in a rather planted feeling.

The bike is MTB inspired, with a 1 x 12 high end level XT, Praxis groupset, hydraulic disc brakes and a dropper post. Built around an alloy frame and carbon fork. Rolling on some 700x42mm tires, you get max clearance for 47mm with a 700c wheel and 2.1” with a 650b wheel. You also get a Praxis Zayante carbon 1X, 40T crank and Shimano XT, 12-speed, 10-45t cassette.

As an update for the 2022 model Specialized have significantly increased the reach, slackened the head tube and lowered the bottom bracket to make you feel like you are more in the bike. Making the ride feel more planted, perfect for sending in down technical single track to ripping flowing fire road descents.  

Commencal FCB

FCB or Fast City Bike! A stylish commuter flat bar gravel bike ready for whatever riding you have in mind! Now you can cruise the streets with speed and style. Whether on tarmac or gravel the Commencal FCB is one of my personal favourite picks!

Available in the Origin or Essential bike builds and frameset. Both bikes have the same geometry and aluminium frameset. Main differences is the cheaper Origin has a 1×11 Apex 1, Sram groupset and the Essential with more capable 1×12 GX Eagle, Rival 1 setup.

Weighing in at 10.9 kg, hydraulic disc brakes, 425 chainstay length, 1.4 stack/reach ratio and comes with the Maxxis Rambler 700c x 45 tires. Prices start from $2,399!

2022 Kona Dew

Technically categorised as a commuter bike the Kona Dew still takes a spot in the flat bar gravel bike list.

Marin DSX 2

View at Jenson USA

Marin have also jumped on the flat bar gravel bike bandwagon and have resealed their take on these cruisy type of bikes. This rig has been build around their Gestalt X dropbar platform. Expanding Marin’s beyond road line of bikes, with a more comfortable and upright position.

Festuring a upper mid-range 1 x 12 Deore groupset, and hydraulic disc brakes. The bike also comes with a Forged Aluminum, 42T Steel Narrow Wide Chainring and Shimano Deore 12-Speed, 10-51T cassette.

The bike also stands out with a low standover and mountain bike sensibilities in a flat bar package capable of fast commutes, long Saturday adventures spanning pavement and light singletrack, and all-day gravel events.

Built around an alloy frame and carbon fork, the DSX frame is equipped for internally routed dropper posts. With 700Cx45mm tires and clearance for up to 29 x 2.1″ tires.

Priority 600

An automotive inspired all-road model, the Priority 600 (600 stands for the Pinion 600% gear range). Designed to be a performance daily commuter, road rambler, and gravel grinder. The gearbox offers unmatched versatility and dependability with no worries for thousands of miles. No servicing required like you would a normal drivetrain gravel bike.

Marin Muirwoods

Make traveling fun again. The Muirwoods by Marin is made for urban jungle and countryside adventures. A sub $1,000 flat bar gravel bike options with the ability to run up to 27.5 x 2.2″ tires. Also making it a perfect budget tourer or gravel commuter.

Caletti Scrambler Flat Bar City Shredder

If you have the budget you could look at the Caletti Scrambler, it’s a little expensive. But you do get a custom build flat bar gravel bike. The geometry is designed from the ground up to utilise flat bars. “The SCRAMBLER is our 100% fun, versatile ripper. It’s ready to rip around town, grind gravel, shred singletrack, commute to work and escape town on a bike tour. It’s the perfect mixed terrain bike.” The bike build features, slider dropouts allow you too easily set it up for geared on single speed use, replace a damaged derailleur hanger or use an internally geared hub. You get the room for up to 50mm tires. While also including Size Specific tubing. Complete bike build prices start from $5700, you can find out more here.

Norco Search XR Flat Bar

The Norco Search Flat bar features a X6 butted aluminum frame and carbon fork offer lightweight, agile stability, efficient pedaling, and comfortable vertical compliance. The Flay Bar setup allows you to explore singletrack trails, tackle a gravel loop, or head out for an extended bike-packing excursion.

Featuring a 1 x 11 Deore groupset with Alloy 2 Piece Narrow Wide, 40T crank and Shimano Deore CS-M5100, 11-51T cassette. On some 700c x 38mm tires.

Rondo Booz

Built with a  robust, lightweight and elegant Chromoly (CrMo) steel frame and TwinTip carbon forks along with a SRAM Apex 1×11-Speed groupset. The Rondo Booz is considered a steel urban racer, with fat and fast rolling 650b x 47c tires and an overall sporty geometry. Weighing in at 10.7 kg.

It’s designed for the best of both worlds thanks to the adjustable forks enabling you to change the geometry to suit the terrain/riding type. Steeper steering angles for a low and aggressive road bike feel. Or slacker angles and a longer wheelbase for endurance or off-road adventure riding.

Octane One Gridd Flat

Categorised as an adventure road bike, the One Gridd Flat gravel bike features an alloy adventure-geometry frame, carbon fork and flat handlebars, paired with Shimano wide-range 1×10-Speed drivetrain, hydraulic disc brakes. The overall geometry is compact and aggressive, while still providing immense comfort on longer rides.

Powered by a Shimano Deore 10-Speed drivetrain with an 11-42T cassette at the rear and 38T FSA crank up front. coming with 700c x 35 tires with a max clearance for 700x43c Tires. With mounting points for panniers and mudguards.

Jamis Sequel S2 Flat Bar

If a flat bar urban adventure/gravel bike is what you are after. This Jamis has you covered! Build to tackle midtown urban rides, with the SSD custom geometry and Reynolds double-butted chromoly frame the bike delivers a comfortable ride with maximum stability in every frame size.

Built to be a comfortable ride, it has a wide and consistent stack and reach range, Jamis set out to design a frame that could handle your everyday city life. With the wide collection of fork and frame mounts this bike is truely versatile.

It comes with, 650b wheels, 12mm thru-axle mounting and the high-volume 47c tires, hydraulic disc brakes, a no-nonsense 1 x 10 drivetrain. This means a FSA Vero Pro 40T crank and Shimano CS-M4100, 10-speed, 11-42T cassette.

Flatbar X Interstellar Blue

This has to be one of the more affordable flat bar bike options. They have stuck with the Poseidon X’s original frame design and full carbon fork which gives you responsiveness where you need it yet a stable ride on descents. Including a complete 1×10 Advent X Groupset with the clutch rear derailleur to keep your chain in line even with the hardest impacts brought on the trail.

Resulting in a rather good gearing range, thanks to the simple 38t narrow wide chainring matched with the Advent X 11-48t cassette. The flat bars offer a more upright position opposed to the dropbar X but it still retains some of it’s sporty responsiveness. The bike comes with 700×35 tires and can handle up to a 700×40 tire with clearance for mud. 650b compatible with up to a 1.9″ wide tire.

Orro Terra Gravel Flatbar 105 Bike

Orro remind us, which I forgot to mention earlier that flat bar gravel bikes especially this Terra Gravel are also great street hardy commuters. These type of bikes make great off-road commuters. Built from a triple butted aluminium frame. It is named a bike that is capable at being a city commuter while also taking you off-road, making it a superb all-in-one explorer. The bike is designed to smooth out the roughest off-road routes with the carbon forks. You also get a Shimano 105 drivetrain with 22 speed and TRP Spyre Mechanical Disc brakes. Rolling on Fulcrum Racing 700DB wheels with Vittoria Zaffiro 28C tyres, with 12mm thru-axles allowing for larger, grippier tyres to be fitted if you wish. You also get the option to mount mudguard and racks. This bike is a super bit of kit at under 10 kg it is well balanced. Priced at $1499.99

Niner RLT 9 Apex

Niner noticed that quite a few of their customers where buying there original drop bar RLT gravel bike and chucking on some flat bars, So they made this build available to everyone by offer it in the form of the RLT 9 Apex flat bar gravel bike. Niner really focused on comfort with this bike offering a slightly longer chainstay, lower bottom bracket, and slacker headtube angle which makes it ideal for a stable rides on and off roads. Again another aluminum frame with a carbon fork. They have continued their award-winning ways with a full carbon, gravel-tuned and custom painted Niner Carbon Fork. Also Niner make sure that their frames and forks are tested to mountain bike safety standards. This bike is a 10 speed with a Shimano Tiagra 4700 groupset. But Niner use the standard PF30 bottom bracket shell offering both geared and single speed compatibility using the new BioCentric 30. Priced at $1650

All-City Macho Man Disc Flat Bar

The Macho Man flat bar gravel bike is perfect for those who are looking for a fast, responsive, bike for quick trips around town or off-roading. With their expanded size range they do a great job at preserving the bike size dimension as the bike scales for larger and small sizes. Meaning that for 43,46, and 49 sizes the Macho Man as 650B size wheels and 700c wheels on the other frame sizes. Smaller riders will benefit from smaller top tube lengths and the greater acceleration that a lighter, smaller wheel provides. Built from a 612 Select CroMoly frame and 4130 CroMoly Fork. The bike features bi-plane lugged fork crown, signature brazed-on seat collar, signature vertical dropouts, custom bottom bracket, bottle boss reinforcements and an E.D. coating. It clears a 38c tire (650b x 42c on the smaller sizes) with plenty of room for mud, has fender mounts for rain days, and rear rack capability. Powered by a SRAM Apex 1 rear derailleur with 11 speed. Priced at $1499 at Tree Fort Bikes.

Cube SL Road SL Bike

When looking at the details of this bike it draws a fine line to a flat bar gravel bike. But I believe you can still put it in this category. Technically called a fitness bike and advertised as a bike that has “been built for Strava KOM chasers or, in fact, anyone who enjoys the thrill of greater speed and more distance.” Considering it has Schwalbe G-One Allround, Perf. Kevlar, 40 tires i would classify this as a potential gravel bike option. The bike combines Shimano’s superb Ultegra groupset, powerful hydraulic disc brakes and DT Swiss wheels. Giving you a 22 speed bike that is capable at getting up those real tough climbs and providing enough speed to also get down quick. The build is light at 9.8 kg and stiff, with a Aluminum frame and carbon fork combo. Priced at $1649

Saracen Levarg Flat Bar Gravel Bike Cherry Red

If you are after a budget friendly flat bar gravel bike the Saracen Levarg might be your answer! A UK bike that is designed to tackle tough roads for your commuting needs. Combining a MTB influenced gravel geometry with a flat bar was the idea behind the Levarg Flat Bar Gravel Bike. Featuring 650b x 47c WTB tires it is purpose built to take you on the more ‘adventurous’ route to work. Priced at £899.99

Cube SL Road Flat Bar

Marketed as a flat bar road bike built to be fast and efficient making it perfect for commuting, casual rides or getting fit. With an aluminium frame and carbon fork to reduce road vibrations. Making it an option to take those gravel commuting routes to work. Not the biggest tire clearance at around 700c x 40mm with some room to move.
