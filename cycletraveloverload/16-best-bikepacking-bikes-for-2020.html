16 Best Bikepacking Bikes For 2020

Read Time:11 Minute, 41 Second

Since bikepacking is the type of riding that is gravel, off-road and epic terrains, one requires a bike to be able to stand up to the demands of such riding.

What should I look for in a Bikepacking bike?

I personally have been looking for a bikepacking bike and considering the options. For something that is purpose built for bikepakcing. I currently have a Surly Long Haul Trucker that I have built to suit adventure cycling conditions. But I feel I am missing out on what a true bikepacking bike is all about.

I have been researching what exactly to look for when considering a bikepacking bike. First thing is the bikes material. You can get bikepacking bikes in carbon, titanium, aluminum and steel. And all have their own benefits.

Furthermore it would be great if the bike came with lots of mounting points for water bottles and cargo cages for extra storage. A large front frame triangle would be great allowing space for a frame bag. Large tyre clearance is always great. Suspension although not required is another factor that some bikepacking bikes have.

Kona Sutra LTD – $2199

This Kona is the ultimate bikepacking bike that feels like a mountain bike with drop bars, but also has the speed of a road bike. Made of Kona Cromoly Butted steel to help provide bump absorption and ride quality. The bike has a nice downhill feel on the bars with a 16-degree flare. Strong stopping power with the SRAM Rival 1 hydraulic disc brakes. Featuring a 1x drivetrain and beefy yet fast-rolling WTB Venture TCS 700x50c tires. The 2020 version provides a couple updates from the 2019 and 2018 models. At first glance you will notice a new color scheme, with the “Gloss Earth Grey” color with grey and “Mustard” decals. They also added a third mount t the forks, making each set a complete three-pack suited for Anything style cargo cages. The frame has been updated with the ability to fit wider tires, some people have reported being able to squeeze in 29 × 2.35″ tires as well as 27.5 x 2.8″ rubber, although both of those sizes might be pretty tight. 29 x 2.25″ and 27.5 x 2.6″ are more realistic max clearances. The wheels have been upgraded, the internal rim width used to be 23mm now it is 25mm.

Salsa Cutthroat Apex 1 – $2,899

The Cutthroat Apex 1 features the 1 X 11 drivetrain, which is the most secure, easy-to-use systems on the market. The bike is built to ride long, hard and fast in comfort. Making it a great bike for bikepacking and off-road touring. With a racing-inspired geometry, made from full carbon, with the Class 5 Vibration Reduction System for increased riding comfort.

The carbon fork has Three-Pack rack mounts and internal dynamo cable routing. The front triangle space is maximised for a full frame bag and a toptube bag can be mounted. And the Cutthroat has a rear rack compatibility with rack-lock. The bike fits up to 29 x 2.4″ tires. The bike comes with Maxxis Ikon 2.2″ Tires. Also comes in a front suspension fork option. You can pick this bike up from $2,899

Salsa Fargo Apex 1 – $2,199

The Fargo Apex 1 features a steel frame and carbon Firestarter 110 fork. A cheaper alternative to the Cutthroat if you are not looking for something that need to be lightweight and fast. Built for bikepacking and off-road touring and single-track. It is more then capable! Especially with the 29 x 2.2” wheel and tire combination providing excellent rollover, low rolling resistance, and great momentum. All powered with a SRAM Apex 1 drivetrain for smooth shifting.

For this 2020 model the frame has not changed since the previous. Still having the capacity for 27.5+ or 29+ wheels and tires via the rotating Alternator dropouts. Appart from the firs red colour. The biggest changes are that it gets i25mm WTB rims and TRP Spyre mechanical disc brakes.

Surly ECR – $1900

The ECR is Surly’s ultimate off-road touring machine. A bike that is designed for routes with way less pavement and a lot more trail. Being re-designed specifically for 27.5 or 29 wheel platforms and has been updated to include Surly”s Gnot-Boost rear hub spacing; compatible with Boost 148 x 12mm thru-axle, 142 x 12mm thru-axle and 135mm quick-release hubs.

The bike features all the mounting possibilities you could ever want in a bikepacking rig. With rack and fender, multiple three-pack mounts and mid-blade. Comes with Surly Knard 29×3.0″ tires, with a clearance for 27.5″ x 3″ (XS–M) or 29” x 3” (M–XL) tires (with or without fenders).

Norco Search XR Steel S1 – $3,299

This Norco bike is a bike that you could call a do anything kind of bike. A slick and hardy adventure bike, that blurs the line between between mountain and road. Pushing the limits for what is possible with a drop bar bike.

Offering a generous tire clearance, with smaller riders getting the best possible ride without any toe-overlap, the 45.5 and 48cm models are designed around 650b wheels, achieving the same angles and ride as the larger frames. Ample tire clearance for 650B x 2.1” or 700c x 28c-45c tires (up to 42c with full fenders). The Search XR still provides versitility with accessory mounts for fender, bottle mounts and racks. While giving you a lightweight adventure frame. Made from a Reynolds 725 chromoly frame and a carbon fork, to reduce road chatter up front.

Lynskey Titanium GR300 – $3,200

The Lynskey is mentioned in this list as one of the better titanium bikepacking bike options on the market. The new 2020 external cable routing bike. Is categorised as gravel bike. Making it suitable for gravel and adventure-focused riders who demand a bike that ticks all the boxes. Weather that be road, gravel, rugged commuter, and bikepacking.

The bike handles extremely well on loose terrain at speed. With a adventure-driven geometry. With a tire clearance for 700c x max 45 or 27.5 x max 2.1″. It comes with a top tube mount and 3 water bottle mounts. Lacks any mounting on the carbon fork as expected. Does have rack and fender mounts. Also the Tapered Headtube ensures precision handling when paired with Lynskey’s Carbon Gravel Fork.

Ghost Fire Road Rage 6.9 – $2,766.50

This is a popular bikepacking bike in the Cycle Travel Overload camp. Absolutely in love with this rig! This bike knows no boundaries. It has mounting bike qualities; with its 29″ tires and the Vario post. While showing it has touring abilities with its stiff frame and luggage loading options all over the bike. The bike features powerful, weather-independent post mount disc brakes which are said to be great for long descents and are what you need when descending with a fully loaded bike.

Kona Woo Fat bike – $1,800

The Kona Woo is a combination of both the Wo and Wozo. Making it in the list for a our best fat bikepacking bikes for 2020. Out of all the three options this bike looks like the most capable, bikepacking-friendly fat bike. Kona describes this bike as “the ultimate fat bike utilizing our favourite attributes from its siblings in the lineup.”

The Woo speaks off some incredible bikepacking utility features. Outfitted with a new carbon fork with triple cage mounts, massive 26 x 4.8″ tires, and bolt-on frame bag compatibility.

One thing about this bike that might not be too favourable is there is no mounts for rear racks. But rather mounting options for front racks. It would have been great if they included both options. Comes with a budget SRAM SX 12-speed Eagle drivetrain which powers 26” Sun Ringle Mulefut 80mm tubeless rims. Vee Rubber Snow Avalanche and Snowshoe XL 4.8” tires ensure you can power through the snow, mud, sand. The bike has options for a Rohloff hub. And the option for 29×3.0″ / 27.5×3.0-4.0″ wheelsets for a mix of riding.

Scott Spark 950 – $3,476.89

If you are looking for a full suspension mountain bike for your bikepacking adventures. This Scott Spark might be the one! The SCOTT Spark 950 has a super light Alloy frame, a FOX Float shock and fork, a SRAM Eagle drivetrain and Scott’s TwinLoc remote to always optimize your ride.

Bombtrack Beyond+ Adv – From $3,499 AUD

“It’s a bike you can pack up your life on and go across the Siberia or the Rockies on or you can simply do what we did and load up for a cheeky midweek overnighter on the South Downs and enjoy the fresh air. It will handle either with ease and will put a smile on your face when those wheels just keep rolling over everything.“

This bike can pretty much go anywhere you want! With the wide 29’’ tires, with a Sram GX Eagle 12-speed drivetrain with 10 – 50t cassette making for easy gearing. And a radical backswept Jones bar. The double butted frame and carbon fork cut weight and increase comfort. They have really thought about how this bike will be used and offer a low-profile geometry. Making for easier stand over when you are up against technical sections. This also makes the bike easier to get on when loaded.

The bike has a aftermarket BPC (bikepacking carbon) fork with triple cage mounts on both legs. Magura Trail Sport hydraulic disc brake with four front pistons and two at the rear for maximum power and control. Lastly you will find, tubeless ready WTB ST i45 front rim, WTB ST i40 rear rim with tubeless ready WTB Ranger Tough / Fast rolling 3.0” tires.

Breezer Radar Expert – $969.99

For a sub $1000 bikepacking bike this is a favourite with many bikepackers. It is quite possibility the best bike you can get for under $1000 for bikepacking! A truely do it all bike for all terrains. The bike comes with 45 mm tires ready for all-conditions. You get a wide range of gearing, rack and fender mounts and a compact geometry making it ride trails and rough terrain with ease.

You get three water bottle cage mount points on the inside frame and 2 eyelets on either side of the fork.

Diamondback Haanjo 5C EXP – $2,000

Categorised as a gravel bike, that is designed to take you off the beaten track. With a light, tough carbon fiber frame and fork with thru axles front and rear. Rolling on 27.5 inch HED Tomcat wheels with forgiving 650x47c tires. The bike has 2×10 Speed gearing which is plenty. Diamondback went with bar-end shifters for ease of on-trail maintenance and reliability.

All City Gorilla Monsoon – $2099

The Gorilla Monsoon is a beast of a bike. Translated to “Monster Cross-bike’ the Monsoon is one of those bikes that allows you to take it pretty much anywhere, from road riding to bikepacking gnarly terrain. Basically a beefed up all rounder. According to All City this steed can do it all. “Want to do mixed pavement and single track rides? Put in long days off-road? Do some fire road touring? Camping? Ride across the continent? No problem.”

Comes with a all steel frame and fork made from a Select CroMoly steel, double butted, select tubing and features a lovely bi-plane crown steel fork. Hayes CX Expert, 160mm Disc breaks front and rear. With 27.5″ x 2.4″ WTB Riddler tires that are tubeless ready. Has all the eyelets and mounting options you need with, front rack, low rider rack, rear rack potential. And also compatibility with Surly-8 and 24-Pack racks. With the option to fit fenders. Three mounts for bottle cages.

Cannondale Topstone Sora – From $1,050

The 2020 Cannondale Topstone Sora is a great budget friendly bikepacking bike option. This is a perfect entry point bike for those looking to get into bikepacking and gravel riding. The bike comes with multiple mounts for bottle cages and storage. The frame is made from a Cannondale SmartForm C2 Alloy. With a full carbon fork which adds an extra touch of comfort and the 12mm thru-axle with tapered steerer enables light and responsive steering for predictable handling no matter the conditions. You can pick up the 2020 Cannondale Topstone Ultegra option which is their top of the line option from $2,100 USD.

Fuji Jari 1.1 – $2,299.99

The Jari 1.1 is designed for gravel riding, light touring, bikepacking or even long road outings. Built with a A6-SL custom-butted alloy frame for light weight, strength, and reliability. Powered by a Shimano 105 2×11 drivetrain for flawless shifting and a wide ratio of gears to tackle any terrain. The fork is made from a FC-440 Cross full carbon with a tapered steerer tube plus rack/water bottle mounts so you can mount everything you need for bikepacking adventures.

Genesis Vagabond – $1,690

The Genesis Vagabond is a monstercross bike in the loosest sense of the term; the illegitimate lovechild of a road and mountain bike, a ‘frankenbike’ that has long been the staple of shed tinkerers, the result of many an experimental ‘I wonder if…’ spare parts box builds.

The base of the bike is built up from a Genesis Mjolnir Seamless Double-Butted Cromoly steel frame. Offering excellent ride quality and versatility. You can expect strength and suppleness with the Cromoly Unicrown steel fork. The SRAM GX 2×10 speed drivetrain provides reliable, fast, light and accurate shifting with improved durability. You get TRP Spyre-C mechanical disc brakes for stopping power. All rolling on WTB Nano 29×2.1″ Tyres.
