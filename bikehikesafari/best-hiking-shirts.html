Buyers Guide

Now that you have more information about the best hiking shirts available, how do you decide which one will be best for you? There are several things you will want to consider when making your choice. 

One of the questions you will need to ask yourself is how important the color and pattern of the shirt is.

Some shirts have limited color choices so it will be harder to find something to match your other hiking clothes. You should also think about whether you want to have a collar on your shirt.

Depending on what style of hiking you are doing, it can be useful if you want to stop off somewhere for lunch or a drink either as part of your walk or activity, or straight afterwards. Some people also prefer the smarter look as opposed to casual active wear. 

Button down shirts can be useful, especially if you want to roll up the sleeves. Snap buttons are easier to use than traditional buttons, particularly if you have sweaty hands!

But some people will prefer a t-shirt style with no buttons at all. Think about whether you want a relaxed fit, an active fit, or a tight fit shirt. 

Having pockets on the shirt will be important for some people, to allow easy access to certain items without having to go into your rucksack.

A zip up pocket is easier to use than a button pocket and arguably more secure for smaller items. Some chest pockets are large enough to store a passport or documentation which is useful if you are travelling.  

Another thing you will need to consider is what materials you would like your shirt to be made from.

Most hiking shirts are made from synthetic materials, but some are blended with natural fabrics like Merino wool.

This is because natural fibres like this have useful properties like thermoregulation, odor resistance and moisture control.

If you are conscious about your impact on the environment, then you might want to choose a shirt made from recycled materials. You can also keep an eye out for whether the shirt is Fair Trade. 

It is important to choose a hiking shirt with moisture wicking technology, and it is helpful if the fabric is quick drying.

There are some other features which can be useful, like a mesh panel to assist with ventilation or UPF sun protection. It depends largely on what climate you expect to be hiking in.

Thermoregulation or an Omni-freeze Zero cooling system can be beneficial in warm climates, and if you are heading to an area with lots of bugs then you might want to get a shirt with BugsAway protection to stop you getting bitten.  

If you are planning on carrying a rucksack during your hike then it is recommended to get a shirt with adjusted seams.

If the shoulder seams are positioned further forward then on a standard shirt, this will prevent chafing and friction from the rucksack strap. 

Once you have decided which features are a priority for your hiking shirt, the next most important thing is comfort.

You will be wearing this shirt for hours at a time so make sure it feels nice against your skin and fits you properly. Use the size guides properly to ensure you buy the right size, and remember to take into consideration any other layers you might wear. If you plan on wearing a t- shirt or a vest under your hiking shirt then you might need to size up to avoid it being too tight. 

Frequently Asked Questions 

Are natural materials better than synthetic materials?

Outdoor clothing manufacturers use different blends of synthetic and natural materials to create their products.
Some natural fibres like Merino wool have some really useful features, but they can be less durable and harder to care for than synthetic materials.
You also need to check whether the wool has been sustainably sourced and is cruelty free. Synthetic materials are designed and made for purpose, which means they will last a long time and perform well.
However, you might want to use recycled synthetic materials to avoid having a negative impact on the environment. 

Why is moisture wicking fabric so important?

When you take part in outdoor activities like hiking, your body will release sweat. If this sweat gets trapped by the fabric of your clothing it won’t evaporate, and will remain close to your skin.
This puts you at risk of developing a chill, or getting a skin rash. It also allows bacteria to grow which could lead to bad body odor or even a skin infection.
Moisture wicking fabric prevents all of that by drawing the moisture away from your skin and towards the outer layer of the fabric. From here it can evaporate.
