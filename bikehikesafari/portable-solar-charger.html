Buyer’s Guide

Solar charger relies on the sun, so you should be able to charge on the go with the solar panels out in the sunshine. However, when it comes to determining what kind of solar charger you want, there are a couple of things you need to consider. 

Number of USB charging Ports

The first thing you need to decide is how many devices you will be using and how many will need to be charged during your trip. This will help determine how many UBB charging ports you will need.

You also need to think about how long your trip is going to be. This is because, the longer the trip, the more gear you will need to bring. Therefore, you will need a solar charger that is small and portable, that doesn’t take up too much space in your backpack. 

Weight

All the Portable Solar Chargers in this review are lightweight, portable and suitable for backpacking trips. Most are more suited to hiking in groups or couples where it is a great idea to split the weight you carry so the overall load is lighter per person. Overall, most of these weigh similar to the weight of a Lightweight Backpacking Power Bank Battery.

Charging Time and Speed

There are a lot of options out there, but not all of them are really powerful. You want something that will charge your devices efficiently, so then it’s less of a worry for you on your trip. 

Also, these chargers require sunlight to work. All these chargers will work when it is cloudy but not with the same level of efficiency. And any of the ones in this review will do a good job as a portable backpacking solar charger.

Conclusion 

Overall all the portable solar chargers in this review are awesome and can be a great addition to your lightweight backpacking gear list, or even your bikepacking gear list. Frequently Asked Questions

How Do I Choose The Best Solar Charger?

It all depends on what is most convenient for you. It is important to keep in mind that the bigger the solar panels, then the more energy is going to be absorbed. Which then results in your devices being charged quicker. 

However, you need to be aware of what much space you have to carry the charger around. A lot can be folded down and made more compatible. Yet, it does depend on how much and often you will need to charge devices. 

Are Backpacking Solar Chargers Worth It?

If you are taking a very long backpacking trip, with an unreliable battery or power source, then you should consider purchasing a solar charger.

Even if it is just to be used in an emergency, you can always guarantee at some point you will get some sunlight to use the charger if you really need to.

They are convenient to have on hand and can provide you with a more reliable power source that you don’t need to worry about.
