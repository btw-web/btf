Patagonia Capilene Air Crew

Patagonia Men’s Capilene Air Crew

Pros:
> Made with 51% RWS-certified merino wool
> Together with 49% recycled polyester
> It stretches to allow ease of movement
Cons:
> Premium product at a premium price

If you’re passionate about the environment, then this is the base layer top for you!

It’s made of 51% RWS-certified merino wool and 49% recycled polyester.

RWS-certified means that it meets the Responsible Wool Standard from farm to fabric which is there to ensure protection for both the animals that supply the wool, and the land that they graze.

The fabric feels really soft against your skin and it stretches to allow movement, eliminating any chafe points. And it’s really nice and warm too.

It’s called an “air crew” because it’s an airy blend of fabrics, made with a special 18.5 micron-gauge yarn.

It’s also really durable and is designed to last. And it provides moisture wicking for when you start to sweat, and it dries out quite quickly too.

It’s available in a choice of 4 different colors. When this article was written the crater blue option was on sale.

Overall, these are a great environmentally responsible baselayer for backpacking.

Buyers Guide

As promised, here’s your buying guide. This will walk you through all the key points to think about before you buy. 

Size and Fit

It is important that you get the right size base layers for your frame. Base layers that are too tight will be particularly uncomfortable, and base layers that are too baggy will allow the breeze to get to your skin and make you too cold.

If you are buying online rather than in store, I would strongly recommend that you check out the size charts, and whether the garments are true to size.

Fabric Properties

Now, this should be your second consideration after size and fit. You need fabrics that will keep you comfortable during your backpacking trip, whatever the weather happens to be doing.

This means that the base layers should help to maintain a comfortable temperature by locking in heat. At the same time however, they also have to be breathable, and if necessary, be able to wick away any sweat. And if it can also resist body odor, that’s a great plus, too.

Aesthetic

Even when you’re out in the woods, it’s nice to be color coordinated. It’s always a good idea to choose new clothing against how well it goes with what’s already in your wardrobe.

Value for Money 

Now, we would argue that “more affordable” does not necessarily translate to better value for money. Better quality clothing tends to cost more money than lower quality items.

But if you do have to be budget conscious, by all means check prices as you go along.
