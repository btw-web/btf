Buyers Guide

When it comes to buying glasses for hiking, any old pair won’t do. There are certain things you need to look for and the most important element you need to consider is what kind of lens you want. Alongside that, you need to make sure that the sunglasses are comfortable.

No one wants a pair of sunglasses that feel heavy and begin to rub your nose and ears as you begin to sweat. Below we are going to go through some key elements you need to know about and think about when choosing the right sunglasses for you.

What kind of lenses should you go for?

UV protection is one of the most important things to have on your sunglasses. This is due to the fact that UV rays can be really harmful to your eyes.

You want glasses that cover all UV protection, but especially UVA and UVB. However, having glasses that cover all three types that include UVC is really important. Especially as UVB is associated with skin cancer. 

Another lens to consider is VLT lenses. These lenses will reduce the amount of light that is transmitted to your eyes. It is all about reducing the harsh glare of the sun and direct sunlight hurting your eyes. 

Most of the sunglasses on this list have polarized lenses, which is really vital when hiking in snow or near large bodies of water. Polarized lenses filter out reflected light and reduce glare massively. By doing this, colors become a lot deeper and richer.

Polarized lens colors are really a matter of preference, but certain colors are more useful for certain weather conditions. Some lens colors let more light or less light in. Grey is a pretty neutral color that works for a range of conditions. However it really is just personal preference.

Lens Materials

Lenses are either made from plastic or polycarbonate, each has either pros and cons.

Polycarbonate is a high quality plastic that is strong and impact resistant. These lenses can scratch if you don’t have an anti-scratch coating but they are very durable, which is ideal when out hiking. Compared to plastic lenses that are much cheaper and a lot less durable. 

When you are looking at what the lens is made from, you should be swayed more towards polycarbonate lenses than just plastic. However you should always make sure that the lenses come with coatings such as anti-scratch or glare.

Frames

When it comes to the frames of your sunglasses, the most durable materials to look for are nylon or nylon and plastic. These frames will last you a lot longer and are suited for tough conditions. Ideally you should look for nylon frames as they are more reliable for a hiker. 

As you will be wearing these sunglasses while hiking, you need sunglasses that are comfortable to wear. Some sunglasses will come with padding to make them easier to wear. While others will have adjustable features that create a more secure fit, while also making them more comfortable to wear.

Looking at the little details of the sunglasses you want to buy will help you understand if you can wear them on a long hike. You need to remember that you may sweat while on your hike, so you want to make sure that the frames stay on your face but also don’t dig in anywhere. 

You want sunglasses that mould to your face and are extremely comfortable to wear that you actually forget that you are wearing sunglasses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Do I Need To Look For In Hiking Sunglasses?

Your number one priority, when buying hiking sunglasses, is that they will protect your eyes from all UV rays and blue light rays. The style and design of the sunglasses consideration should come after you have found a pair of sunglasses that will protect your eyes.

Are Polarized Sunglasses Better For Hiking?

Polar sunglasses are really a must have for hiking, especially if you like hiking in the snow or near large bodies of water. Polarized sunglasses give you protection from direct sunlight and glare, which is created by snow and water.

Should You Wear Sunglasses While Hiking?

It is up to you, but we recommend that you do. They not only protect your eyes from the sun, but they help you see better in all weather conditions. These sunglasses can come with colored lenses that help you see things in more detail and more accurately, than not wearing sunglasses at all.
