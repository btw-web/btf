Best Hiking Sandals

This is a gear review about the best hiking sandals for backpacking and hiking 2022.

Some hikers love to use Hiking Sandals for backpacking, as a replacement for lightweight hiking boots or lightweight hiking shoes. Just add a pair of hiking socks and off you go. Backpacking with hiking sandals places less of a burden on your feet compared with heavy boots.

When at camp, using a pair of ultralight hiking sandals for camp shoes gives the feet a chance to rest and recover after a long day of hiking. They also make a great pair of footwear for river crossings.

Which one should you buy? Having looked at what is important in a hiking sandals, I prepared a list of the best hiking sandals for your consideration.

Best Hiking Sandal 2022

Keen Newport H2

Keen Newport H2

Pros:
> Clever design means more traction.
> Very comfortable.
> Designed to last
> Can be used as a replacement for shoes or boots
Pros:
> Heavy

The first thing we need to talk about is the grip. For any hiking shoe, traction is a necessity, and in the Newport H2 they have it in spades. These are my current favorite hiking sandals and the ones I use on day hikes at the moment.

The soles on these sandals have traction in all but muddy trails. It is also using non-marking rubber which means that no marks will be left should you go inside.

It has a soft insole and the soft polyester webbing mean that your feet stay comfortable all day, no matter the length of your journey. 

The fit is super comfortable thanks to a wide toebox. And there is lots of protection for the toes thanks to the enclosed rubber toe guard.

The Keen Newport Sandal uses a hydrophobic mesh lining to prevent the sandal becoming heavy when wet. It is a solid sandal that shouldn’t break, crack, or absorb water, meaning you don’t have to worry about losing a shoe on a trek, and they are even machine washable. The one thing about these sandals, they are heavy.

Overall, these are the best sandals for backpacking.

Best Ultralight Hiking Sandal 2022

The Bedrock Cairn 3D Adventure

The Bedrock Cairn 3D Adventure

Pros:
> Substantial grip.
> Minimal design keeps things comfortable.
> Easily repaired or to have parts replaced.

Cons:
> Wears over time.

With a more minimalist design than competitors, you would think the Bedrock Cairn 3D Adventure would lose some edge. However, anything lost on top has been put into the grip of the shoe, allowing it to hold traction in muddy terrain.

 Comfort is at a premium, as less material means less rubbing on your feet, so blisters and sensitive skin are kept to a minimum.

While you would think this would mean that the sandal would be loose on your foot, the 3 adjustable straps on top keep it in place tightly and securely. 

The minimal nature of the design can lead to wearing on the sole a little, but it takes an extremely long time for the sandal to reach that point. Even if the sole wears away, it is easily replaced.

Overall, these are the best ultralight hiking sandals and the best lightweight camp sandals.

Best of the Rest – Lightweight Hiking Sandals

Xero Z-Trail

Xero Z-Trail

Pros:
> Flexible design gives grip.
> Soft foam for maximum comfort.
> Flexible design also makes it durable
Cons:
> Not as durable as come of the other sandals in this review

The Z-trail offers a hardened rubber outer sole that will cling to rocks or trails with ease, while also remaining remarkably flexible, able to bend to the shape of your foot and object underneath.

The sole of the sandal is made up of three layers with the top one being foam, which adds a level of comfort. Not only that, but the straps cross your foot, meaning less pressure and therefore less likelihood of sore skin or blisters. 

The flexibility and contouring of the shoe, mean that no matter the situation the Z-trail will still be strong and sturdy, showing no hints of cracks or tears that other sandals may get.

This level of durability is even more impressive when you realize the shoes are 100% vegan.

Overall, these are a very well made pair of lightweight hiking sandals.

Chaco Z/2 Classic

Chaco Z/2 Classic

Pros:
> Stable with good traction.
> Incredibly durable.

Cons:
> Uncomfortable.

Their flagship products always include adjustable outdoor sandals and have always focussed on practicality above all things.

Having a hard-plastic outsole, the Z/2 utilizes Siping to increase traction on the sandal, while having a wide base with secure straps means that this sandal is incredibly stable under foot.

Unfortunately, it is the comfort on the Z/2 that lets it down, as the sole is hard and offers little comfort to new feet. Over time, the comfort level increases, but it is not something you want to worry about when paddling down a river or using as camp shoes only. 

What the Z/2 loses in comfort, it more than makes up for in durability, with the hard shell of the shoe incredibly resistant to any form of damage, which is very useful when you are miles away from any kind of help.

Overall, these a strong hiking sandal best suited as a camp sandal or for use on shorter hiking trails.

Buyers Guide

When it comes to buying hiking sandals, the key thing to think about is are they going to be as robust and useful as boots without the negatives.

With boots, we know what we are looking for, and we should use this line of thought for sandals, as they are going to be put through the same paces.

As such, I settled on three things that every hiker needs from a good boot, while applying them to sandals.

Traction

Traction is incredibly important. If you are hiking in the rain, on a muddy trail, or over unstable ground, you want your sandals to grip the surface well. All the sandals in this gear review do that.

Comfort

If you are wandering the trails all day, miles from home in the middle of nowhere, or out on the river or ocean, the very last thing you want is a blister, soreness, or cramp in your foot.

Discomfort from your backpacking sandals sours your enjoyment and leaves you in pain throughout, with a worst-case scenario leaving you unable to continue and potentially stranded. This makes comfort essential for hiking sandals.

Durability

This is perhaps the most important factor in deciding hiking footwear. Loss of traction makes you slower and more careful, lack of comfort can slow you down and sometimes stops you, the loss of your footwear through breakage can leave you stranded. If you are climbing or walking through thick undergrowth in the forest, a broken shoe is the worst possible outcome. Any continuation would mean injuring yourself and leaving you vulnerable, it also might mean you can’t turn around depending on how far you have come.

Conclusion

This list has provided some of the best hiking sandals on sale. We looked at the traction they have, the comfort they provide, and the durability they need for you to progress.

Without appropriate footwear, it would be impossible for many adventures to even start, making them some of the most important equipment available to the experience and the beginner alike.

Should you be deciding on a new trek or having already started one, be sure to keep ahead of the curve by purchasing the appropriate hiking sandal for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which are better: hiking boots or hiking sandals?

This question is dependent on your circumstances. If you are hiking through boggy terrain with a high chance of objects injuring unprotected feet, then a boot is the obvious choice.
However, if you are hiking through coastal areas with open ground, then sandals are probably a better choice. It is about weighing up your options and choosing appropriately.

Are hiking sandals only for hiking?

No, you can use them however you wish! The comfort and ease at which they are put on means many people use them in lieu of regular shoes, especially at hotter times of year.
In fact, many people have them as a kind of outdoor slipper, allowing the indoor pair to remain intact. The durability of the sandal as well means they can last no matter what you are using them for.
