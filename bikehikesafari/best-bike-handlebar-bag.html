Buyers Guide

As promised, here’s your buying guide. You can use it as a checklist to make sure you’ve thought of everything before you buy.

Capacity

Most Bikepacking Handlebar Bags are between the 8L to 14L which is big enough for most people to store some of their camping gear.

Handlebar Bags for cycle touring are generally around the 4L to 8L capacity which is perfect for valuables, cameras, snacks and sunscreen etc.

Secure Fastening

The two different styles of bags have very different fastening systems.

Most Bicycle Touring handlebar bags have some sort of quick release system and shoulder straps that allow the bag to removed quickly when off the bike.

Most Bikepacking bags have a series of clips or velcro straps to attach the rolltop bag to the handlebars and frame. These bags are secure enough to handle being bounced around off when when cycling.

Waterproof Material

All the bike handlebar bags in these gear reviews are waterproof. There are non waterproof handlebar bags out there but they are best avoided. When it comes to material being used in these bags they are not only waterproof but they are durable and resistant to the abrasion from the unfortunate falls that we all have when cycling.

Speed of Installation

Bike Handlebar bags are rather quick to install and remove. Bags that are specifically designed for bicycle touring such as the Vaude Aqua and Ortlieb Classic have quick release systems that allow the bags to be removed quickly for time off the bike.

Bikepacking Bags take a little longer to install and remove but once you get used to it the systems are quite quick. While each manufacturer has their own design and style they are all very similar in their use of straps and clips.

Handlebar bags for cycle touring are much quicker to install and remove than Bags for bikepacking.

Value for Money

Handlebar bags are not cheap and can really vary in price quite a lot. Some cost as little as $15, while others at the other end of the spectrum can cost over $100.

If you do have a budget you need to stick to, be sure to check prices as you go along. But like most things it is better to spend a couple of bucks extra for something that is going to last.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Handlebar Bags Affect Handling?

Unfortunately handlebar bags do affect handling, but you can prepare for this by ensuring that the contents of the bag are as well balanced as possible.

What Should I Pack In My Handlebar Bag?

A handlebar bag is a great place to stash small and frequently used items such as your map, snacks, your sunglasses and/or your smartphone.
