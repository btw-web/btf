Buyer’s Guide

Searching for the best lightweight trekking poles can be a challenge. There are so many different trekking poles to choose from and at such varying prices, that it can be difficult to distinguish between them.

It might be tempting to opt for the cheapest trekking poles that you come across. However, there are a few factors you will need to consider first.

We’ve put together a handy list of all the features you’ll need to consider when it comes to choosing the best lightweight trekking poles for your needs.

As long as your chosen trekking poles have these covered, then you can rest assured that you’ve found the product.

Take a look at everything you need to know about lightweight trekking poles below!

Carbon vs Aluminum

One factor to consider is what material you want your trekking poles to be made from. The main choices will be between carbon and aluminum.

Carbon fiber shafts are lightweight, and ideal for taking with you on hikes. These types of trekking poles will be lighter than the majority of aluminum poles. However, they might not be as durable as aluminum.

Carbon will tend to shatter rather than bend and break. And I know, I’ve had it happen on 3 separate occasions. Yes, not once, not twice but three time! It is an expensive accident when you slip and fall onto a Carbon Fibre pole and it breaks.

Another thing about carbon fibre poles, they have a great ability to absorb that shock of constant banging into the ground. Making them more comfortable than aluminum over time.

Aluminum poles are slightly weightier than carbon fiber poles, however, they are much more durable. They can deal with trickier terrains and support heavier weights. If you are unfortunate enough to take a fall and land on the poles they will tend to bend. If you are careful they can be bent back into shape. Well, most of the time anyway. And did I mention they are cheaper than carbon!

Handles

You should look for a set of trekking poles that come with comfortable handles. The main handle grip to think about is handles made from cork. These will be more comfortable the longer you use them as they mold to your grip.

They will also work to wick away moisture as you walk. Then there is the fact that they absorb some of the constant vibration that poles go through as they are used over thousands of miles of their lifetime.

It will be worth looking for handles that also feature an extended foam grip. These will be more comfortable to use over tricky terrain.

Straps

Trekking poles that come with a strap will be much easier to use. These work to give you a better grip of your trekking poles. It might be worth looking for straps that are able to wick away moisture, as this will make them more comfortable to use on long hikes.

The better straps should be easy to adjust along your wrist so that they’re more comfortable. Take a look at how easy the straps are to adjust so that you can get the right support.

Adjustable Height

Another great feature to look for is adjustable height. This will make the trekking poles easier to use across different terrain, as well as different parts of your hike. When walking downhill, you will need to extend the trekking poles to give you the proper support you need to prevent injury or unnecessary strain on your joints.

Trekking poles with adjustable heights will also be more versatile. You can easily adjust them to suit your personal height, so you can get the right support when you need it most.

As a general rule the height of the trekking poles should be set so that the angle of the elbow is 90 degrees when the hands are placed on the grip of the trekking pole. This is not a rule and some people enjoy the poles to be adjusted a little higher or lower.

You should also look at how to adjust the height of your trekking poles. Is this easy to do while you’re out hiking? Does it feature a twist lock design or a flip lock design?
