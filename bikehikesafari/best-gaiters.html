Best Gaiters – Overall

Outdoor Research Crocodile Gore-Tex Gaiters

Outdoor Research Crocodile Gore-Tex Gaiters

Pros:
> Waterproof

> They secure in two places
> Abrasion-resistant
> Usually true to size

Cons:
> Not as breathable as most people think

We’re going to kick this off with a classic. These gaiters, fondly known as crocs have been around forever and they still remain a mainstay to this day.

These award-winning gaiters are waterproof yet at the same time breathable, thanks to their Gore-tex fabric. They come up almost to your knees, providing excellent protection from abrasion caused by overgrowth on the trails. They secure to your person both at the top of the gaiter and also to your hiking boots as well.

It’s important to note at this point that you should size down one full size for a good fit over your hiking boots. And the strap is supposed to go over your socks rather than underneath your shoes.

Overall, these are one of the best hiking gaiters on the market in 2022.

Altra Trail Gaiters

Outdoor Research Helium Hiking Gaiters

Outdoor Research Helium Hiking Gaiters

Pros:
> Lightweight fabric.

> 100% waterproof.
> They offer excellent coverage.
> They are easy to pull on.
> They feel very secure on.
> Abrasion-resistant fabric.

Cons:
> Lightweight strap underneath the boots can get damaged with use

These gaiters are made with Outdoor Research’s ever-popular Helium fabric, which is well known for being lightweight and waterproof. These particular gaiters weigh a mere 4.9 ounces (that’s 139 grams).

They offer excellent coverage, almost reaching the knee. They are easy to pull on and fasten up, with their Hypalon instep strap that goes under your hiking shoes and with the drawcord cinch at the top hem.

Thanks to the hook and loop closure at the front and the elastic across the back heel, they feel really secure.

They have a 100% nylon ripstop 30 denier fabric in the upper leg panel, you don’t have to worry about getting scratched by bushes.

It’s available in a choice of 3 different color options and in a wide range of sizes, starting at small and going all the way through to extra, extra-large.

Overall, these are one of the best lightweight full length waterproof gaiters on the market. These are the perfect gaiter for early season thru hiking when there is still a lot of snow on the trail or for someone wanting to hike on formed trails with lots of mud but not much in the way of abrasive bushes.

Buyers Guide

Here are some things you might need to consider before purchasing gaiters.

Water Resistant Gaiters

The best waterproof gaiters are made with some form of waterproof and breathable membrane such as Gore Tex or eVent. Gaiters made with these materials are great for use in snow or muddy trails. For use in the desert to prevent sand and rocks from entering being waterproof is no as important.

Breathable Gaiters

The best breathable gaiters are ultralight and mostly used in the desert to prevent debris from getting into your shoes. And they do a great job without making your feet too hot.

Durability

The best durable gaiters are solid enough to withstand walking off trail and bush bashing through potentially rough terrain. The overall weight of the gaiters are usually a good indication of how durable they will be

Conclusion

There are some really great gaiters out there to choose from, and the main factor influencing your decision about which ones to get will probably be the height they reach. Knee-high waterproof gaiters are great for snow sports and deep muddy trails. But if you’re trail running or thru hiking you will be better off with spandex based gaiters that are pulled down to your ankles.
