What Makes A Good Backpacking Pillow?

You might think that all there is to consider when pillow shopping is whether it feels nice to lay your head-on. Sure, that’s fine when you’re using it inside the house, but things need to be a little more rigorous when it comes to backpacking gear.

You need to consider the following things in a camping pillow:

  • Comfort
  • Size and Weight
  • Durability
  • Ease of Maintenance
  • Simplicity

Comfort

If it doesn’t softly cradle your head, either using foam, air inflation or another method of padding, then it’s not meeting the basic criteria of being a decent pillow. You might have a preference for a particular texture or material.

Some of the comfortable pillows are covered in neoprene where others have a rougher external fabric – depending on how you feel about texture and whether your skin is sensitive, you might want to consider if you’ll be able to settle down on top of it before you buy.

There are a couple of different types of backpacking pillows and each are different when it comes to comfort.

The inflatable pillows have the advantage of allowing the head to sink into the pillow depending on how much air was inside.

Foam pillows are usually set and unable to be changed which can be a good thing for a comfortable pillow.

Stuff sack pillows can be adjusted by adding more clothing to the stuff sack to fill it up. It can be rather annoying to sleep on a stuff sack pillow when the jacket inside is full of zips.

Size and Weight 

If you’ve been backpacking before, you’ll be well aware of this already. Space is a vital commodity in your traveling bag, the majority of which you’re going to want to reserve for clothes, daily essentials, and other important belongings.

The smaller and flatter a pillow can be made, the better. Some are designed to roll up as small as a pair of socks inside their own carrying pack, so if you’re short of room it could be worth valuing size over everything else. Inflatable pillows are best compressible pillows for the weight.

Durability

Even if you’re not going very far, you want this pillow to survive being repeatedly shoved in and yanked out of your bag, rubbing up against all of your other stuff, possibly getting leaked on or scratched up – it could go through a lot!

Aiming for a material that’s able to stand up to more distress than the average pillow will experience, constructed with temperature changes, staining or spillages and other rough stuff in general is your best bet to make sure it lasts the whole trip.

Ease Of Maintenance

Accidents happen. When something sticky explodes inside the bag, or your nighttime drooling gets out of control, you’re going to want to give your pillow a thorough cleaning to get it back to its original comfortable condition.

This will be much easier and quicker for you if the pillow can be machine washed! It doesn’t matter if it’s dryer friendly as air-drying is cheaper anyway, but just being able to toss it into a washer when you get a chance is a useful convenience.

Simplicity

Nobody wants to spend ten minutes messing around assembling a pillow before they can go to sleep, especially if you’ve been out adventuring all day. Those products that need you to use all the air in your lungs are not a backpacker’s friend.

Prioritize picking up a pillow that will have you snoozing in seconds. You might spend a couple of extra bucks on it, but your incredibly tired backpacking self from the future will absolutely thank you for splashing that cash.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you use the Nemo Fillo pillow?

All you need to do is locate the red inflation valve system and ensure it is in the ‘open’ position, by twisting once to the left and then pulling upwards and add about 2 breaths.
Once it feels good, push down and twist the inflation valve again, returning it to the ‘locked’ position and sealing in all of that air. You can vary the air pressure for a harder or softer pillow.

How do you keep a pillow on a sleeping pad?

There are plenty of hacks to try, from buying a pillow with in-built loops or attachment points to being clever with some Velcro attached to the pillow and the sleeping pad. It’s pretty difficult to verbally spell those out, though.

How to deflate a backpacking pillow?

To deflate, simply open up the valve on the inflatable pillow and give it a squeeze to remove all of the air. Then place the backpacking pillow into either the supplied stuff sac or pack it inside with the sleeping bag.
