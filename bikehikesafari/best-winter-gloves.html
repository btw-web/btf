Buyers Guide

Choosing the right hiking gloves for winter can be a challenge. There are so many different models to choose from that it can often be difficult when trying to make your purchasing decision.

How can you know what the essential factors to consider are when choosing the best hiking gloves?

We’ve put together this helpful buyer’s guide to aid you in your search. Below we have collated all of our essential research criteria. As long as your chosen product has these features covered, you can rest assured that they will be perfect for your needs.

Let’s take a look at everything you need to know about hiking gloves, or skip ahead to the frequently asked questions.

Insulated

The most important feature to look for in a pair of hiking gloves, of course, is that they are sufficiently insulated.

When you’re out in extreme weather conditions in the mountains, you will need a pair of hiking gloves that can deliver on the warmth you need. There will be no point in bringing them with you if they don’t keep your hands warm!

Ensure that your preferred pair of hiking gloves come with a good amount of insulation. It will also be worth looking for a pair of gloves that come with removable liners.

This will allow you to adjust the amount of insulation you have in the gloves, to prevent them from getting too hot in milder weather.

You should take a look at how much insulation the gloves are able to give you. Do they feature multiple layers? This can give you added warmth when you need it most.

Waterproof

Another factor to consider is to choose a pair of backpacking gloves for winter that offer you great waterproof protection. The whole point of wearing backpacking gloves is to keep your hands warm while you’re out on the trail.

Gloves that don’t seal out moisture from the elements will just become soggy, exacerbating your problem, and leaving you with cold and wet hands.

Make sure that the gloves you choose are waterproof rather than water resistant. Gloves that are water resistant will start to allow moisture into them once they reach a certain saturation.

Waterproof gloves will lock out moisture so that you can keep your hands dry. This will be a lifesaver if you’re out in snowy environments.

Breathable

It will be crucial to look for a pair of winter gloves that are breathable as well as coming with sufficient insulation and waterproof features.

This is because if the gloves do their job too well, but don’t allow your hands to breathe, you will be left with sweaty hands while out hiking.

And nobody wants sweaty hands! Not only will this be unpleasant, but it will also make it much more difficult to use hiking equipment should you need to.

A pair of breathable gloves will allow your hands to stay warm and toasty, but without getting uncomfortably sweaty. This will be essential if you plan on being out on the trail for long periods of time.

It will also remove the need to expose yourself to the elements just to cool your hands down enough to stop them sweating.

Easy to fasten

You will of course want a pair of winter gloves that will be easy to fasten in place. Getting one glove on is never a hassle. It’s when you need to try and get the second glove on and are finding it hard to fasten in place that issues can arise.

So it will be crucial to think about the type of fastening that your chosen gloves come with. The easier they are to pull on and off as you need to, the better.

Choosing a set of gloves that feature easy pull on fasteners will be ideal. These will be super easy for you to use regardless of your environment.

Dexterity

Choosing a pair of winter gloves that gives you the dexterity you need to accurately use additional equipment such as axes will be crucial if you will be hiking through extreme environments.

That added dexterity could be the fine line between life and death, so you will need a pair of gloves that won’t let you down.

This will mostly come down to whether you want to opt for mittens or traditional style gloves. Winter Mittens will make it much harder to use things like axes.

However, some of the more traditional style gloves might not be able to offer you the best insulation. There is also a solo finger mitt style available for added dexterity. 

We would recommend taking a closer look at the products we’ve reviewed in more detail above to ensure that you get the dexterity you need.

Budget

It will also be important to consider how much budget you have to play with when it comes to choosing your next pair of hiking gloves.

It may be tempting to opt for the cheapest pair you can find, but there’s no guarantee that these will be able to deliver the warming results that you need. Similarly, some of the more expensive models may be outside your budget.

Before you make your final decision, it will be important to make a list of the essential features you need from your hiking gloves.

This should give you a better idea of which gloves you will need to aim for when it comes to saving up the money. It may be worth waiting a month or two so that you can save up enough to buy the higher end hiking gloves that come with all the essential features you need.

Size

To get the best fit for your hands, it will be worth considering which size gloves you need. It may be tempting to opt for a one-size-fits-all pair, but these might not be able to keep your hands as warm as a pair of gloves that comes in a more specialized size.

You should opt for a pair of gloves that fits more snugly on your hand, but still gives you plenty of room to use equipment such as axes.

It is also worth remembering that some sizes may differ in price, depending on the product that you choose.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose hiking gloves?

There are several things you will need to bear in mind when it comes to choosing the best hiking gloves for your needs.
These will include the amount of insulation included, how waterproof the gloves are, how breathable they are, how easy they are to put on and fasten, how much dexterity they give you, how much budget you have to play with, what sizes are available, as well as how durable they are.

Do you need waterproof gloves for hiking?

If you plan on hiking in extreme weather conditions where there will be lots of snow and perhaps even rain, then you will need a pair of waterproof gloves for hiking, yes. 
This is because soggy gloves won’t keep you warm, and will even make the issue worse. That cold water in the gloves could freeze if you get up to higher altitudes, which will just make your hands even colder. 
A pair of waterproof gloves will lock out any moisture, and keep your hands nice and toasty.
