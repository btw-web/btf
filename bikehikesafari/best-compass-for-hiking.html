Buyers Guide

There are a couple of things you may want to know prior to buying a compass. While I have not included a guide to using a compass (that is a rather large article that I am working on), the info below may assist in helping you make your purchasing decision.

Sighting Mirror on a Sighting Compass

Some of the compasses in this review have a sighting mirror to be used as a sighting compass. When you are trying to get a bearing or a back bearing you can hold the compass at arms length from the body and get a very accurate fix on your intended landmark. A sighting mirror aids in making accurate compass readings.

My first ever compass had a sighting mirror. As I got more experienced with using a paper map and compass I moved away from sighting mirrors and back to the lighter, more simple compass without a sighting mirror. Your choice if you want them or not.

Rotating Bezel

Not all the compasses in this review have a rotating bezel, but all the best ones do. The bezel is used to rotate to account for the difference between magnetic north and true north, known as the declination. I will not go into too much detail about what that is, or how to use the rotating bezel (as mentioned above, that will be the focus of a whole in-depth article). Just know this, if you want accurate readings you will need a rotating bezel.

Global Needle

Not all compasses are set up to be used anywhere in the world. If you want to take your compass anywhere you will need a compass with a global needle. This is often indicated with the latter ‘G’ somewhere in the description of the compass.

For compasses that are not global they are often only balanced for either the Northern Hemisphere or Southern Hemisphere.

Compasses balanced for the Northern Hemisphere usually have ‘NH’ in the description.

Compasses balanced to the Southern Hemisphere usullay have ‘SH’ in the description.

Compasses balanced for use anywhere in the world have a ‘G’ in the description.

Knowing this is possibly the number one most important thing you need to know when purchasing a compass.

Conclusion
