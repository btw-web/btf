Buyers Guide

Here is the buyers guide that may assist you with your choice of which biodegradable soap to take on your next camping trip or backpacking trip.

Scented or Unscented Biodegradable Soap

If backpacking in bear country, you will not only be carrying a Bear Proof Canister and Bear Spray, but you will want to opt for the unscented biodegradable soaps.

Scented Biodegradable Soap

Scented biodegradable soaps are are great to give you that fresh smell, or add a nice smell to otherwise gross smelling clothing. Some are known to assist in repelling insects. But I have found that this doesn’t work very well for me.

Scented wipes are great to use to freshen up in dry places such as the desert.

Unscented Biodegradable Soap

Unscented biodegradable soaps are the way to go when hiking, backpacking and camping in bear country. These freaky animals have a keen sense of smell and having a more neutral soap is better. Having said that, they will be able to smell the lack of smell anyway. If you have skin allergies, the unscented may be a little better on the skin.

Biodegradable Soap Vs Biodegradable Wipes

What the advantages and disadvantages of each and which one is better for you.

Biodegradable Soap

Typically, these biodegradable soaps are much more versatile and have plenty of uses. Alongside coming in conveniently small bottles or containers, that don’t take up much space on your own bag, or can fit inside a pocket.   They are very concentrated and only a very small amount is needed to be used.

Biodegradable Soaps are versatile and can usually be used for not only washing a dirty sweaty face, but can also be used anywhere on the body, to wash clothes, dishes and more.

Biodegradable Wipes

Biodegradable Wipes are great for wiping down your face, arms and body. Many people use them to clean up after a bathroom break on the trail. They can also be used to clean dirty dishes but that is about it.

While these Biodegradable Wipes do break down in the environment they should be either buried with your toilet poo or carried out with you .

Biodegradable Soap and the Environment

Although biodegradable soap is better for the environment, it can still harm the environment. You need to dispose of these soaps in the correct way as you don’t want to affect any water sources. Even though biodegradable soap is better, it can influence water quality.

Therefore, when disposing of any soap including biodegradable soap, dispose of the water at least 200 feet away from any water sources. As it is the soil that will biodegrade the soap, and it does not impact any aquatic life.

Biodegradable soaps are different from traditional soaps, as they don’t contain any man-made ingredients or chemicals. They are normally made from vegan or vegetable-based ingredients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I Really Need To Use Biodegradable Soap For Hiking?

Whether you are hiking, backpacking or camping, you want to try and have the smallest impact on the natural environment around you.

Therefore, one way of going about this is using little to no soap. If you are planning on a long hiking trip, then you may want to take soap with you, but the soap you are using should be biodegradable.

How Do I Know If My Soap Is Biodegradable?

It should say on your label if the soap is biodegradable. However, soap is considered to be biodegradable, if bacteria can break the soap down to at least 90% water and any organic matter in 6 months. 

Also, a biodegradable soap will contain all-natural ingredients and no synthetic chemicals. 

How Long Does Biodegradable Soap Last For?

Normally, biodegradable soap should last around 6 months before it starts to degrade.
