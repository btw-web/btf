Best Top Tube Bags

Buyers Guide

As promised, here’s your buying guide. It highlights some of the key things to think about before you buy, and makes for a handy checklist.

Aesthetic

This is the least important consideration in buying a Top Tube Bag but we can all get picky when it comes to colors and styles. Most top tube bags tend to come in either black or slate gray, designed to look like part of the bike itself.

Sometimes you can get other color options as well. Where this applies, I’ve mentioned that in our reviews.

Attachment

It is important that the bag stays securely attached to the bike as you ride. Top tube bags that have 3 attachment points tend to stay fixed to the bike better than those with only 2 attachment points.

Similarly, the top tube packs that have have adjustable points of attachment at the front are better. Generally, having it fixed at the front higher is better than lower but this is not always possible.

Capacity

The top tube is an excellent place to store electronics, keys, snacks, sunscreen, lip balm, wallet and keys. All the really essential stuff that you may need to get hold of quickly. But they are not designed to carry too much stuff. They are simply not solid enough for that purpose.

Some top tube bags can be particularly small, so be sure to check the size first, so you know you’ll safely be able to include all your essential items. One liter is the average size for these bags with anything bigger tending to lose its firm fit.

Easy Opening

Unlike with a pannier bag, with a top tube bag, ideally you shouldn’t have to fiddle around adjusting straps to get to your gear.

The best top tube bags are the ones with convenient closures that you can open and close one-handed, so that you can keep at least one hand on the handlebar at all times.

Value for Money

Top Tube bags can really vary in price. You can get some for as little as $15, while others can cost more than $70.
