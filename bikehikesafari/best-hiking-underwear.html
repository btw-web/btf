Buyers Guide

Now that you know the best products available on the market, how do you choose which one is best for you? 

The first thing to consider is the climate you will be hiking in. If you are heading to cold areas or up very tall summits with snowy peaks, you will want underwear that acts as a thermal layer.

The products that are made from a Merino wool blend are best for this. If you intend to be hiking in warmer, more humid climates then you might want a more lightweight option made from a blend of synthetic materials. 

The next thing to consider is whether you are hiking for the day only, or going on a camping trip or hiking as part of a longer travelling adventure.

You may want to choose underwear that is quick drying and lightweight as this is much easier on the go. You can wash the boxers, dry them quickly, and they won’t take up much space or weight in your luggage. 

You should also think about how you are likely to wash your backpacking underwear. If you go for a hike every weekend and want to chuck your backpacking gear in the washing machine with your other clothes on a Sunday evening, you will want to choose items that are machine washable and suitable for tumble dryers. 

I recommended choosing underwear with a comfort flatlock seam to prevent chafing and rubbing. If you are walking for hours at a time, even the slightest bit of friction can cause problems. This is why it is also important to choose tagless boxers if possible. 

One of the most important features of hiking underwear is how flexible they are. You want your clothing to move with you, not restrict you. Stretchy waistbands and flexible fabrics are the best choices. 

If you like your clothes to match, or you want to express yourself with different colors, then choose a product with lots of color choices. Some products only come in more functional colors like black and grey. 

You might also want to consider whether the product you choose is ethical. Does it conform to fairtrade standards? Is it made from recycled products? Is the material sustainable?  

By considering all of the above you should be able to find the best hiking underwear to suit your individual needs. I recommend the Smartwool Merino boxers as a good all rounder.

They are thermoregulating, moisture wicking, odor resistant, with a comfortable fit. They are easy to care for as they are machine washable and they are durable. You can’t really go wrong with this product. 

Frequently Asked Questions

What is so special about Merino Wool? 

Merino wool has excellent natural properties which make it perfect for use in the manufacturing of outdoor clothing. It is naturally odor resistant so won’t hold on to the smell of sweat.

It has thermoregulating abilities which means it will keep you cool in the summer and warm in the winter, making it an ideal base layer for hiking. It is very absorbent and will draw the moisture away from your skin.

It is also very soft unlike other wools that can be scratchy and itchy. All of these features make it perfect for hiking clothing including underwear. 

Is Merino Wool Ethical?

Merino wool that comes from New Zealand is nearly always ethical and sustainable as they have very good standards of animal welfare. This makes it more ethical than many other types of wool.

The Merino wool that comes from Australia is not always as ethical. If ethically sourced clothing is important to you then you may want to research the individual brand and where they source their Merino wool from. 

Are Natural fabrics better than synthetic ones? 

Natural fabrics have some excellent qualities, but they are usually harder to care for and less durable than synthetic fabrics. Synthetic materials have been designed and created from scratch, so can be built to last.

Natural fabrics are made from organic materials which will deteriorate over time. If you look after them well and follow the care instructions, you can still get a lot of wear out of them. We recommended choosing a product made from a natural and synthetic blended fabric to get the benefits of both types of material. 

Why is moisture wicking so important? 

When you do exercise like hiking, your body will produce a lot of sweat. You can often be out for hours at a time. If that sweat sits against your skin, you could develop a skin rash or even get a bacterial infection in your skin. If you stop moving and the sweat dries against your skin, you can quickly develop a chill.

Moisture wicking fabric draws the sweat away from your body and keeps it in the outer layers of the fabric, where it can evaporate. This keeps your skin dry and fresh, is more hygienic and prevents a lot of discomfort.
