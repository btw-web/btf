Best Day Packs

This is a gear review about the best daypacks for day hiking. To go hiking, you need the proper equipment. This ensures you are safe, prepared, and ready for anything you may encounter along the trail.

Part of your equipment list needs to include a sturdy daypack to keep all your necessities in and fit comfortably on your back so that you can reach it easily wherever you might find yourself. Buyers Guide

With so many factors, it can be hard to find the perfect day pack on your own. This buyer’s guide will guide you on what you should be keeping an eye out for whilst researching.

Weight

When you come to buying a day pack, you don’t want something that is going to weigh you down on the trail. Look at the weight of it and consider that weight on top of the load you will already be carrying.

If it’s too heavy for you, consider opting for a lighter backpack that might hold slightly less, your back and shoulders will thank you.

Some designs even have technology that transfers the weight to your hips so if you struggle to carry heavy loads, this might be something to keep an eye out for too.

Activity

Each pack will be designed for a different activity and a different length hike.

For shorter hikes, you want something lightweight and minimalist yet if you’re climbing to the summit or you like going on overnight hikes, something with more support and something slightly more durable built to last in harsher weather conditions could be the best option.

How long your hike is will also determine the size of your day pack. If you’re staying overnight, you’re going to need to pack a lot more than if you were going hiking for a few hours.

Moreover, an easy-to-access main compartment is useful along the trail for whichever activity you end up doing and so have a look at how you access this before buying any day pack.

Does it have two zippers? Does it have mesh storage pockets on the side? These are all things to find out before making that all-important purchase.

Fit

Just like clothes, day packs are designed for different-sized people. You may get an option to pick a size and this will be according to the buyer’s measurements, so check these first.

The measurements are usually referring to a buyer’s torso and the ranges vary from manufacturer and by gender. Hip belts also accommodate a range of waist sizes and so ensure you measure this too.

They usually range from mid-20 inches to around the mid-40s Finally, the load lifter straps and sternum strap should fit correctly on a day pack and form a 45-degree angle between the shoulder straps and the pack.

The sternum strap boosts stability and ensures you don’t fall over on rough terrain. Often it is removable and adjustable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Day Pack?

A daypack is a smaller version of a large backpack that is usually lightweight, but big enough for essential items to take on day hikes or sometimes overnight trips.

What Should I Pack In My Day Pack?

This will depend on what you are using the day pack for, but if it is for hiking, we recommend a spare change of clothes, water, snacks, lunch, sun protection, and a first aid kit as the bare essentials.

Do You Need A Day Pack For Hiking?

For everyday hiking a day pack is vital.  You want to find something that is small and lightweight, durable and sturdy, and designed to take on many different weather conditions.
