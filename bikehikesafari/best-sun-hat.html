Buyers Guide

Buying the right sun hat is important. The sun hat is going to protect you from the harsh UV rays from the sun and also do the job of keeping you cool.

You may think that you don’t need a sun hat, but as the sun comes out more and the temperature rises, you need to have a sun hat, especially while you are hiking.

This will stop you from getting sunburn and sunstroke and avoid any permanent skin damage that the sun could do to you. It is very easy to find out what kind of sun hat you require.

Depending on where you go hiking, you need to make sure that it offers you as much protection as possible. We aren’t just talking about your head. We are also referring to your face and more importantly your neck.

A good-sized brim should protect your face quite well. You need to think about your neck, as it often gets overlooked and that’s where more people tend to get burnt.

You may notice that every hat in this collection has a dark gray underside. Through our research, we discovered the reason for this is to absorb light and decrease any glare.

Alongside this, you want a hat that is made from very light materials. Which will then make wearing the hat more comfortable. Also, you want it to be breathable.

This way your head will remain cool while wearing the sun hat, thus creating a more enjoyable hiking experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Is The Best Colored Sun Hat To Have?

It may be surprising, but darker-colored sun hats are better than white or pastel-colored sun hats. This is because the darker colors absorb more of the UV rays, which then blocks the rays from touching your skin. But light or white colored hats reflect more sunlight which can make the hat cooler.

How Tight Should Your Sun Hat Be?

You want the hat to be snug on your head. You want the hat to be comfortable, yet quite secure on your head, so you don’t have to worry about it falling off your head if there is a breeze. The use of a strap can stop the hat blowing off your head when it is windy.

Conclusion

When it gets warm and the sun is out, that is the perfect time to go for a hike, but don’t forget your sun hat.

A good sun hat will protect you from those harsh UV rays, which will prevent you from sunburn and sunstroke.
