2022 Rocky Mountain Blizzard Carbon Announced

Introduced as an alloy fatbike way back in 2015, the Rocky Mountain Blizzard has been the company’s flagship “alt” bike for a while now. It’s received a few updates here and there, such as the 2017 remodel that we reviewed in 2017. For 2022, Rocky Mountain released a significant update in the form of a lightweight, carbon version. Read on for details on the 2022 Rocky Mountain Blizzard Carbon…

The funky red and green 2022 Rocky Mountain Blizzard Carbon features a “Smoothwall Carbon” frame with a 100mm BSA bottom bracket, zero stack tapered headtube, fully guided internal cable routing, dropper post routing for a 30.9mm post, 197mm rear dropouts, and a Rocky Mountain Carbon Fat fork. It also has plenty of mounts, with two three-pack cage mounts on the fork and two within the main triangle, plus a three pack under the top tube for a bolt-on bag or accessory. It also comes equipped with rear rack mounts for winter bikepacking pursuits.

The frame clears up to 27.5 × 4.5″ and 26 × 5.0″ tires and is suspension fork compatible for up to a 100mm fork with 27.5″ wheels or a 120mm fork with 26″ wheels. It was also given the longer/slacker geometry treatment for more fun and games in the snow.

The 2022 Rocky Mountain Blizzard Carbon comes in two build kits: the Blizzard Carbon 30 retails for $2,699 USD ($3,399 CAD) and the Blizzard 50 for $3,299 USD ($4,099 CAD). They both share the same frame and a few components (listed below), but the 50 gets an XT drivetrain, an SLX 12-speed cassette, 4-piston brakes, and a dropper post, whereas the cheaper 30 comes with a rigid post, Deore components, and 2-piston brakes.

  • Headset: FSA Orbit NO.57E
  • Stem: 40mm Rocky Mountain 35 AM, 5° Rise, 35mm Clamp
  • Handlebar: 780mm Rocky Mountain AM, 25mm Rise, 9° Backsweep, 5° Upsweep
  • Grips: Ergon GE10 EVO Lock On
  • Spokes: 2.0 Stainless
  • Rims: Sun Mulefüt 80, 32H, Tubeless Tape and Valves
  • Tires: Terrene Cake Eater Fast Rolling Tubeless Studdable 27.5 x 4.5
  • Saddle: Rocky Mountain 148 Cromo

Find all the details, plus the new 2022 alloy Blizzards over at Bikes.com.
