Fizik Releases the Terra Argo Gravel-specific Saddle

Gravel-specific is kind a silly term when associated with a lot of products, but it appears that Fizik put a lot of thought into their new Terra Argo gravel saddle. For one, they claim that its shorter design and wave profile puts riders in a more planted position, and allows riders to sit further forward without placing undue pressure on sensitive areas. Additionally, the waved profile offers support that helps riders hold their position while climbing. And, the Terra Argo has a shell made to offer a certain degree of flexibility and compliance to absorb vibrations.

The Terra Argo’s padding is also a little more cushy than others in their line. Its made from fizik’s Type-2 foam, which is slightly thicker around the ischial sit bones area. Fizik states that this supports a more upright riding posture and it slightly softer than what they use on their racing saddles.

Lastly, unlike many saddles with cutouts, the Terra Argo has an odd mud-guard covering up the opening, complete with a small drain hole to keep water from puddling.

The Fizik Terra Argo comes in two flavors, the X3 and the X5. The lighter 238g/245g X3 model features Fizik’s Kium rails and costs $129.99, and the 250g/257g X5 has S-Alloy rails and costs $89.99. Both are offered in 150mm and 160mm widths. Here are the specs on both sizes:

150MM

Length: 270 mm
Width: 150 mm
Height at 75mm width: 47 mm
Length from nose to 75mm width: 113 mm
Rail: 7×7 mm

160MM

Length: 270 mm
Width: 160 mm
Height at 75mm width: 47 mm
Length from nose to 75mm width: 113 mm
Rail: 7×7 mm

Find more info on the Fizik Terra Argo gravel saddle over at Fizik.com.
