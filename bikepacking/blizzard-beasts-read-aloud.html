Blizzard Beasts Read Aloud by Bjorn Olson

Originally published in Issue 02 of The Bikepacking Journal, Blizzard Beasts tells the story of Bjørn and his partner Kim being forced to take refuge from a snowstorm inside a tiny, isolated shelter cabin while pedaling through Alaska. Despite their remote location, they’re joined by multiple surprise visitors during the time they’re stuck in the cabin, all of whom try to make the best of their unfortunate situation together, grateful for the warmth and company inside the little structure. Now you can hear the story, read aloud by the author during his “Social Isolation Stories” series. This is the fourth episode. Find the other three below.

Episode 1: Cycling Toward Culture

This story is about the relationship between fat-bikes and dog mushing in Alaska.

Episode 2: White Gas For White Guys

This story is about a beautiful misadventure with fat bikes on the Arctic Slope of Alaska. Note from author: contains strong language.

Episode 3: There is no Tomorrow (Part 1 and 2)

There is no Tomorrow is about Bjørn’s Arctic fat-bike and packrafting trip from Point Hope to Utqiagvik. This was originally written for the Arctic Sounder.
