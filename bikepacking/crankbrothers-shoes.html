Crankbrothers Launches Mountain Bike Shoe Lineup

Best known for their mountain bike pedals, Crankbrothers just debuted its full lineup of mountain bike shoes, which they claim addresses several inconsistencies related to the pedal-to-shoe interface. The new range features proprietary rubber sole compounds, their Match System, which optimizes pedal engagement for both Crankbrothers and SPD, high-quality materials, and three different closure systems for each model. The Mallet is their clipless downhill / enduro shoe, the Mallet E is for enduro / trail with a more aggressive tread and stiffer sole, and the Stamp is their new versatile flat pedal shoe. Each model is offered in 17 different sizes, from EU 37 to 48 (US 5-14), including half sizes.

Check out a quick breakdown of each shoe below, including pricing and colour options for each model available.

Crankbrothers Mallet Shoe

The Crankbrothers Mallet shoe is their more downhill-focused shoe, featuring extended cleat channels for big descents and downhill-style riding. The cleat area is ramped for easier clip-in / out and is made of a mid-friction MC1 rubber compound for quick exits. It has a cushioned EVA midsole for shock absorption, perforated mesh panels for breathability, and TPU reinforced sections on the toe and heel for protection and abrasion resistance. The Mallet shoe comes with a Crankbrothers shim and cleat pre-installed.

Crankbrothers Stamp Shoe

The Stamp Shoe, named after the popular Crank flat pedal, is their standalone flat pedal shoe in the lineup. It features a high-friction rubber sole, a lug depth designed to absorb pedal pins for maximum grip, and directional angles lugs at the toe and heel for hiking. Just like the Mallet and Mallet E, the Stamp is reinforced with TPU is key areas, has hidden eyelets, and lace pockets when needed. The Stamp Shoe is priced a touch lower than the toe clipless models, with the highest-end Stamp BOA coming in at $179 USD.
