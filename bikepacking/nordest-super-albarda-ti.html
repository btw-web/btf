Nordest Super Albarda Ti

According to Nordest, the new Super Albarda Ti frame was conceived and designed for those looking for a lightweight and versatile gravel bike with the ability to get rowdy on technical singletrack. To achieve this, Nordest designed the Super Albarda frame with a longer reach, a 68° head tube angle, and an interestingly low 80mm bottom bracket drop.

The Nordest Super Albarda Ti frame is constructed from Ti3Al2.5V double butted titanium with a modern geometry designed by Pedro Jerónimo for incredible stability. It features 420mm stays that are compatible with tires up to 27.5 x 2.1″ or 700c x 50mm, and a tapered steerer for forks with a 400mm length and 52mm rake.

The Nordest Super Albarda Ti is available as a complete bike with a Shimano GRX800 group for 3,199€ (~$3,780 USD), as a frame only for 1,399€ (~$1,655 USD), or as a frame kit with a carbon fork and a Chris King Inset 7 headset for 1,754€ (~$2,075 USD).

The Nordest Super Albarda Ti frame can be purchased directly from the Nordest Cycles website and has a delivery time of 10 weeks. And, as with most Nordest Cycles models, it is also available in CrMo 4130. Learn more over at NordestCycles.com.
