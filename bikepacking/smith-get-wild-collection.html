Smith’s New Get Wild Collection is Loud

Smith just announced its new Get Wild collection featuring some very loud 1980’s inspired graphics. With tiger stripes and hot pink, the kit offers a funky alternative to the traditional high-vis green. The new line consists of several helmets, MTB goggles, Ruckus ChromaPop sunglasses, a full road cycling clothing kit, and a few accessories. The collection also includes a “Wild” version of Smith’s Session helmet, which I’ve been using for over two years now, and have found it offers great ventilation, coverage, and is quite comfortable for long days on the bike. Whether tiger stripes make it even better is for you to decide.

Here’s what Smith had to say about the new collection. “The door pops open on the DeLorean and the Flux Capacitor reads 1989, which means hair bands are cranking, rear-entry boots are all the rage and a kid from a small farm in Idaho just made his pilgrimage to Squaw Valley to claim his title as Hot Dog. The tiger stripes and archive logo take us back to a time where Smith’s passion was dedicated to catching first chair/ last call and designing righteous snow goggles. Now we’re able to channel that same energy into biking. So tighten up your fanny pack, turn it to eleven, and Get Wild!!!”

Learn more at SmithOptics.com.
