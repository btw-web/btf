Brendan Walsh Sets Record for Fastest North-South Crossing of the US by Bicycle

Words and photos by Brendan Walsh (@bicyclebrendan)

On May 7th, 2019, during a morning training ride to Walden Pond, I was struck by an oncoming car. The 50mph collision speed tossed me into the air and subsequently bounced me off the ground. I was transported to the nearest ER for a dozen tests: x-rays, cat scans, etc. I limped out of the emergency room with no broken bones, but a severe concussion, damage to my left meniscus and right ankle, and PTSD. Unfortunately, Gary Truman, the bike I was going to ride, was totaled. This has not stopped this mission, only fueled it more. Through support of everyone we gain the courage to move forward. This is only built into the mission’s true purpose, inspiring those who can’t!

On September 30th, 2019, I left on a journey down Atlantic Coast of the United States. From Madawaska, Maine, on the border of Canada to the southern most point Key West, Florida, I rode for 11 days, 9 hours, and 33 minutes, covering 2,288 miles and set a new Guinness World Record for the Fastest Crossing of America by Bicycle (North to South). My mission is to raise $10,000 for NAMI, The National Alliance on Mental Illness, with this adventure. I’ve lost multiple friends to the terrible disease called depression, and suffer from it myself, which helps me empathize with others. My hope is to inspire others to ride, run, swim, ski, and hike their ways to a clearer, happier life. Through endurance sports like cycling, I have found a better version of myself. You are not alone in life’s journey!
