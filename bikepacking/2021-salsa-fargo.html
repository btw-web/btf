2021 Salsa Fargo Gets “Green” Paint and Minor Revisions

Salsa just released its 2021 steel bike lineup. This includes Fargo, the original steel drop-bar 29er mountain bike. The Fargo is built for mixed-surface rides with a triple-butted steel frame, carbon fork, Boost spacing, and clearance for 29 x 3.0″ tires. The 2021 Salsa Fargo frame hasn’t changed. It still has the ability to run 27.5+ or 29+ wheels and tires via the rotating, singlespeed and Rohloff-fiendly Alternator dropouts. But they changed the color and made a couple minor build kit tweaks. Find the details below…

2021 Salsa Fargo Apex 1 Build

The only real change in the build kit from 2020 is that it gets a Cowchipper handlebar instead of the Woodchipper. Here’s the full Apex 1 model component list…

Frameset

  • Frame Fargo
  • Fork Firestarter 110
  • Color Red

Drivetrain

  • Rear Der SRAM Apex 1
  • Cassette SRAM PG 1130, 11-42t
  • Chain SRAM PC 1110
  • Crankset Stylo 6K DUB, 32t
  • Shifter SRAM Apex 1
  • Brakes & Rotors TRP Spyre-C, 160mm

Components

  • Headset Cane Creek 40
  • Stem Salsa Guide
  • Handlebar Salsa Cowchipper
  • Grips Salsa Gel Cork Bar Tape
  • Seatpost Salsa Guide
  • Saddle WTB Volt Comp

Wheels and Tires

  • Front Wheel WTB Serra Hubs 15 x 110mm, WTB ST i25 TCS 2.0, 32h
  • Rear Wheel WTB Serra Hubs 12 x 148mm, WTB ST i25 TCS 2.0, 32h
  • Tires Teravail Sparwood 29 x 2.2″ Durable, Tubeless Ready

The 2021 Salsa Fargo Apex 1, as specced above, will retail for $2399. Learn more over at SalsaCycles.com. Also, note that you can still get the black Fargo frameset for $1099 or the Ti version for $2999. Salsa also released new colors and slight build kit revisions for their other two steel bikes, the Vaya and Marrakesh. Check out SalsaCycles.com for more.
