Dawn to Dusk’s Cage Ecosystem: Bottle Tech

Looking for more ways to carry bottles on your bike? Or perhaps your cages aren’t doing a good job at keeping your water in place when bouncing down rough gravel roads? Dawn to Dusk’s new Cage Ecosystem is designed to address these issues via a lineup of location-specific bottle cages. Their location-specific cages take terrain slope and roughness into consideration and offer a range of grip forces that are tailored for different types of riding. The idea is that a bottle cage mounted under your downtube will experience different forces than a bottle on your seat tube, and deserves a different cage to keep the bottle in place.

See a breakdown of the various Cage Ecosystem components below, including key specs that differentiate them.

Dawk to Dusk Kaptive 8

  • Primary Feature: Semi Side Pull Access
  • Frame Location: Down Tube, Seat Tube
  • Grip Force: 8 pounds
  • Weight: 39g
  • Price: $64.95 USD
  • Manufacturer’s Details: DawnToDusk.bike

Dawk to Dusk Kaptive 10

  • Primary Feature: Straight Pull
  • Frame Location: Down Tube, Top Tube
  • Grip Force: 10 pounds
  • Weight: 39g
  • Price: $64.95 USD
  • Manufacturer’s Details: DawnToDusk.bike

Dawk to Dusk Kaptive 14

  • Primary Feature: High Grip Straight Pull
  • Frame Location: Under Down Tube
  • Grip Force: 14 pounds
  • Weight: 49g
  • Price: $69.95 USD
  • Manufacturer’s Details: DawnToDusk.bike

Dawk to Dusk Sideburn 8

  • Primary Feature: High Grip Side Access
  • Frame Location: Down Tube, Seat Tube
  • Grip Force: 8 pounds
  • Weight: 33g
  • Price: $59.95 USD
  • Manufacturer’s Details: DawnToDusk.bike

Dawk to Dusk Bear Hug Mount

The Bear Hug Mount uses a compliant base that’s designed to flex to fit virtually any bike’s tube shape. Two velcro straps hold the mount in place, providing a standard two-bolt bottle mount for your favourite cage. The mount weighs 59 grams and retails for $19.95 USD. Find more details here.

Dawn To Dusk’s website is full of additional photos and details on each component, so head over to DawnToDusk.bike to learn more.
