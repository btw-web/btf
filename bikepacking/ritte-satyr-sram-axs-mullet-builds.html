Ritte Satyr Gets a (SRAM Force AXS) Mullet

Ritte’s flagship steel gravel bike, the Satyr—which I recently reviewed—is sporting a fresh ‘do for summer. In partnership with SRAM, the company just announced limited-time pricing on two new complete builds featuring the new wide range Force AXS Mullet drivetrain, starting at $4,999.

For those unfamiliar, a “mullet” drivetrain simply refers to pairing road shifters with a mountain bike derailleur and cassette, allowing you to eke out as much range as possible on your drop-bar rig. Logan recently wrote a comprehensive guide to mullet drivetrains, in fact, which is well worth checking out. In the case of the Satyr, that means a SRAM Eagle AXS mountain bike derailleur and cassette paired with Force eTap AXS electronic road shifters.

Pre-orders for both the alloy and carbon configurations are open as of this morning and will close on Thursday, July 9th, just two weeks from now. Pricing is $4,999 for the alloy build (Hunt X-Wide Alloy Gravel wheels and an FSA Energy Alloy Cockpit) and $6,699 for the carbon build (ENVE G23 or 3.4AR wheels and a full ENVE Carbon Cockpit). And as with all of Ritte’s complete builds, riders can select their choice of handlebar width, stem length, crank length, tires, and gearing at no extra cost. Find both complete build kits below.
