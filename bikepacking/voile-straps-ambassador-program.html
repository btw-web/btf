Voile Straps Ambassador Program is Open

Packrafting photos by Voile Strap Ambassador Jan Zdansky (@janzdansky)

If there was ever an official bikepacking accessory, it’d surely be the Voile Strap. What started as an accessory for backcountry skiers has evolved into an infinitely useful strap for hauling gear, emergency repairs, and even ultra-lightweight bark camp shoes. The straps are made in Utah from tough, semi-elastic polyurethane, and we never leave home without ours.

Voile just opened up applications for their Ambassador Program, and they are on the hunt for outdoor enthusiasts who are passionate about using their straps to help spread the stoke. They make it clear that being an influencer or professional athlete isn’t a prerequisite. Rather, they’re looking for people who are excited about using their straps and are willing to share stories, photos, or videos of them in use.

Although Voile won’t be offering paid positions, they’re trading gear stipends for content, in the form of $250 to $400 in credit that can be used towards any Voile gear. The credit amount is based on what kind of content is created, and how many photos/words are produced.

Head over to VoileStraps.com to learn more and apply.
