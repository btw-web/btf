The Rig That Won the 2021 Colorado Trail Race (Video)

The Colorado Trail Race is a grueling ride across one of the most challenging rideable trails in the US. Course-record holder Neil Beltchenko took his fifth ride on the Colorado Trail this year and finished first out of a field of over 70 other riders. In our latest YouTube video, Neil walks through the bike and gear he used to make it happen. Watch below, then scroll down to see the full build kit, his gear list, and more about his Colorado Trail history.

If you haven’t already, please subscribe to our YouTube Channel to ensure we can keep making videos like this one.

Neil’s Colorado Trail History

2012 – Tour with a friend in 10 days, first bikepacking trip
2013 – 4d 21h 30m – first race
2014 – 4d 09h 55m – first win
2016 – 3d 19h 50m – Started with the group and broke my shifter; pedaled 25 miles before going back to get my bike fixed at a bike shop. Left 24 hours after the start for an ITT – I set the overall course record that year.
2021 – 4d 03h 07m

Salsa Spearfish Build Kit

Here’s the full component list and bike build used on in the race this year.

  • Frame: 2020 Salsa Spearfish -Medium, I’m 5’ 9.5”
  • Shock: 100mm Fox Float DPS Performance Elite
  • Fork: 120mm Fox Factory 34 Stepcast
  • Wheels: Industry Nine Trail280 Carbon, 24h
  • Front Tire: 2.4” Maxxis Recon
  • Rear Tire: 2.35” Ardent
  • Derailleur: Shimano SLX 12-speed
  • Cassette: XT 10-51T
  • Chain: XTR
  • Crankset: 170mm Raceface Next R, 30t Raceface chainring
  • Brakes: XT levers and 4 piston Calipers
  • Seatpost: Trans-X dropper with Ergon SM mens saddle
  • Handlebar: Salsa Guide 35.0
  • Grips: Ergon GA3 Grips + bar tape on top
  • On The Bar: Fenix BC21R 2.0 light, Etrex 32x Spurcycle Bell
