The Overland Launches Refurbished Store and Trade-In Program

A few months after launching their Trade-In program, England’s The Overland is now offering refurbished products on their website at discounted prices. Overland ReStored is run in partnership with Circular Inc., which quality checks, repairs and, if need be, washes (using Nikwax products) the clothing to prepare them for a second life. Refurbished secondhand clothing extends a garment’s life by about two years, cutting its combined carbon, waste, and water footprint by 82% according to a report by ThredUp. Despite only launching a few days ago, the ReStored Collection has almost sold out. We have been told more products will be added over the forthcoming weeks.

Want to trade in your old clothing? The Overland will give up to 50% of the value of your old Overland clothing when you trade it in via their website. The value will be issued as a discount code against future purchases, including the recently launched ReStored range.

The Overland’s ReStored range is the latest among a growing number of brands that are offering similar programs. Apidura has their Revive program, PNW Components has their PNW Cycled dropper posts and accessories, and lots of our favourite bag makers offer repairs as well. Shop the ReStored range here.
