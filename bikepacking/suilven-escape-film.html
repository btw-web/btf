Suilven Escape (film)

Alastair Humphreys is no stranger to using folding bikes for far-flung adventure. For this particular outing, he took the new Brompton Explore into Scotland’s remote Inverpolly National Nature Reserve to find a mountain he’d only dreamt about in maps and poetry. The summit of Suilven had been tempting him to the Scottish Highlands for a while. So alongside filmmaker Tem, he headed off on this multi-modal trip to create this video.

The Brompton Explore

The bikes used in this film were released just a week or so ago. The new Explore model is undoubtedly a Brompton, but it has a few specific kit choices to make it a little more “road less travelled” friendly, according to Brompton. The Explore uses a 3-speed internal gear hub with two sprockets, giving it a 6-speed gearing. It also comes with 16 x 1.35″ Schwalbe Marathon tires, a complete repair kit, and a 28-liter roll-top bag with custom camo fabric. Here are a few pics and details, for those interested…

  • Edition Brompton Explore
  • Frame Material Steel
  • Handlebar type M type (1015mm)
  • Gears 6 speed
  • Gear Ratio Reduced (-12%) 6 speed with 44T chainring
  • Color Forest Green with Explore Orange highlight
  • Saddle Brooks Cambium C17 All Weather Saddle
  • Seatpost Standard (inside leg up to 33 inches)
  • Tires Marathon Racer Tanwall folding tyres
  • Grips Gum rubber grips – softer compound for increased comfort
  • Folded dimensions 565 (H) x 585 (W) x 270mm (D) (22.2 x 23 x 10.6″)
  • Bike Weight (approx) 11.88kg (26.2 pounds)
  • Front bag Explore Edition Borough Rolltop 28L bag

You can learn more about the Brompton Explore here. And, thanks to Al Humphreys for sending along these photos and stills.
