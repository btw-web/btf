Salsa Releases New EXP Framepacks for Hardtails and Fat Bikes

A couple of years ago Salsa introduced their EXP line of bikepakcing bags. Included in the lineup was the Cutthroat EXP Framepack. They also teased making others available for their Woodsmoke, Fargo, Timberjack, Mukluk, and Blackborow. And here they are, all available now for $149. Like the Cutthroat model which Gin used on our Cuba trip, the EXP Series HT and Fat Framepacks are sewn from a durable and highly water resistant fabric with welded-in zippers.

Salsa EXP Series HT (Hardtail) Framepacks

The EXP Series HT are designed to fit Salsa’s range of bikepacking hardtails perfectly. These include the Timberjack, Fargo and Woodsmoke. Materials include 500D nylon with TPU lamination and PU coating, 1000D polyester with dual-sided TPU lamination, #10 weather-resistant YKK Zippers, and Duraflex hardware.

  • Weather resistant with fully welded-in #10 YKK weather-resistant zippers
  • Internal hook-and-loop divider for separating gear and managing storage
  • Exit port along toptube for use with water bladder or lights
  • Non-driveside flat pocket for storage of maps, tools, phones, wallet, etc.
  • More on the Hard Tail Framepacks at SalsaCycles.com

Salsa EXP Series FAT Framepacks

The FAT Framepacks feature wider dimensions for bulky cold-weather clothing. In addition, similar to the Revelate Mukluk specific frame bag, they have a tapered front for added volume.

  • Weather resistant with fully welded-in #10 YKK weather-resistant zippers
  • Internal hook-and-loop divider for separating gear and managing storage
  • Exit port along toptube for use with water bladder or lights
  • Non-driveside flat pocket for storage of maps, tools, phones, wallet, etc.
  • More on the FAT Framepacks at SalsaCycles.com
