REI’s New Co-op Cycles DRT 4.1 Fat Bike

REI’s house brand, Co-op Cycles, just released their first fat bike and at first glance it looks pretty promising. It’s centred around an aluminum frame and fork, Innova 26 x 4.8″ tires, and a SRAM SX Eagle 12-speed drivetrain. As a more affordable option, it’s nice to see front and rear thru-axles and Shimano Acera MT-400 hydraulic disc brakes—leaving little to be desired considering it’s price tag. What caught our attention was its massive main triangle, which is begging for a frame bag, as well as the triple pack mounts on the fork legs.

The Co-op Cycles DRT 4.1 Fat Bike is offered in three sizes (X-Small, Medium, and Large), and the medium weighs 39 pounds. The frame is only available in the colourway pictured, which REI is calling Terra. Here’s the full build kit:

Co-op Cycles DRT 4.1 Fat Bike Build Kit

  • Frame Co-op Cycles 6061 aluminum
  • Fork 6061 aluminum
  • Crankset SRAM X1 1000 Eagle DUB fat 5
  • Bottom Bracket SRAM DUB English BSA 100mm fat
  • Shifter SRAM SX Eagle 12-speed
  • Rear Derailleur SRAM SX Eagle 12-speed
  • Cassette SRAM Eagle, 11-50t, 12-speed
  • Chain SRAM SX Eagle
  • Brakes Shimano Acera MT-400 hydraulic disc
  • Rims 26″ aluminum
  • Front Hub15mm thru-axle
  • Rear Hub 12mm thru-axle
  • Tires Innova 26 x 4.8″, 60 TPI
  • Handlebar Co-op Cycles alloy butted aluminum riser
  • Stem Co-op Cycles 6061 aluminum
  • Seatpost Co-op Cycles 6061 aluminum
  • Saddle WTB Pure Sport
  • Headset Internal headset

The Co-op Cycles DRT 4.1 Fat Bike is available now through REI.com for $1,299 USD.
