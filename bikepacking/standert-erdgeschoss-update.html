Standert Announces Updated Erdgeschoss

Building on the success of the previous edition, the latest Standert Bicycles Erdgeschoss is claimed to be packed with more features and better suited to the demands of bikepacking. The Erdgeschoss is a stainless steel gravel bike that comes stock with Standert’s own carbon adventure fork. The frame and fork are kitted out with loads of mounting options, including top tube, down tube, and fork legs mounts, plus fender and rear rack compatibility. The frames, which are made in Taiwan, are handpainted with heavy-duty wet paint for a long-lasting, vibrant colour and finished with a glossy clear coat over the entire frame. The Erdgeschoss has a clearance for 650b x 47mm / 700 x 40mm tires.

Standert Carbon Adventure Fork

The new Standert carbon adventure fork is included with both the framesets and complete builds, and provides the same clearance as the frame and a full-carbon construction. The fork has fender eyelets, triple pack mounts, flat mount disc brakes (up to 180mm rotor), internal cable routing, a 12 x 100mm thru-axle, and a total weight of just 450g. We’ve confirmed with Standert that the fork is not available to purchase separately from the frame.

Standert Bicycles Erdgeschoss Complete Builds

The Erdgeschoss is offered in two complete builds: an SRAM AXS eTap build and SRAM Force 1x build, and prices start at €3,749 (~$4,458 USD). Framesets are also available for €1,799 (~$2,138 USD). Find a complete parts list and photos for both build kits, followed by the geometry chart, below.
