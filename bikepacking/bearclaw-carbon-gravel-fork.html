Bearclaw Announces The Ramhorn Carbon Gravel+ Fork

Bearclaw Bicycle Co just announced the Ramhorn Gravel+ fork designed for big-tire 650B bikes, drop-bar 29ers, and “high speed exploration,” according to the Michigan-based company. Adding to that, “it’s built to withstand extreme bikepacking adventures,” which seems appropriate considering it can fit wide trail 29er tires or monster 27.5″ semi-slicks. “We designed it to be 35% stronger than test standards require. Most importantly, it maintains a 405mm axle-to-crown length while clearing 27.5 x 2.8 and 29 x 2.4 tires. It also has plenty of clearance for low knob 27.5 x 3 and 29 x 2.6 tires.”

We have a review of Bearclaw’s Thunderhawk in the works; our test model had a Ramhorn on it which stood up to Miles’ long and rugged test, including the Julian Bikepack Challenge. Find the full specs and photos below.

  • STEERER TYPE 1 1/2″ Taper
  • STEERER LENGTH 300mm
  • FORK LENGTH 405mm
  • MATERIAL Carbon
  • RAKE 47mm
  • AXLE 15mm x 100mm Thru Axle
  • GROWN RACE 40mm
  • BRAKE TYPE Flat or Post (post adaptor included)
  • MAX ROTOR 180mm
  • CAGE MOUNTS Triple Boss x 2
  • MAX TIRE 29 x 2.4-2.6″ / 27.5 x 2.8-3.0″
  • WEIGHT 600g

Find this fork in our full Gear Index of utility forks with bottle cage mounts here. The fork will be available for $500 between now and Saturday at BearclawBicycleCo.com.
