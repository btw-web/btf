Zpacks Plex Solo Tent is Their Lightest Yet

Weighing in at just 13.9 ounces (395 grams), the new Zpacks Plex Solo Tent is made in the USA from Dyneema Composite Fabric and is their only sub-14-ounce enclosed shelter. The Plex Solo sets up with 6-10 stakes and a single trekking pole adjusted to around 52″ (132cm), and a replacement tent pole can also be used for us bikepackers. Like the other Zpacks tents I’ve tested, the two storm flaps can be opened or closed independently with a single-wall canopy that overhangs the bathtub floor. The Plex Solo has sewn-in 8″ (20cm) tall bathtub floor that measures 7.5 feet (2.3 meters) long and 38″ (97cm) wide at the center and tapers to 28″ (71cm) at the head and foot. This tent rolls up to about 5″ x 12″ (15 x 30cm) and comes with its own loose-fit stuff sack. Find the full specs from Zpacks, photos, and a video below…

Packed Dimensions

6″ diameter by 12″ tall (15 cm x 30.5 cm) / 340 cubic inches (5.6L)

Exterior Dimensions

Peak height: 52″ (132 cm)
Center width including vestibule: 62″ (157 cm)
End width: 36″ (91 cm)
Front vestibule space: 18.5″ depth (47 cm)
Length: 100″ (254 cm)

Interior Dimensions

Peak height: 52″ (132 cm)
Center floor width: 38″ (97 cm)
End floor width: 28″ (71 cm)
Floor length: 7.5 feet (2.3 meters)
Zipper entry height: 36″ (91 cm)

The Zpacks Plex Solo Tent comes in six color options for $599, and all Zpacks gear has a two-year limited warranty against defects in materials or workmanship. Learn more over at Zpacks.com.
