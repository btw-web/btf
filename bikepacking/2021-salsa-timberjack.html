2021 Salsa Timberjack Gets New Geometry and 9 Builds

The Salsa Timberjack made our list of top 5 bikepacking bikes for 2020, and for good reason. It’s well specced, reasonably priced, and has a balanced geometry that’s perfect for bikepacking and trail riding. The Timberjack is a value-packed aluminum hardtail built up around a 130mm travel fork, modern components and specs, and is one of the safest bets for anyone looking for a versatile mountain bike for bikepacking.

Although I dealt out a lot of praise in my review last year, there’s always room for improvement, and Salsa appears to have addressed some of the major geometry related critiques I had. The biggest change is a slacker head tube angle, from 67.6° to 66.35°, and a steeper seat tube, from 73.2° to 75.1°. These changes should result in a more efficient pedalling position and a slacker front end that will help on steep, technical descents. The chainstay length and bottom bracket height remain unchanged, while the effective top tube length and wheelbase are also relatively similar. Otherwise, the only other changes are the addition of top tube bosses, down tube three pack mounts, Salsa’s Alternator 2.0 dropouts, revised cable routing, and some integrated chainstay protection. The frame is also now designed to handle up to a 150mm travel fork, for those exploring bigger, more challenging terrain.

The 2021 Salsa Timberjack is offered in eight complete builds, from $1,699 to $2,499 USD—depending on what level of components are desired. Within each build is the option to choose between 29 x 2.6″ or 27.5 x 2.8″ tires, and new for 2021 is a singlespeed Timberjack at the lowest price point. Salsa will continue to offer the Timberjack as a frame-only for $599 USD as well as a titanium frame-only for $2,699 USD, though the latter didn’t receive a geometry update. Find each model, build highlights, pricing, and photos below.
