Grass up the Middle Releases Free Digital Zine

Originally intended for print, our friends of Grass up the Middle Cycling Club just made a digital version of their first zine, and it’s freely available to the world. Created by Steve Makin, Paul Rance, and Paul Davy, the zine documents stories and impressions of the club members using bikes to fuel friendships and adventures on their relaxed country lane rambles around the United Kingdom.

For those who aren’t familiar with Grass up the Middle, they describe themselves as “a group green laners, rough stuffers and folk of a relaxed persuasion based in Manchester.” Following in the tire tracks of the Rough-Stuff Fellowship, the club is about “friendship, bikes, and taking life as easy and as unseriously as possible.” Building on their regular blog and Instagram feed, the first issue of the publication paints a lovely picture of their wholesome way of cycling, and we hope to see the zine make the leap to print in the future. Thanks for making your zine available, Grass up the Middle!

Leaf through the digital zine at GrassUpTheMiddle.cc and show them some love on Instagram @grassupthemiddlecc.
