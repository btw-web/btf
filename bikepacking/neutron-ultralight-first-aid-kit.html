Neutron Components’ Ultralight First Aid Kit

If you’re the type of person who leaves home on rides or trips without any sort of first aid kit, Neutron Components’ new lightweight kit might be for you. New Zealand-based Neutron Components, makers of the Oh Sh!t Kit (O.S.K.) bar-end tool kit, just released an ultralight, super portable first aid kit for cyclists, and it looks like it could make a good option for bikepacking.

The Ultralight First Aid Kit was developed with the help of MTB paramedics to offer all of the essentials, packed into a neoprene pouch that’s about the size of a wallet. What makes the kit unique is its elasticized strap that allows the kit to be strapped directly onto your bike. They’ve also included a self-adhesive hook and loop strap for even more flexibility and security.

Here’s what’s inside:

  • 1x small dressing
  • 1x medium dressing
  • 2m duct tape
  • 2x alcohol swabs
  • 1x CPR barrier shield
  • 2x plasters
  • 5x 3M steri-strips
  • 2x nitrile gloves
  • 1x rip-proof quick-reference guide
  • 1x neoprene pouch
  • 1x hook & loop elasticated mount strap
  • 1x self-adhesive hook & loop mount pad

The kit’s dimensions are 100x70x20mm and it weighs just 55 grams. It’s offered in a red or black neoprene pouch and costs $35 NZD (~$25 USD). Head to NeutronComponents.com to get yours.
