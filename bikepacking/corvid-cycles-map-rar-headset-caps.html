Corvid Cycles Donates MAP for SJ Brooks + RAR Headset Caps

Colorado-based Corvid Cycles just announced that they’ll be donating a complete MAP to RAR (Radical Adventure Riders) for the SJ Brooks Scholarship find. The fund was founded in 2017 by the RAR to “provide financial, gear, and bicycle support for BIPOC cyclists who are trans, non-binary, genderqueer, two-spirit, femme, or women.” Find the press release below and learn how you can make a donation and get a custom RAR stem cap.

By Chad Corbin (Corvid Cycles)

I am very excited to announce that I am donating this complete MAP to the SJ Brooks Scholarship Fund. The fund was founded in 2017 by the Radical Adventure Riders to “provide financial, gear, and bicycle support for BIPOC cyclists who are trans, non-binary, genderqueer, two-spirit, femme, or women.” This donation is part of my ongoing commitment to support diversity, equity and inclusion in the cycling community. This donation is part of my ongoing commitment to support diversity, equity and inclusion in the cycling community.

In addition, I’ve teamed-up with RAR and Dispatch Custom Cycling Components to raise additional funds through the sale of RAR stem caps. Profits from each stem cap sale will be donated to RAR to help fund the scholarship and RAR’s efforts. If you are interested in supporting RAR’s mission, please visit the Headsetcap.bike store to make your purchase and donation.

A donation of this size is significant for me, and I couldn’t have done it without the support of my industry friends. A huge thank you goes to JPaks for donating the complete set of packs and Astral Cycling for donating the rims. Special acknowledgement goes to Whisky Parts (fork, bar, stem, post), White Industries (headset, BB, crank, ring, hubs), Shimano (drivetrain, controls), Panaracer (tires), and Paragon Machine Works (frame components) for reduced NAHBS pricing, the show for which this bike was originally designed and built.

Learn more about the Corvid Cycles MAP here, and find more photos of the MAP over at CorvidCycles.com.
