Salsa Debuts 2019 All-Road and Hardtail Models

Following a fat bike release back in August, Salsa just announced full details on the model lines for eight of its bikes. Here is each with a few details, photos, and links.

2019 Salsa JOURNEYMAN

Timberjack NX Eagle 27.5+ (orange/$1,899), Timberjack SLX 27.5+ (olive green or pink/$1,499), Timberjack Deore 27.5+ (red or dark teal/$1,199)

Current models that were introduced in March 2018 continue, but Salsa adds a new $1399 Apex 1 drop bar build for both the 700c and 650b wheel/tire size. Learn more here

2019 Salsa VAYA

Steel All Road Bike

Vaya receives a big upgrade with the addition of the new Waxwing carbon fork. The Waxwing fork features side-mounted Three-Pack mounts, low rider mounts, and internal dynamo wire routing. The Vaya is available as a complete with a Shimano 105 drivetrain and 37mm WTB Riddler tires for $1,799 (in two color ways), or as a frameset in black for $999. Learn more here

2019 Salsa MARRAKESH

Full-On World Touring Bike

Marrakesh has all the bits necessary for a long bike tour: a rear rack, kickstand plate, spare spoke holder, and a heavy-duty frameset designed to ride well when loaded down with panniers. The 2019 Marrakesh is available in two colors as a complete for $1,499. Both feature a rear rack, Shimano Deore 3×9 drivetrain, Maxxis Roamer 700 x 42mm tires, and Cowchipper handlebars. It’s also available as a frameset in black for $799. Learn more here.

2019 Salsa FARGO

Classic Drop Bar 29er

Fargo goes wide trail with 29 x 2.6” tires. As I mentioned in my Timberjack Ti build, I’ve been especially impressed with this tire size for both trail riding and dirt road touring. It offers a great blend of rollover, traction, and bump absorption, all the while maintaining a manageable size for speed and handling. The 2019 Fargo also gets the new Firestarter 110 fork, which now has boost spacing, dual sets of Three-Pack mounts on each fork leg, low rider mounts, and internal dynamo hub wire routing. The 2019 Salsa Fargo is available with an Apex drivetrain for $2,499 (dark green) or a Tiagra 2x for $1,999 (orange or blue). Both feature Woodchipper handlebars and Maxxis Ikon 2.6″ tires. Learn more here

2019 Salsa CUTTHROAT

Tour Divide Race Bike

The 2019 Cutthroat gets an all new build kit making four complete models. Salsa claims the new front-suspension version was inspired by engineer Sean Mailen’s excellent Cutthroat ride in the 2017 Trans North Georgia. The 2019 Salsa Cutthroat Apex 1 SUS gets Maxxis Ikon 2.2 tires, Cowchipper handlebars, and a RockShox Reba RL 100mm fork for $2,899. Other models include the teal/sunrise fade Cutthroat Force 1 for $4,499, the red/blue Rival 1 for $3,499, and the black Apex 1 model for $2,699. It’s also available as a frameset in black for $1,999. Learn more here

2019 Salsa TIMBERJACK

Aluminum Hardtail Trail Bike

The Timberjack hardtail now comes in multiple model levels and colors, in both 29er and 27.5+ wheel sizes, all of which now feature dropper seatposts: Timberjack NX Eagle 27.5+ (orange/$1,899), Timberjack SLX 27.5+ (olive green or pink/$1,499), Timberjack Deore 27.5+ (red or dark teal/$1,199), Timberjack NX Eagle 29 (silver/$1,899), Timberjack SLX 29 (orange/$1,499), Timberjack Deore 29 (black/$1,199). It’s also available as a black frame for $449. Learn more here

2019 Salsa TIMBERJACK KIDS

For the youngsters

One year after Salsa’s entry into Kids bikes, the Timberjack 20 and 24 with rigid forks return ($559/549). In addition, Salsa added Timberjack 20 SUS and Timberjack 24 SUS to the lineup ($599/589), both sporting front suspension forks for little shredders everywhere. Learn more here

Also, check out the separate release for the new Warbird, now available in 650B. Availability within each bike platform varies by model level, but everything introduced here will be available Fall 2018.
