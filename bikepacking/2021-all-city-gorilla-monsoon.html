2021 All-City Gorilla Monsoon Comes in Two Color Fades

The most visible change is that the 2021 All-City Gorilla Monsoon now comes in two colors, with two different build kits. However, the 2021 model also gets a few tweaks under the hood, such as a lowered bottom bracket, which All-City claims will help support the load . According to the geometry chart, they changed it by 9mm (from 71.5 to 62.5mm). I didn’t have any complaints about the former geometry, so I’ll be curious how much this changes the ride. They also redesigned the dropouts and fork ends to accommodate 12mm thru-axles (the former version had a 15mm front axle) and flat-mount disc brakes. Both come in All-City’s proprietary 612 Select double-butted 4130 Chromoly steel tubing and have a lugged bi-plane fork crown, pantographed bottom bracket, and custom-designed Shot Collar.

Like past models, the 2021 All-City Gorilla Monsoon is built around a 27.5 x 2.4” tires and has clearance for 700c x 42mm rubber as well. The 2021 model is available in seven frame sizes from 43cm to 61cm, the 43cm size being a new addition to the range this year. Both complete models as well as the framesets will be available at your local All-City (QBP) dealer sometime in late September. Find more info over at AllCityCycles.com.
