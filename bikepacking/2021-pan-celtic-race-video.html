2021 Pan Celtic Race Video

Photos by Rupert Hartley (@ruperthartleyphoto) and Dan King (@BreakawayDigital)

On July 4th, 2021, after a postponed 2020 race, riders from all over the UK set off from Maker Heights in Cornwall to embark on a much-needed and long-awaited adventure through Celtic nations and parts of South West England steeped in Celtic tradition. The typically brutal weather paired with a challenging route tested even the strongest wills, as both mental strength and physical endurance were pushed to the limit. There were 130 riders spread across two route options at the grand depart, 49 of whom finished the long route. Among the fastest were Angus Young in first place with a time of 5 days, 3 hours, 36 minutes, and Jenny Graham, the fastest female, at 7 days, 4 hours, 5 minutes.
