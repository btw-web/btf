Curve Dirt Hoops Now Available in Alloy

Modeled after the carbon Dirt Hoops, which are Curve’s strongest carbon off-road wheels, the new Dirt Hoops Alloy take all the best features and roll them into a more affordable package. They’re designed to be versatile wheels, perfect for day rides on local singletrack or massive bikepacking trips in far off places. They feature a 30mm internal width, which can be set up with tires from 2.2″ to 3.0″ wide, and they ship with tubeless tape and valves.

The Curve Dirt Hoops Alloy are offered in both 27.5″ and 29″ diameters, built up around DT Swiss 350 hubs for their performance, durability, and serviceability. Looking to charge some gadgets or power a light while you’re riding? Curve also offers an optional dynamo front hub build, featuring the popular SP Dynamo PD-8X hub. Both options are based around boost hub spacing, including a 15mm front axle and 12mm rear.

Digging into the specs (below), it’s worth noting that didn’t carry over the straight-pull spokes from the original Dirt Hoops, which was a selling point. So, we think the pricing seems a bit high, but it’s nice to see another relatively lightweight out of the box wheel option with aptly specced rims and Dynamo hub.

Curve Dirt Hoops Alloy Specs

  • Rim Profile: 30 internal x 35 external x 19 deep
  • Spokes: Sapim Race
  • Rim Weight 29″: 569g
  • Rim Weight 27.5″: 465g
  • Wheel Weight 29″ (Standard/Dyno): 2200g / 2430g
  • Wheel Weight 27.5″ (Standard/Dyno): 1990g / 2220g
  • Hubs (standard build): DT 350 SP CL 28H Front and Rear
  • Hubs (Dyno build): SP Dynamo PD-8X 6 Bolt 28H Front, DT 350 J-Bend 6-Bolt Rear
  • Hub Spacing: 15 x 110mm front / 12 x 148mm rear
  • Standard Price: $1,179 AUS (~$915 USD)
  • Dynamo Price: $1,229 AUS (~$954 USD)
  • Manufacturer’s Details: CurveCycling.com

Curve’s website says the Dirt Hoops Alloy are available as rim only for custom builds, although we aren’t sure of pricing just yet. They recommend reaching out to find out what kind of other non-stock options are available.
