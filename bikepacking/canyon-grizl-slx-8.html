Canyon Grizl gets Force eTap and Rudy Suspension Fork

Announced a few months back, the Canyon Grizl is the most adventurous gravel bike in the brand’s lineup, and it also proved to be one of the most comfortable drop-bar rigs I’ve ever ridden during my time with it (check out my review here).

Earlier this week, Canyon announced two brand new builds in the Grizl lineup, both based around their top-end carbon CF SLX frame but decked out with SRAM’s new Force XPLR eTap AXS gravel drivetrain. Riders tackling especially rough gravel will be pleased to see one of those models specced with the new RockShox Rudy Ultimate gravel fork, which provides 30 or 40mm of travel, solid fender compatibility, and clearance for up to 700 x 50mm tires.

The Canyon Grizl CF SLX 8 ETAP retails for $5,999 USD and the Rudy-equipped Grizl CF SLX 8 ETAP Suspension build is priced at $6,499 USD. Both will be available for US customers later this year. Learn more at Canyon.com.
