The New MRP Baxter Fork Has Bottle Mounts…

The second generation of the MRP Baxter gravel suspension fork was designed from the ground up. Of particular note is that it’s the first suspension fork to come equipped with a pair of bottle (or cargo) mounts on each leg. I’ve been hoping someone would do this, although it would also be nice to see a mountain bike fork with mounts. According to the press information MRP sent over, each leg can carry up to 3 pounds (1.36kg). The fork is offered in both 40 and 60mm travel versions, which will be suited to “gravel” bikes but may work for some non-suspension-corrected mountain bikes too. Read on for the specs and details.

The new MRP Baxter was redesigned with 40 or 60mm of progressive travel made to smooth out washboards and small rocks on dirt roads. The fork features a 1.5” tapered steerer, a 430mm or 456mm axle-to-crown length, adjustable offset, clearance for 45 or 50mm tires (40 or 60mm versions, respectively), and 12 x 100mm or 12 x 110mm axle spacing. Here are the complete specs as sent in the press release:

  • Stanchion Size: 32mm
  • Travel: 40mm or 60mm
  • Weight: 1,420 grams
  • Spring: FulFill Air
  • External Adjustments: 3-position compression damping, 20-position rebound damping
  • Axle-to-crown Length: 430mm* (*40mm model with 45mm clearance, +5mm for 50mm clearance), 456mm (60mm model)
  • Steerer Options: Tapered (1.125 – 1.5″)
  • Offset: 40mm version: 40.5, 44, or 47.5mm, 60mm version: 41.4, 45, or 48.4mm.
  • Brake Mount: Flat-mount 160 or 180mm
  • Bottle/Accessory Mounts: 2 (one per leg, 3 lb/1.36 kg max load per side)
  • Tire Clearance: 40mm version 700x45mm default (50mm optional), 60mm version 700x50mm (29×2.0”)
  • Axle: 12×100 or 12x110mm (Boost)
  • MSRP: $989.95 USD
  • Availability: July 2021
