The new Giger Jungle Boy is wild!

Created by Zurich-based, titanium bike manufacturer Giger Bicycles, the Jungle Boy pays homage to some of the more eccentric mountain bikes from the 90s. Anyone remember the Nishiki Alien, Yeti’s Ultimate, or the Haro Extreme? Giger states that the Jungle Boy “is a true off-roader, an uncompromising adventure mountain bike designed for the wilderness.” Find details and specs below…

The Jungle Boy features a titanium frame with elevated chain stays, internal cable routing, and of course, the specially designed hatchet mounting system. In addition, the build also gets a motocross handguard to add a little more retro zing. Under the hood, the Giger Jungle Boy is built around a SRAM GX Eagle drivetrain with 148/110mm Boost spacing and 27.5 x 2.8″ tires. Its titanium fork has what appears to be two sets of three-pack bosses on each blade for mounting accessories and the bike comes with a customized bag set from UK-based Restrap.

  • Frame: Giger Titanium Custom
  • Fork: Giger Titanium Custom Fork (Boost)
  • Stem: Giger Titanium Custom
  • Bar: Giger Titanium Custom
  • Headset: Withe Industries
  • Seatpost Clamp: Giger Titanium Custom
  • Saddle: Tioga Spyder Twintail 2 Steel
  • Brakes: Avid BB7 Mountain Mechanical Disc Brake
  • Disc: Galfer
  • Hubs: White Industries XMR Boost
  • Rims: DT Swiss M524
  • Spokes: Sapim X-Ray
  • Tires: WTB Ranger 27.5 x 2.8″
  • Bottom Braket: White Industries T47
  • Crankset: White Industries
  • Pedals: Blue Lug Panda Foot
  • Drivetrain: Sram GX Eagle
  • Handguard: Acrebis
  • Axe: Fiskars X5
  • Bags: Restrap Custom
  • Axe Holder: Woolf Tooh B-Rad
  • Price (frameset) $3,000
  • Price (complete) $8,500
