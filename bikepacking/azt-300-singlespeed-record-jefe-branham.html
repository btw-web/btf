Jefe Branham Sets New AZT 300 Singlespeed Record

Hailing from Gunnison, CO, longtime ultra-endurance racer Jefe Branham crossed the Picket Post finish line at 3:24 AM local time setting the new AZT 300 singlespeed record for the ~280 mile courseâ€”2:01:24 (2d 1h 24m). The former singlespeed record was set in 2016 by Neil Stitzer (2d 6h 23m). Jefe was riding a new titanium hardtail from Why Cycles with 30×21 gearing and 29 x 2.6″ tires…

When asked about the race, Jefe had this to say: “ cooked. I pushed really hard last night and this morning, legs are f@cked! But I am so stoked, and I can hardly believe I still pulled it off. My GPS was not setup right and I made two big missed turns that scored me prob 5-6 miles of extra out-and-back action. All my fault! But getting over it somehow opened some doors in my head. It was a great time.” Congrats on an amazing accomplishment, Jefe!
