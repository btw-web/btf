Introducing Grav.elle: A Cycling Resource for Women

Main photo by Saara Snow (@saarasnow)

Julia Polloreno and Jess Cerra just launched a new website called Grav.elle, with a mission of addressing a void they’ve seen in the cycling industry and content landscape, specifically within gravel riding.

Grav.elle is an online resource they say they “wish existed when we were getting into gravel: A trustworthy, ego-free go-to for useful information, inspirational profiles, recs for where to ride (travel-worthy events and destinations) as well as editor-tested gear reviews. Plus, whatever else we think you might also be into as a person that identifies as female and is into gravel biking. All are welcome and celebrated, whatever your experience level.”

Currently, the site has a gravel events section and a handful of articles, including an Asparagus Frittata recipe, considerations for picking a gravel tire, and some personal reflections from women gravel riders, such as Gravel As The Great Equalizer from our good friend Devin Cowens.

Head over to Gravelle.Life to see more. You can also check them out on Instagram (@grav.elle).
