All New 3T Exploro RaceMax Series Announced

The team at 3T had a simple goal with their new Exploro RaceMax: optimize the aerodynamics of a gravel bike to the same level as a road bike. Although they’ve been hesitant to claim “world’s fastest,” they’re confident the new design just might be the fastest gravel bike available. To accomplish this, 3T honed in on eliminating drag, which meant effectively shielding the tires. This required figuring out exactly how big the tires were—which as you may know, usually isn’t what it says on the sidewall—and resulted in 3T’s new tire measurement concepts: WAM (Width As Measured) and RAM (Radius As Measured). Using more accurate measurements, the engineers prioritized a tight-fitting rear wheel cutout, a downtube optimized to re-direct the airflow coming off the front wheel and onto the water bottle, a narrow headtube with a big steerer inside, and dozens of other small details to eke out every last Watt.

3T also recognized the need for larger tire clearance, and made room for 61mm clearance or 27.5 x 2.4″ tires on the Exploro MAX series, while the Exploro RACE series is specced with 700c x 35-42mm tires for those looking for the best in speed. It’s nice to see a threaded bottom bracket, integrated fender mounts, as well as top tube and under the downtube mounts—not to mention the standard frame tech that 3T is known for. Check out the 3T Exploro RaceMax launch video below.

The 3T Exploro MAX comes in three different complete builds ranging from $4,199 to $5,899 USD, as well as frameset only for $3,199. The base Exploro MAX GRX 1x build is built up around 650b x 57mm Schwalbe G-One Allround tires, a 1×11 Shimano GRX drivetrain, and an assortment of 3T components. Find the full build kit below.

It looks like the Exploro RaceMax frameset is available to purchase now, while the complete builds are scheduled to be ready for August 2020. Head over to 3T.Bike to learn more.
